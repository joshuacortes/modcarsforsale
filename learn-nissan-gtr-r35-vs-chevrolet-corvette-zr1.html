Nissan GT-R R35 vs. Chevrolet Corvette ZR1



Stock Motor

Nissan GT-R R35:

0-60 Acceleration: The GT-R R35 is renowned for its exceptional acceleration. With its powerful 3.8-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine, it can achieve a blistering 0-60 mph time in around 2.9 to 3.2 seconds, depending on various factors such as road conditions and driver skill.

Torque and Horsepower: The GT-R R35 produces impressive torque and horsepower figures. It generates around 470 lb-ft of torque and approximately 565 to 600 horsepower, depending on the specific model and year.

Top Speed: The GT-R R35 has a top speed of around 196 to 200 mph, making it one of the fastest production cars on the market.

Quarter-Mile Time: In terms of quarter-mile performance, the GT-R R35 can achieve times in the range of 10.8 to 11.2 seconds, showcasing its remarkable straight-line speed.

Lap Times: The GT-R R35 is renowned for its performance on the track. It has set impressive lap times on various circuits around the world, making it a formidable contender in the realm of sports car performance.

Powertrain Configuration: The GT-R R35 features a front-engine, all-wheel-drive layout, allowing for excellent traction and stability.

Transmission Type: It comes equipped with a dual-clutch six-speed automatic transmission, providing lightning-fast gear changes and precise control.


Chevrolet Corvette ZR1:

0-60 Acceleration: The Corvette ZR1, equipped with a supercharged 6.2-liter V8 engine, delivers exhilarating acceleration. It can achieve a 0-60 mph time in the range of 2.8 to 3.2 seconds, depending on various factors such as tire grip and launch technique.

Torque and Horsepower: The ZR1 produces impressive torque and horsepower figures, thanks to its supercharged powerplant. It generates around 715 lb-ft of torque and approximately 755 to 755 horsepower, depending on the specific model and year.

Top Speed: The ZR1 boasts an impressive top speed of around 212 to 214 mph, making it one of the fastest production Corvettes ever produced.

Quarter-Mile Time: The ZR1 can achieve quarter-mile times in the range of 10.6 to 11.2 seconds, showcasing its incredible straight-line performance.

Lap Times: The ZR1 has proven itself on the track, setting impressive lap times and demonstrating its capabilities as a track-ready sports car.

Powertrain Configuration: The ZR1 features a front-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout, providing a classic sports car driving experience.

Transmission Type: It comes equipped with a seven-speed manual transmission or an eight-speed automatic transmission, offering options for different driver preferences.


Power-to-Weight Ratio and RPM (Shaft Speed):

The GT-R R35 boasts a power-to-weight ratio of approximately 370 to 390 horsepower per ton, depending on the specific model and configuration.

The ZR1 offers a power-to-weight ratio of approximately 520 to 550 horsepower per ton, showcasing its exceptional power output relative to its weight.

The RPM (shaft speed) of the GT-R R35 can reach up to 7,000 to 7,500 RPM, while the ZR1 can rev up to 6,500 to 7,000 RPM, enabling both cars to deliver exhilarating performance and power.


In conclusion, both the Nissan GT-R R35 and the Chevrolet Corvette ZR1 are high-performance sports cars that offer incredible speed, power, and exhilaration. While the GT-R R35 emphasizes exceptional all-wheel-drive grip and acceleration, the Corvette ZR1 showcases blistering straight-line speed and track prowess. Whether it's acceleration, top speed, quarter-mile times, or power-to-weight ratio, both cars are formidable contenders in the world of high-performance sports cars, catering to different driving preferences and delivering unforgettable driving experiences.





Tuning Ability

Intake Modifications:

The GT-R R35 offers various aftermarket options for improving intake airflow, such as high-flow air filters and cold air intake systems. These modifications enhance air delivery to the engine, resulting in increased horsepower and torque gains.

The Corvette ZR1 also has a range of aftermarket intake options, including cold air intakes and high-performance air filters. These modifications improve air intake efficiency, leading to better engine performance and responsiveness.


Exhaust Modifications:

Both the GT-R R35 and the Corvette ZR1 benefit from aftermarket exhaust upgrades. Performance exhaust systems, including headers, mid-pipes, and mufflers, can enhance exhaust flow, reduce backpressure, and provide a more aggressive exhaust note.

The GT-R R35's exhaust system can be modified to produce a deeper and more pronounced tone, while the ZR1's exhaust can be upgraded for improved flow and a more aggressive sound.


Forced Induction:

The GT-R R35 comes equipped with twin turbochargers, providing significant potential for increased boost levels and power gains. Upgrading to larger turbos or installing a boost controller can enhance performance.

The Corvette ZR1 is already supercharged from the factory, but aftermarket upgrades, such as larger superchargers, intercoolers, and pulley upgrades, can further increase boost levels and power output.


Fuel Management and Ignition:

The GT-R R35's engine management system allows for precise control over fuel delivery and ignition timing. Aftermarket engine management systems and tuners can optimize performance by customizing fuel and ignition maps to maximize power and efficiency.

The ZR1 features advanced engine management technology that allows for tuning adjustments. Aftermarket tuners can recalibrate fuel and ignition settings to optimize performance and accommodate modifications.


Overall Tuning Potential:

The GT-R R35 has gained a reputation as a highly tunable car due to its robust engine design and extensive aftermarket support. With the right modifications and tuning, power outputs well beyond the stock levels can be achieved.

The Corvette ZR1 also offers great potential for tuning, thanks to its supercharged V8 engine. Upgraded components, custom tuning, and increased boost levels can result in substantial horsepower and torque gains.

It's important to note that tuning any car comes with its own set of considerations. It's crucial to work with experienced tuners or professionals who specialize in the specific model and have a thorough understanding of the engine's capabilities and limitations.





Suspension

Nissan GT-R R35 Suspension:

The GT-R R35 features an advanced suspension system designed to deliver exceptional handling and stability. It employs a multi-link suspension setup in the front and rear, offering independent control over each wheel's movement.

The GT-R R35's suspension benefits from advanced technologies such as adaptive dampers, which adjust to changing road conditions and driver inputs in real-time. This feature allows for a dynamic and comfortable ride, regardless of the driving scenario.

Nissan's advanced ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system works in conjunction with the suspension to provide optimal traction and control. The system actively distributes power between the front and rear wheels to enhance grip and cornering capabilities.

The GT-R R35's suspension tuning is focused on delivering precise and responsive handling, allowing the driver to confidently tackle corners and curves. It strikes a balance between comfort and performance, making it suitable for both spirited driving and everyday use.


Chevrolet Corvette ZR1 Suspension:

The Corvette ZR1 is renowned for its impressive track capabilities, and its suspension plays a crucial role in delivering exceptional performance. It features a lightweight and rigid aluminum-intensive suspension system.

The ZR1 utilizes a double-wishbone suspension setup in the front and rear, which provides excellent control and allows for precise wheel movement. This design enhances grip, stability, and responsiveness.

Magnetic Selective Ride Control, an advanced suspension technology, is available as an option for the ZR1. It utilizes magnetorheological dampers that adjust damping characteristics in real-time based on road conditions and driver inputs. This system offers improved ride comfort and enhanced handling capabilities.

The ZR1's suspension tuning focuses on delivering exceptional track performance, with stiff springs and anti-roll bars providing excellent body control during aggressive driving maneuvers. The suspension setup ensures minimal body roll and precise turn-in response, allowing for confident and agile cornering.


Comparing the Suspension:

Both the GT-R R35 and the Corvette ZR1 have suspension systems that are tuned to deliver exceptional performance, but they cater to different driving preferences.

The GT-R R35's suspension system combines advanced technologies with a focus on everyday drivability. It strikes a balance between comfort and performance, providing a confident and controlled ride.

The Corvette ZR1's suspension is geared towards track performance and aggressive driving. Its rigid construction, lightweight design, and advanced suspension options ensure superb handling and responsiveness on the track.





Interior

Nissan GT-R R35 Interior:

The GT-R R35's interior design is focused on providing a driver-centric cockpit. It features a clean and functional layout with a blend of modern and sporty aesthetics.

The materials used in the GT-R R35's interior are of good quality, but they may not match the luxury feel of some other high-end sports cars. Soft-touch surfaces, leather upholstery, and carbon fiber accents are used in key areas to enhance the premium feel.

The seats in the GT-R R35 are supportive and comfortable, designed to hold the occupants in place during high-performance driving. They offer good bolstering and adjustment options for a personalized fit.

In terms of features, the GT-R R35 comes equipped with a range of technology and amenities, including a multi-function steering wheel, dual-zone automatic climate control, a touchscreen infotainment system, and a premium audio system. However, some of the interior technology may feel a bit dated compared to more recent sports cars.


Chevrolet Corvette ZR1 Interior:

The Corvette ZR1's interior has seen significant improvements over previous generations. It features a more refined and modern design, combining sportiness and luxury.

The materials used in the ZR1's interior are of higher quality compared to its predecessors. Soft-touch surfaces, premium leather upholstery, and carbon fiber accents are used extensively, giving the cabin a more upscale and luxurious feel.

The seats in the ZR1 are designed to provide excellent support and comfort during spirited driving. They offer a combination of sporty bolstering and adjustable features to accommodate different body types and driving preferences.

The ZR1 boasts a range of advanced features and technology, including a driver-focused instrument cluster, a touchscreen infotainment system, a premium Bose audio system, and various driver assistance features. The interior tech is more up-to-date compared to the GT-R R35, enhancing the overall driving experience.


Comparing the Interior:

Both the GT-R R35 and the Corvette ZR1 offer well-designed interiors, but they cater to different priorities.

The GT-R R35's interior is functional and driver-centric, focusing on performance and functionality rather than luxury. It provides a comfortable and ergonomic space for the driver, with technology and features that enhance the driving experience. The Corvette ZR1's interior has made significant strides in terms of luxury and refinement. It offers a more upscale ambiance with higher-quality materials and modern features that cater to both comfort and performance.
