The 30 Best Race Tracks in America



The United States is home to a plethora of world-class racetracks that offer the perfect playground for unleashing your inner speedster. Whether you're a seasoned track day veteran or a novice looking to experience the thrill of high-speed driving, these 30 race tracks open to the public are sure to satisfy your need for speed.


1. Circuit of The Americas - Austin, Texas

Home to Formula 1's US Grand Prix, this state-of-the-art facility offers a challenging 3.4-mile track with dramatic elevation changes and hairpin turns.


2. Laguna Seca - Monterey, California

Famous for the "Corkscrew" section, Laguna Seca's 2.2-mile track provides a unique driving experience with its tight turns and steep elevation changes.


3. Road America - Elkhart Lake, Wisconsin

Stretching over 4 miles, this historic track boasts high-speed straights, sweeping corners, and a variety of elevation changes.


4. Watkins Glen International - Watkins Glen, New York

With a rich racing heritage, this 3.4-mile track features a mix of challenging turns and fast straights, making it a favorite among drivers.


5. Sebring International Raceway - Sebring, Florida

Known for its bumpy surface, this 3.74-mile track is a true test of driver and machine endurance.


6. Virginia International Raceway - Alton, Virginia

VIR offers multiple configurations, including a 3.27-mile Full Course and a 4.2-mile Grand Course, providing a diverse range of challenges.


7. Thunderhill Raceway Park - Willows, California

This 3-mile track offers various layouts and is known for its technical sections, making it ideal for honing your driving skills.


8. Mid-Ohio Sports Car Course - Lexington, Ohio

Featuring 15 turns over a 2.4-mile length, this track offers a balanced mix of speed and technicality.


9. New Jersey Motorsports Park - Millville, New Jersey

With two separate tracks, Thunderbolt and Lightning, NJMP offers diverse layouts and challenges for drivers of all levels.


10. Sonoma Raceway - Sonoma, California

Formerly known as Infineon Raceway, this 2.52-mile track features elevation changes and a mix of corners to test your driving abilities.


11. Pittsburgh International Race Complex - Wampum, Pennsylvania

This relatively new facility offers multiple configurations and prides itself on providing a safe and exhilarating driving experience.


12. Carolina Motorsports Park - Kershaw, South Carolina

With its technical layout and variety of corners, this track is a great place to develop your driving skills.


13. Buttonwillow Raceway Park - Buttonwillow, California

Known for its challenging turns and high-speed straights, Buttonwillow offers a range of configurations to keep drivers engaged.


14. Lime Rock Park - Lakeville, Connecticut

A shorter track at 1.5 miles, Lime Rock's tight corners and fast sections require precision and skill.


15. High Plains Raceway - Deer Trail, Colorado

Situated in the Rocky Mountains, this track offers a mix of elevation changes and high-speed stretches.


16. Thompson Speedway Motorsports Park - Thompson, Connecticut

With a tight layout and various configurations, Thompson Speedway is a great venue for improving your driving technique.


17. Oregon Raceway Park - Grass Valley, Oregon

Surrounded by stunning landscapes, this track's flowing design provides an enjoyable and challenging experience.


18. Utah Motorsports Campus - Grantsville, Utah

Formerly known as Miller Motorsports Park, this facility offers a range of configurations and challenges for drivers.


19. Grattan Raceway - Belding, Michigan

A classic track with technical sections and elevation changes that demand precision and finesse.


20. NCM Motorsports Park - Bowling Green, Kentucky

Adjacent to the National Corvette Museum, this track features interesting corners and elevation changes.


21. New York Safety Track - Jefferson, New York

Designed with safety in mind, this track offers an exciting layout to push your limits.


22. Eagles Canyon Raceway - Decatur, Texas

This Texas track offers both technical challenges and high-speed sections for a well-rounded experience.


23. Blackhawk Farms Raceway - South Beloit, Illinois

A shorter track with a focus on technicality, Blackhawk Farms is perfect for refining your driving skills.


24. Nelson Ledges Road Course - Garrettsville, Ohio

Known for its flowing layout, this track provides a mix of challenges for drivers of all levels.


25. Motorsport Ranch - Cresson, Texas

With two tracks, one 1.7 miles and the other 3.1 miles, Motorsport Ranch offers varied experiences for drivers.


26. Atlanta Motorsports Park - Dawsonville, Georgia

Designed by Formula One architect Hermann Tilke, this track features elevation changes and technical corners.


27. The Ridge Motorsports Park - Shelton, Washington

Nestled in the Pacific Northwest, this track offers a scenic setting and challenging layout.


28. Autobahn Country Club - Joliet, Illinois

With its member-focused approach, this track provides various configurations and amenities for driving enthusiasts.


29. New Hampshire Motor Speedway - Loudon, New Hampshire

In addition to NASCAR events, this track offers opportunities for track days on its 1.6-mile road course.


30. MSR Houston - Angleton, Texas

Known for its variety of turns and straights, this track is a favorite among Texas-based driving enthusiasts.


For those with a need for speed, these 30 car race tracks in the USA offer a range of challenges, layouts, and experiences that cater to drivers of all skill levels. Whether you're aiming to improve your driving technique or simply seeking an adrenaline rush, these tracks are your gateway to a world of high-performance excitement. So, fasten your seatbelt, strap on your helmet, and get ready to hit the asphalt at these fantastic public race tracks!
