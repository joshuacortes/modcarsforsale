Nissan 370Z vs. Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack



Stock Motor

Nissan 370Z:

0-60 mph (0-97 km/h): The Nissan 370Z delivers a respectable 0-60 mph time of around 5.3 to 5.6 seconds, depending on the model year and specific trim.

Torque: The 370Z is powered by a 3.7-liter V6 engine, producing torque in the range of approximately 270 lb-ft (366 Nm).

Horsepower: The V6 engine in the 370Z generates a horsepower output of around 332 to 350 hp, depending on the model year and any performance package options.

Top Speed: The Nissan 370Z has an electronically limited top speed of approximately 155 mph (250 km/h), offering a thrilling high-speed experience.

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time for the 370Z is roughly 13.7 to 14.2 seconds, showcasing its straight-line performance capabilities.

Lap Times: While not as track-focused as some high-performance sports cars, the 370Z still handles well on the racetrack, but specific lap times vary depending on the driver and conditions.

Powertrain Configuration: The Nissan 370Z comes with a front-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout, making it a classic sports car in terms of its power delivery.

Transmission Type: The 370Z is available with a 6-speed manual transmission for enthusiasts who prefer the engaging driving experience. Additionally, an optional 7-speed automatic transmission is available.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: The power-to-weight ratio of the 370Z is reasonably balanced, offering a good power output relative to its weight, enhancing its performance characteristics.

RPM (Shaft Speed): The V6 engine in the 370Z delivers its peak power at relatively high RPM, encouraging drivers to explore the higher rev range for exhilarating acceleration.


Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack:

0-60 mph (0-97 km/h): The Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in an impressive 4.2 to 4.5 seconds, showcasing its powerful performance.

Torque: The Mustang GT is equipped with a 5.0-liter V8 engine, delivering impressive torque in the range of around 410 to 420 lb-ft (556 to 570 Nm).

Horsepower: The V8 engine in the Mustang GT produces an exhilarating horsepower output of approximately 450 to 460 hp, making it a true muscle car.

Top Speed: The Ford Mustang GT boasts an electronically limited top speed of about 155 mph (250 km/h), similar to the 370Z.

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time for the Mustang GT is around 12.5 to 12.8 seconds, making it noticeably quicker than the 370Z in straight-line performance.

Lap Times: With its track-focused Performance Pack, the Mustang GT can achieve competitive lap times, especially on race circuits, thanks to its sport-tuned suspension and other performance enhancements.

Powertrain Configuration: Like the 370Z, the Mustang GT features a front-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout, delivering a classic muscle car experience.

Transmission Type: The Mustang GT comes standard with a 6-speed manual transmission, offering a direct and engaging connection to the road. An optional 10-speed automatic transmission is also available for those seeking convenience.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: The Mustang GT Performance Pack features an impressive power-to-weight ratio, leveraging its potent V8 engine and lightweight construction.

RPM (Shaft Speed): The V8 engine in the Mustang GT also offers substantial low-end torque, making it a powerful performer throughout its RPM range.


Comparison:

The Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack outperforms the Nissan 370Z in acceleration and quarter-mile times, thanks to its potent V8 engine.

The Nissan 370Z offers a lighter and more nimble feel, making it a responsive and engaging sports car, but the Mustang GT exudes raw power and muscle car charisma.

The Mustang GT's Performance Pack adds track-focused enhancements, giving it an edge in track performance compared to the 370Z.

The 370Z provides an option for a manual transmission as standard, appealing to purists who prefer a more involved driving experience, while the Mustang GT offers the same choice with the added convenience of an automatic option.

Both cars have a similar electronically limited top speed of around 155 mph, providing thrilling high-speed capabilities.





Tuning Ability

Nissan 370Z:


Upgraded Intake:

A high-flow cold air intake system can improve airflow to the engine, enhancing power and throttle response.

Brands like Stillen, K&N, and Injen offer popular cold air intake options for the 370Z.


Exhaust Upgrades:

Cat-back exhaust systems reduce exhaust restriction, providing a more aggressive sound and a modest power boost.

Companies like Borla, Invidia, and HKS offer performance exhaust options for the 370Z.


Forced Induction:

Supercharger kits can be added to the 370Z to significantly increase horsepower and torque.

Tuning companies like Stillen and Vortech offer complete supercharger packages for the 370Z.


Fuel Management:

Upgraded fuel injectors and fuel pumps may be necessary to support higher power levels when adding forced induction.

Brands like DeatschWerks and Injector Dynamics provide fuel system upgrades for the 370Z.


Ignition Options:

Upgraded ignition coils and spark plugs can improve combustion efficiency and power delivery.

Companies like NGK and Denso offer performance ignition components for the 370Z.


Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack:

Upgraded Intake:

Cold air intakes enhance airflow and can provide noticeable power gains for the Mustang GT.

Brands like K&N, Roush, and Airaid offer popular intake options for the Mustang GT.


Exhaust Upgrades:

Cat-back and axle-back exhaust systems improve exhaust flow, resulting in increased power and a more aggressive exhaust note.

Companies like Borla, MagnaFlow, and Flowmaster offer performance exhaust systems for the Mustang GT.


Forced Induction:

Supercharger kits for the Mustang GT significantly boost horsepower and torque, turning it into a high-performance machine.

Tuning companies like Whipple, Roush, and ProCharger offer comprehensive supercharger solutions for the Mustang GT.


Fuel Management:

High-flow fuel injectors and upgraded fuel pumps are essential for providing adequate fuel delivery with forced induction.

Brands like DeatschWerks and Aeromotive offer fuel system upgrades suitable for the Mustang GT.


Ignition Options:

Performance ignition coils and spark plugs can optimize combustion and ignition timing.

Companies like MSD and Accel offer ignition upgrades for the Mustang GT.


Comparison:

The Nissan 370Z and Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack offer similar tuning potential, with both being popular choices among enthusiasts looking to upgrade their cars.

The Mustang GT, with its V8 engine, may have a slight advantage in terms of aftermarket support and a broader range of available performance parts.

Forced induction options for both cars can significantly increase horsepower and torque, transforming them into potent high-performance machines.

The 370Z's V6 engine can still benefit from various bolt-on upgrades like intake and exhaust systems to improve performance and sound.

Both cars have a strong community of aftermarket tuners and performance companies that cater to their specific needs.





Suspension

Nissan 370Z Suspension:

Front Suspension: The Nissan 370Z features a double-wishbone front suspension, which is a tried-and-true design known for providing precise steering response and agile handling.

Rear Suspension: At the rear, the 370Z employs an independent multi-link suspension, further contributing to its nimble and responsive nature during cornering.

Sport-Tuned Dampers: The 370Z comes equipped with sport-tuned dampers, enhancing stability during spirited driving while maintaining acceptable ride comfort.

Stiffer Springs: The 370Z features stiffer springs compared to standard sedans, reducing body roll and providing a sportier driving feel.

Limited Adjustability: The suspension of the 370Z offers limited adjustability, which may be a drawback for enthusiasts seeking precise fine-tuning for track performance.


Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack Suspension:

Front Suspension: The Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack boasts a unique front suspension setup known as the MagneRide dampers. This system uses electromagnetically controlled dampers for superior adaptability to road conditions and driving situations.

Rear Suspension: Similar to the 370Z, the Mustang GT Performance Pack features an independent rear suspension, which contributes to improved handling and ride quality.

MagneRide Dampers: The highlight of the Performance Pack is the MagneRide adaptive dampers, providing instantaneous and continuous adjustments to improve handling, cornering stability, and ride comfort.

Adjustable Driving Modes: The Mustang GT Performance Pack comes with various selectable driving modes, including Track, Sport, Normal, and Comfort, allowing the driver to tailor the suspension and overall driving experience.

Performance-Oriented Tuning: The MagneRide system is tuned to deliver optimal performance on both the track and the street, offering an excellent balance between sportiness and everyday usability.



Comparison:

The Nissan 370Z features a more traditional sport-tuned suspension with double-wishbone front and independent multi-link rear, providing predictable and sharp handling.

The Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack, with its MagneRide adaptive dampers, offers superior adjustability and responsiveness, adapting to different road conditions and driving styles.

The Mustang's MagneRide system allows for precise tuning and customization through selectable driving modes, giving the driver more control over the car's dynamics.

The 370Z's sport-tuned suspension delivers a direct and connected driving experience, while the Mustang GT's MagneRide system offers a blend of performance and comfort.

Enthusiasts who value precise and intuitive handling may appreciate the traditional suspension of the 370Z, while those seeking advanced adaptability and technology may lean towards the Mustang GT Performance Pack.





Interior

Nissan 370Z Interior:


Design and Layout:

The interior of the Nissan 370Z features a driver-focused design with a simple and straightforward layout.

The center console is angled towards the driver, enhancing accessibility to essential controls and infotainment.


Materials and Finishes:

The 370Z offers a mix of soft-touch materials and hard plastics in its cabin, which may not match the premium feel of some competitors.

While the quality is acceptable, it may not meet the luxury expectations of certain buyers.


Seating Comfort:

The sports seats in the 370Z provide excellent lateral support and bolstering, keeping occupants firmly in place during spirited driving.

However, some drivers may find the seats a bit snug for long journeys.


Infotainment System:

The 370Z comes with a dated infotainment system that lacks modern features such as touchscreen functionality or smartphone integration like Apple CarPlay or Android Auto.

The absence of these features may be a drawback for tech-savvy drivers.


Interior Space:

The Nissan 370Z offers a relatively cozy interior, which may feel tight for taller drivers or passengers.

Rear seating is limited, and the car is better suited for two occupants, with the rear seats functioning as additional storage space.


Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack Interior:


Design and Layout:

The interior of the Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack exudes a more modern and upscale vibe compared to the 370Z.

The dashboard design integrates high-quality materials, stylish accents, and a driver-oriented layout.


Materials and Finishes:

The Mustang GT Performance Pack features premium materials and soft-touch surfaces throughout the cabin, providing a more luxurious feel.

The use of higher-grade materials contributes to a more upscale ambiance.


Seating Comfort:

The Mustang GT comes with comfortable and supportive seats, making it suitable for extended drives.

The optional Performance Pack seats add even more lateral support and bolstering for a sportier feel during spirited driving.


Infotainment System:

The Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack features a user-friendly and modern SYNC 3 infotainment system.

SYNC 3 offers a responsive touchscreen display, smartphone integration with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto, and voice-activated controls.


Interior Space:

The Mustang GT provides a more spacious interior, comfortably accommodating four occupants.

While the rear seats are still more suitable for shorter trips, they offer better usable space compared to the 370Z.


Comparison:

The Nissan 370Z boasts a driver-focused design and straightforward layout, ideal for enthusiasts who prioritize a purist driving experience.

The Ford Mustang GT Performance Pack offers a more modern and luxurious interior, appealing to those seeking a balance between performance and comfort.

The 370Z's interior materials and finishes are adequate but may not match the upscale feel of the Mustang GT Performance Pack.

The Mustang GT comes equipped with a more advanced and user-friendly infotainment system, offering seamless smartphone integration and convenience features.

While both cars provide comfortable front seating, the Mustang GT's additional interior space and optional Performance Pack seats offer superior comfort during longer journeys and spirited driving.
