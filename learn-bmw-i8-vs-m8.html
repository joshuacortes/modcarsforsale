BMW i8 vs BMW M8



Stock Motor

When it comes to performance-oriented vehicles, BMW is known for producing some of the most exciting cars on the market. The BMW i8 and M8 are two distinct models, each designed to deliver an exhilarating driving experience. In this article, we'll compare their stock motors and performance metrics in detail:


BMW i8:

0-60 mph (0-97 km/h):

The BMW i8, with its plug-in hybrid powertrain, can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in approximately 4.2 to 4.4 seconds.


Torque:

The i8's electric motor and combustion engine together produce a combined torque output of around 420 lb-ft (570 Nm).


Horsepower:

The combined horsepower of the BMW i8's electric motor and combustion engine is approximately 369 to 374 hp, depending on the model year and specific variants.


Top Speed:

The i8's top speed is electronically limited to around 155 mph (250 km/h).


Quarter Mile Time:

The BMW i8 covers the quarter-mile distance in about 12.5 to 12.9 seconds, showcasing its impressive acceleration.


Lap Times:

Though not specifically designed for track performance, the i8 can still handle well on the track due to its lightweight construction and balanced chassis.


Powertrain Configuration:

The BMW i8 features a plug-in hybrid powertrain, consisting of a 1.5-liter turbocharged three-cylinder gasoline engine paired with an electric motor.


Transmission Type:

The i8 comes equipped with a 6-speed automatic transmission for the gasoline engine and a 2-speed automatic transmission for the front electric motor.


Power-to-Weight Ratio:

The i8 boasts a respectable power-to-weight ratio due to its lightweight construction, contributing to its agility and performance.


RPM (Shaft Speed):

The RPM (shaft speed) of the i8's gasoline engine can reach up to around 6,500 to 7,000 RPM, providing a spirited driving experience.



BMW M8:

0-60 mph (0-97 km/h):

The BMW M8, equipped with a powerful twin-turbocharged V8 engine, accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in approximately 3.0 to 3.2 seconds.


Torque:

The M8's V8 engine produces an impressive torque output of around 553 to 617 lb-ft (750 to 835 Nm), depending on the specific variant and model year.


Horsepower:

The BMW M8 delivers a robust horsepower output of approximately 600 to 617 hp, depending on the model year and performance packages.


Top Speed:

The M8's top speed is electronically limited to around 155 mph (250 km/h) in standard form, but it can reach higher speeds with optional packages.


Quarter Mile Time:

The BMW M8 completes the quarter-mile run in about 11.1 to 11.5 seconds, showcasing its exceptional straight-line performance.


Lap Times:

The M8 is designed with track performance in mind, featuring performance-oriented suspension, brakes, and advanced chassis tuning.


Powertrain Configuration:

The BMW M8 is powered by a potent twin-turbocharged 4.4-liter V8 engine, renowned for its power and torque delivery.


Transmission Type:

The M8 comes with an 8-speed automatic transmission with manual shift mode for precise gear changes and seamless acceleration.

Power-to-Weight Ratio:

The M8's impressive power-to-weight ratio, achieved through a powerful engine and lightweight construction, contributes to its remarkable performance.


RPM (Shaft Speed):

The RPM (shaft speed) of the M8's V8 engine can rev up to around 6,500 to 7,000 RPM, delivering a thrilling and responsive driving experience.



Comparison:

The BMW i8 and M8 are two entirely different performance cars, catering to different driving preferences and priorities.

The i8 excels in its eco-friendly hybrid powertrain, delivering a combination of performance and efficiency.

The M8, with its potent V8 engine, is designed for high-performance driving and offers exceptional acceleration and straight-line speed.

The i8 is a plug-in hybrid, allowing for electric-only driving for short distances and reduced emissions.

The M8 prioritizes track performance with advanced chassis and suspension components, making it a true sports car.



In conclusion, the BMW i8 and M8 represent two different philosophies in the world of performance cars. The i8 is a plug-in hybrid that combines efficiency with spirited driving, offering a unique blend of power and eco-consciousness. On the other hand, the M8 is a high-performance powerhouse, designed for enthusiasts seeking adrenaline-pumping acceleration and track-ready capabilities. The choice between these two iconic BMW models comes down to individual preferences, whether one prioritizes sustainability and a futuristic powertrain or seeks the raw power and excitement of a traditional high-performance sports car.





Suspension

BMW i8 Suspension:


Front Suspension:

The BMW i8 features a double-wishbone front suspension, a setup commonly used in high-performance sports cars.

The double-wishbone design provides precise steering response, enhanced stability, and improved handling during spirited driving.


Rear Suspension:

At the rear, the i8 utilizes a multi-link suspension, a sophisticated system that optimizes wheel control and handling dynamics.

The multi-link setup contributes to the car's balanced and agile handling characteristics.


Ride Comfort:

The i8's suspension strikes a good balance between sportiness and comfort, offering a relatively smooth and composed ride.

The adaptive suspension system, available in some models, further enhances ride comfort by adjusting damping in real-time.


Adjustability and Customization:

The BMW i8's suspension system, while capable, does not offer extensive adjustability for track-focused tuning.

The suspension setup is optimized for a wide range of driving conditions, with a focus on efficiency and eco-friendliness.


Performance on the Road:

The i8 delivers a nimble and agile driving experience, making it well-suited for twisty roads and spirited driving.

Its suspension system, combined with its lightweight construction, contributes to responsive handling and impressive cornering capabilities.


BMW M8 Suspension:


Front Suspension:

The BMW M8 features a double-wishbone front suspension, similar to the i8, optimized for high-performance driving.

The double-wishbone setup provides enhanced stability and precise steering response, essential for a sports car of this caliber.


Rear Suspension:

The M8 also employs a multi-link rear suspension, further refining wheel control and handling characteristics.

The sophisticated multi-link design contributes to the car's ability to handle high speeds and challenging corners with confidence.


Ride Comfort:

The M8's suspension is tuned for sportier and more aggressive driving, resulting in a firmer ride compared to the i8.

Adaptive suspension options with selectable drive modes allow for some adjustment to ride comfort and handling characteristics.


Adjustability and Customization:

The BMW M8 offers a high degree of suspension adjustability, allowing drivers to fine-tune the car's handling to their preferences.

Adjustable dampers, sway bars, and alignment settings cater to various driving conditions and track experiences.


Performance on the Road and Track:

The M8 delivers exceptional performance both on the road and the track, with its suspension system providing sharp handling and high-speed stability.

The car's advanced suspension components and tuning make it a formidable track machine, capable of posting impressive lap times.



Comparison:

The BMW i8's suspension system is optimized for a balanced and efficient driving experience, suitable for daily use and spirited driving.

The BMW M8's suspension, on the other hand, is focused on high-performance driving, with extensive adjustability for track-oriented tuning.

The i8's adaptive suspension offers a smoother and more comfortable ride, prioritizing overall comfort and eco-consciousness.

The M8's suspension prioritizes sharp handling and performance, providing a firmer ride but enhanced driving dynamics.



In conclusion, the BMW i8 and M8 offer two distinct suspension setups catering to different driving preferences and priorities. The i8's suspension strikes a balance between comfort and sportiness, offering agile handling and an efficient, eco-friendly driving experience. On the other hand, the M8's suspension is designed for high-performance driving, delivering sharp handling, adjustability, and track-ready capabilities. The choice between these two iconic BMW models ultimately comes down to individual preferences, whether one seeks a sporty and eco-conscious hybrid or a powerful and track-focused performance machine.





Interior

BMW i8 Interior:

Design and Layout:

The BMW i8's interior follows a futuristic and driver-centric design, reflecting its eco-conscious and sporty nature.

The cockpit is designed to provide a sense of being in a high-performance vehicle, with controls angled towards the driver.


Materials and Finishes:

The i8's interior is crafted with high-quality materials, featuring a mix of leather, Alcantara, and sustainable materials like recycled plastics and natural fibers.

Attention to detail and premium finishes contribute to an upscale and environmentally conscious cabin environment.


Seating Comfort:

The i8's seats are sporty and supportive, providing a good balance between comfort and bolstering during spirited driving.

The available electric seat adjustment allows for customization to suit individual driving preferences.


Infotainment System:

The BMW i8 comes equipped with BMW's iDrive infotainment system, featuring a high-resolution display and intuitive controls.

The system integrates various functions, including navigation, multimedia, and vehicle settings, enhancing the overall driving experience.


Tech Features:

The i8 offers a range of tech features, such as a digital instrument cluster, smartphone connectivity, and a premium audio system.

Advanced driver-assistance systems, like adaptive cruise control and collision warning, add to the car's modern appeal.


Interior Space:

The i8's interior provides adequate space for the driver and passenger, but it may feel more snug due to its coupe design.

The rear seats are relatively small and suitable for occasional use or extra storage space.


BMW M8 Interior:

Design and Layout:


The BMW M8's interior exudes a more luxurious and performance-focused design, combining sportiness with a touch of elegance.

The cockpit is driver-centric, featuring an array of M-specific controls and finishes.


Materials and Finishes:

The M8's interior boasts top-notch materials and impeccable craftsmanship, with a focus on premium leather, carbon fiber, and aluminum accents.

M-specific touches, like illuminated M badges and contrast stitching, contribute to the cabin's upscale feel.


Seating Comfort:

The M8's seats offer outstanding comfort and support, specifically designed to hold occupants during high-speed maneuvers.

Available comfort features like massaging and ventilation add to the car's luxury and driving pleasure.


Infotainment System:

The BMW M8 comes equipped with the latest version of iDrive, featuring a larger touchscreen display and improved gesture control.

The infotainment system offers seamless smartphone integration and a variety of connectivity options.


Tech Features:

The M8 is packed with advanced tech features, including a customizable digital instrument cluster, head-up display, and Harman Kardon premium audio system.

Sophisticated driver-assistance systems, such as parking assistance and lane-keeping assist, enhance safety and convenience.


Interior Space:

The M8's interior provides ample space for the driver and front passenger, creating a comfortable and luxurious environment.

The rear seats offer more practical usability compared to the i8, making them suitable for adult passengers on shorter trips.


Comparison:

The BMW i8's interior design is modern, sporty, and eco-conscious, reflecting its hybrid nature and sustainable materials.

The BMW M8's interior is luxurious, performance-oriented, and adorned with premium materials, creating an upscale and sophisticated ambiance.

The i8 emphasizes technology related to eco-consciousness and efficiency, while the M8 focuses on high-tech features for luxury and performance.

Both cars offer excellent seating comfort, with the M8 prioritizing more luxurious appointments and sporty driving ergonomics.



In conclusion, the BMW i8 and M8 represent two different philosophies in the world of luxury sports cars. The i8's interior exudes a futuristic and eco-conscious design, emphasizing sportiness and sustainability. On the other hand, the M8's interior combines luxury and performance, offering premium materials and advanced technology for an upscale driving experience. The choice between these two iconic BMW models comes down to individual preferences, whether one prioritizes eco-consciousness, sporty appeal, and futuristic design or seeks luxury, performance, and high-tech features for an elevated driving experience.
