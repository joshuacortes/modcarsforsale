Infiniti G35 vs G37



Stock Motor

Compare the stock motors of the Infiniti G35 and G37 to understand the key differences and improvements between these two vehicles.


Engine Specifications:

The Infiniti G35, introduced in 2003, was equipped with a 3.5-liter V6 engine. This powertrain generated an impressive 280 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque. It utilized a dual-overhead-cam (DOHC) design, with aluminum-alloy construction for enhanced efficiency and weight reduction.


In 2008, the Infiniti G37 was introduced as the successor to the G35. It featured a more powerful 3.7-liter V6 engine, which marked a significant improvement over its predecessor. The G37's engine delivered a robust 330 horsepower and 270 lb-ft of torque, showcasing the advancements in engine technology. The G37's engine maintained the DOHC configuration and also utilized lightweight aluminum-alloy construction.


Performance:

The increased displacement and improved engine technology of the G37's stock motor translated into enhanced performance on the road. The G37 boasted a faster 0-60 mph acceleration time, providing a thrilling driving experience. The additional horsepower and torque allowed the G37 to deliver better overall performance and greater responsiveness compared to the G35.


Fuel Efficiency:

While the G37 showcased superior performance, it's worth noting that the increased power came at a slight cost to fuel efficiency. Due to the larger displacement and higher output, the G37 had a slightly lower fuel economy compared to the G35. However, advancements in engine management systems and other technologies mitigated this difference to some extent, ensuring that the G37 still provided respectable fuel efficiency considering its performance capabilities.


Technology and Innovation:

Apart from the engine specifications, the G37 also brought technological advancements that were not present in the G35. The G37 featured refined electronic control systems, improved fuel injection technology, and enhanced aerodynamics. These innovations contributed to a more refined driving experience, better handling, and improved overall efficiency.


The comparison of the stock motors between the Infiniti G35 and G37 reveals a clear progression in terms of performance and technology. The G37's 3.7-liter V6 engine offered a notable increase in horsepower and torque, resulting in improved acceleration and overall driving dynamics. However, the G37's performance gain came at a slightly lower fuel efficiency when compared to the G35. Nevertheless, the advancements in technology and overall driving experience offered by the G37 make it a compelling choice for enthusiasts seeking a blend of luxury and performance.

Both the Infiniti G35 and G37 have their merits, and the choice between the two ultimately depends on personal preferences and priorities. Whether you opt for the G35 or the G37, both vehicles offer an exhilarating driving experience and embody the essence of Infiniti's commitment to performance and luxury.





Tuning Ability

Suspension

Suspension Design:

The Infiniti G35, introduced in 2003, featured a well-regarded suspension system known for its balance between comfort and sportiness. It utilized a double-wishbone front suspension and a multi-link rear suspension setup. This configuration provided excellent handling characteristics, precise steering feedback, and a comfortable ride quality.


In 2008, the Infiniti G37 was introduced, featuring an enhanced suspension system compared to its predecessor. While the G37 retained the same double-wishbone front suspension, it incorporated a revised multi-link rear suspension design. The modifications aimed to further improve the vehicle's handling dynamics, stability, and overall ride quality.


Performance and Handling:

The modifications made to the G37's suspension system resulted in improved performance and handling compared to the G35. The revised multi-link rear suspension contributed to enhanced stability during high-speed cornering and improved traction when accelerating. The G37 exhibited better body control and a more planted feel on the road, providing a more engaging driving experience overall.

Additionally, the suspension upgrades on the G37 refined the balance between comfort and sportiness. The revised setup allowed for better shock absorption and reduced body roll, resulting in a smoother and more composed ride. This balance appealed to drivers seeking a luxurious yet performance-oriented driving experience.


Adaptability and Adjustability:

Both the Infiniti G35 and G37 come equipped with suspensions that offer a degree of adaptability and adjustability, allowing drivers to tailor the handling characteristics to their preferences. However, it's worth noting that the G37's suspension enhancements provided a more refined and responsive platform for adjustments.


Aftermarket Support:

Both the G35 and G37 have a robust aftermarket support system, offering a wide range of suspension upgrades. From coilover kits and sway bars to performance dampers and bushings, tuners have numerous options to further enhance the handling capabilities of these vehicles. The availability of aftermarket components provides flexibility for drivers to fine-tune the suspension system according to their specific needs and preferences.


The suspension systems of the Infiniti G35 and G37 demonstrate a clear progression in terms of performance, handling, and comfort. While the G35's double-wishbone front and multi-link rear suspension already offered a commendable balance, the G37's enhancements provided superior stability, responsiveness, and ride quality.

The Infiniti G37's revised multi-link rear suspension system showcased improved handling characteristics, translating into better traction, stability, and body control during spirited driving. The refined suspension setup strikes a balance between sportiness and comfort, making the G37 an appealing choice for enthusiasts seeking a more engaging driving experience.

Nevertheless, both the G35 and G37 offer a solid foundation for suspension modifications, allowing drivers to tailor their ride according to personal preferences and driving style. Whether one chooses the G35 or the G37, both vehicles deliver a comfortable yet dynamic driving experience, highlighting Infiniti's commitment to performance and refinement.





Interior

When it comes to luxury vehicles, the interior plays a significant role in providing a comfortable and premium driving experience. In this article, we will compare the interiors of the Infiniti G35 and its successor, the Infiniti G37. By examining the design, materials, and features of each model, we can gain insight into the evolution of Infiniti's interior craftsmanship.


Design and Layout:

The Infiniti G35, introduced in 2003, featured a sleek and driver-focused interior design. The dashboard layout placed emphasis on ergonomics, with controls and instruments arranged intuitively for easy access. The G35's interior design emphasized simplicity and elegance, with clean lines and a minimalist approach.

With the introduction of the Infiniti G37 in 2008, the interior design received updates that further enhanced the luxurious ambiance. The G37's cabin boasted refined details and upscale touches. The center console was revised, featuring more contemporary styling and improved functionality. The overall design of the G37's interior showcased a more modern and sophisticated aesthetic.


Materials and Quality:

Both the G35 and G37 incorporated high-quality materials to create a luxurious and comfortable interior environment. Soft-touch surfaces, premium leather upholstery, and tasteful trim accents were utilized in both models to convey a sense of refinement.

The G37, however, demonstrated a step forward in material quality and attention to detail. The use of higher-grade materials, such as genuine wood trim and improved leather upholstery, added an extra touch of luxury to the cabin. The fit and finish of the G37's interior were further refined, providing a more polished and premium feel compared to the G35.


Technological Features:

As technology advanced, the G37 benefited from updated features that were not available in the earlier G35 model. The G37 boasted an enhanced infotainment system, incorporating a larger display screen, improved graphics, and advanced connectivity options. Additionally, the G37 introduced features like a rearview camera, Bluetooth integration, and an optional premium audio system, further enhancing the overall driving experience.


Comfort and Space:

Both the G35 and G37 offered comfortable seating arrangements, providing adequate legroom and support for occupants. The G37, however, presented a slight improvement in rear passenger space, allowing for a more accommodating environment for rear-seat passengers.

Furthermore, the G37 introduced available features like heated and ventilated seats, offering enhanced comfort options for both the driver and passengers. These additional comfort features elevated the luxury experience inside the G37.


The interior comparison between the Infiniti G35 and G37 reveals a progression in terms of design, materials, and features. While the G35 showcased a well-executed and driver-focused interior, the G37 elevated the luxury quotient with its refined design, higher-grade materials, and advanced technological features.

The Infiniti G37's interior demonstrated a commitment to providing a more luxurious and modern driving environment, incorporating contemporary design elements and improved comfort features. However, both models offer a comfortable and premium interior that aligns with the expectations of the luxury segment.

Ultimately, the choice between the G35 and G37 will depend on personal preferences and budget considerations. Whether one opts for the G35 or the G37, both models offer an interior experience that combines comfort, elegance, and driver-focused design, reflecting the commitment of Infiniti to deliver a luxurious driving experience.
