Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III vs. Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI



Stock Motor

Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III, produced from 1995 to 1996, represented a significant leap forward in performance and technology for the Evolution series. Under its hood, the Evolution III housed a robust and high-revving engine that became synonymous with the model's reputation.

Powering the Evolution III is a 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine, known as the 4G63. This powerplant features Mitsubishi's advanced technology, including an intercooler and a DOHC (Dual Overhead Camshaft) design. The Evolution III's 4G63 engine produces around 270 horsepower and 228 lb-ft of torque, making it a formidable force on the road.

The Evolution III's engine is lauded for its quick throttle response, strong mid-range power, and ability to rev to high RPMs. The turbocharger provides a substantial boost, delivering exhilarating acceleration and impressive top-end speed. This engine's performance, combined with the Evolution III's advanced all-wheel-drive system, creates an agile and dynamic driving experience.


Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI, produced from 1999 to 2001, showcased Mitsubishi's dedication to refining and improving the Evolution series. The Evolution VI boasted a range of upgrades, including advancements in aerodynamics and chassis enhancements. Under its hood, it carried a revised version of the iconic 4G63 engine.

The Evolution VI's 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine, also known as the 4G63, received several upgrades over its predecessor. These upgrades included revised camshafts, a redesigned turbocharger, and improved engine management systems. As a result, the Evolution VI's 4G63 engine produces around 276 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque, further enhancing its performance credentials.

The Evolution VI's engine retains the characteristic quick throttle response and high-revving nature of the 4G63 series. The revised turbocharger provides improved low-end response, ensuring power delivery throughout the RPM range. The engine's performance, coupled with the Evolution VI's advanced all-wheel-drive system and enhanced chassis, offers exceptional handling and an engaging driving experience.


Performance Comparison:

Comparing the stock motors of the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI highlights the evolutionary improvements made by Mitsubishi over the years. While both engines share the same displacement and overall architecture, the Evolution VI's enhancements result in a slightly higher power output and improved response.

The Evolution VI's revised engine management system and turbocharger design contribute to improved low-end torque and overall performance. These enhancements enhance the car's responsiveness and acceleration, making the Evolution VI a thrilling machine to drive.


Driving Experience and Enthusiast Modifications:

Both the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI have gained a cult following among automotive enthusiasts. Their stock motors offer a solid foundation for further performance modifications.

Enthusiasts can explore aftermarket upgrades such as intake and exhaust systems, upgraded intercoolers, and engine management tuning to extract even more power from these legendary engines. Additional modifications such as upgraded suspension components and brake systems can further enhance the overall driving experience and track performance.


Comparing the stock motors of the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI showcases the continuous evolution of the Evolution series. The Evolution III's 2.0-liter turbocharged engine delivers impressive power and thrilling performance, establishing its place in automotive history. The Evolution VI builds upon this legacy with slight upgrades, offering improved low-end response and a refined driving experience.

Choosing between the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI depends on individual preferences, budget considerations, and the desired level of performance. Both models embody the spirit of rally-inspired performance, delivering exceptional power, handling, and all-wheel-drive prowess. Whichever Evolution you choose, you can be confident in its ability to provide an adrenaline-pumping driving experience and a lasting legacy in the world of high-performance cars.





Tuning Ability

Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III, produced from 1995 to 1996, represents a significant milestone in the Evolution series. With its rally-inspired DNA, the Evolution III offers a solid foundation for performance modifications and tuning.

The Evolution III's 2.0-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine, the legendary 4G63, has gained fame for its tunability. Enthusiasts can explore various modifications to extract more power and enhance overall performance. Upgrades such as aftermarket intake and exhaust systems, larger intercoolers, and performance turbochargers can significantly increase horsepower and torque output.

In addition to engine modifications, handling improvements are popular among Evolution III owners. Upgrading suspension components, such as coilovers, sway bars, and strut tower braces, can enhance stability, reduce body roll, and improve overall cornering capabilities. Brake upgrades, such as larger calipers and rotors, can also improve stopping power and fade resistance.

Engine management tuning is a crucial aspect of maximizing the Evolution III's performance. A standalone engine management system or a custom ECU (Engine Control Unit) tune can optimize fuel and ignition parameters, allowing for precise control over engine performance. These modifications, combined with careful calibration, can result in significant power gains and improved drivability.


Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI, produced from 1999 to 2001, builds upon the Evolution III's legacy with additional refinements and improvements. Its advanced technology and performance enhancements make it an enticing platform for tuning enthusiasts.

The Evolution VI's 4G63 engine, like its predecessor, is highly tunable. Upgrades such as larger turbochargers, high-flow fuel injectors, and aftermarket camshafts can unleash more power and torque. Enhanced engine breathing with intake and exhaust modifications further improves performance by optimizing airflow.

Handling improvements remain a popular choice for Evolution VI owners. Upgraded suspension components, including adjustable coilovers, sway bars, and chassis braces, can enhance responsiveness and provide better control during aggressive driving. High-performance brake systems with larger rotors and upgraded calipers can improve braking performance and overall safety.

Advanced engine management tuning is crucial for extracting the full potential of the Evolution VI. Tuning options such as standalone engine management systems or custom ECU maps allow for precise control over fuel delivery, ignition timing, and boost pressure. Fine-tuning these parameters can optimize performance and drivability, ensuring a seamless integration of all modifications.


Performance Comparison:

Comparing the tuning ability of the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI reveals similarities and incremental improvements. Both models share the same 4G63 engine, renowned for its tunability and robustness. However, the Evolution VI benefits from advancements in technology and improved engine management systems, providing better control over performance parameters.

The Evolution VI's revisions, including a more efficient turbocharger, improved intercooler design, and refined engine management, result in incremental power gains and enhanced responsiveness compared to the Evolution III. These improvements offer a solid foundation for achieving higher horsepower levels and sharper throttle response.

Suspension

Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III, produced from 1995 to 1996, introduced notable improvements in suspension technology over its predecessors. The Evolution III aimed to deliver enhanced agility and stability, especially during spirited driving and competitive motorsport events.

The Evolution III features a performance-oriented suspension system designed to optimize handling and control. It incorporates MacPherson struts at the front and a multi-link suspension setup at the rear. This setup allows for improved weight distribution and stability, essential for aggressive cornering and precise maneuverability.

To further enhance its performance capabilities, the Evolution III utilizes stiffer springs and upgraded shock absorbers. These modifications reduce body roll, improve responsiveness, and provide better control over various road surfaces. Additionally, larger diameter stabilizer bars enhance the car's stability, reducing understeer and promoting a balanced driving experience.


Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI, produced from 1999 to 2001, builds upon the suspension advancements of its predecessors. It further refines the suspension setup to deliver exceptional handling and control, catering to the demands of enthusiasts and professional drivers.

The Evolution VI benefits from extensive chassis enhancements, including revised suspension geometry and refined components. It features MacPherson struts at the front and a multi-link suspension at the rear, similar to the Evolution III. However, the Evolution VI incorporates additional reinforcements and bracing for improved rigidity and stability.

The Evolution VI's suspension tuning is further optimized to deliver sharper handling and enhanced feedback. It employs revised shock absorbers, stiffer springs, and upgraded stabilizer bars. These modifications contribute to reduced body roll, improved responsiveness, and precise cornering capabilities. The Evolution VI's suspension system is finely tuned to strike a balance between performance and everyday drivability.


Performance Comparison:

Comparing the suspensions of the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI reveals their shared commitment to delivering exceptional handling and control. Both generations incorporate advanced suspension designs and enhancements to enhance their dynamic capabilities.

While the suspension setups are similar between the Evolution III and Evolution VI, the refinements made in the Evolution VI provide a slight edge in terms of responsiveness and overall handling. The Evolution VI's chassis reinforcements and fine-tuned suspension components result in improved stability and sharper feedback, inspiring confidence in high-speed corners and challenging road conditions.


Driving Experience and Enthusiast Modifications:

Both the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution III and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI offer an exhilarating driving experience, thanks to their well-tuned suspensions. However, these suspensions can be further enhanced through aftermarket modifications to suit individual preferences and driving styles.

Enthusiast modifications may include adjustable coilover systems, upgraded sway bars, and performance-oriented strut tower braces. These upgrades provide enthusiasts with the ability to fine-tune their suspension setups, tailoring the handling characteristics to their specific needs. Additionally, performance-oriented braking systems can further enhance the overall driving experience by improving stopping power and reducing brake fade.

In terms of technology, the Evolution VI offers enhanced features compared to its predecessor.
