Mazda RX-8 vs. Chevrolet Camaro 1SS



Stock Motor

Mazda RX-8:

0-60 mph (0-97 km/h): The Mazda RX-8 features a 1.3-liter rotary engine and achieves a 0-60 mph time of around 6.2 to 6.4 seconds, depending on the specific model year and transmission.

Torque: The rotary engine produces torque in the range of approximately 159 lb-ft (215 Nm).

Horsepower: The Mazda RX-8 generates horsepower in the range of around 212 to 232 hp, depending on the model year and trim level.

Top Speed: The top speed of the Mazda RX-8 is electronically limited to approximately 145 mph (233 km/h).

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time for the RX-8 is around 14.8 to 15.0 seconds, showcasing its respectable straight-line performance.

Lap Times: While not extensively tracked, the RX-8 delivers a balanced and nimble handling experience that is well-regarded among driving enthusiasts.

Powertrain Configuration: The Mazda RX-8 utilizes a unique 1.3-liter Wankel rotary engine, which is a departure from conventional piston engines found in most sports cars.

Transmission Type: The RX-8 offers a 6-speed manual transmission as standard, providing an engaging and precise driving experience. An optional 6-speed automatic transmission is available.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: The power-to-weight ratio of the RX-8 is relatively modest due to its lightweight build and moderate power output.

RPM (Shaft Speed): Rotary engines, including the one in the RX-8, achieve high RPMs. The RX-8's engine can rev up to around 9,000 RPM, providing a unique and exhilarating driving experience.


Chevrolet Camaro 1SS:

0-60 mph (0-97 km/h): The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS is powered by a robust 6.2-liter V8 engine and achieves a swift 0-60 mph time of approximately 4.3 to 4.5 seconds, depending on the transmission and drivetrain.

Torque: The V8 engine generates a substantial torque output in the range of around 455 lb-ft (617 Nm).

Horsepower: The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS boasts a powerful horsepower output of approximately 455 to 485 hp, depending on the model year and available performance packages.

Top Speed: The top speed of the Camaro 1SS is electronically limited to around 155 mph (250 km/h).

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time for the Camaro 1SS is around 12.8 to 13.2 seconds, highlighting its impressive straight-line performance.

Lap Times: The Camaro 1SS, particularly when equipped with performance packages, delivers commendable lap times on racetracks, showcasing its track-focused capabilities.

Powertrain Configuration: The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS features a front-engine, rear-wheel-drive layout, a classic setup for high-performance muscle cars.

Transmission Type: The 1SS comes standard with a 6-speed manual transmission, offering a direct and engaging connection to the road. An optional 10-speed automatic transmission is also available.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: The Camaro 1SS features an impressive power-to-weight ratio, thanks to its potent V8 engine and modern lightweight construction.

RPM (Shaft Speed): The Camaro's V8 engine delivers its peak power at a relatively lower RPM range compared to the high-revving rotary engine in the RX-8.


Comparison:

The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS offers significantly faster acceleration and quarter-mile times than the Mazda RX-8, courtesy of its potent V8 engine.

The RX-8's rotary engine provides a unique and high-revving driving experience, but its power output is more modest compared to the Camaro's V8.

The Camaro 1SS's track-focused capabilities and impressive lap times set it apart as a potent muscle car for enthusiasts seeking performance on the racetrack.

The RX-8 excels in handling and nimbleness, providing an engaging driving experience in twisty roads and corners.

The Camaro 1SS's V8 engine offers substantial torque, delivering exhilarating acceleration and a commanding presence on the road.





Tuning Ability

Mazda RX-8:


Upgraded Intake:

A cold air intake system can improve airflow to the RX-8's rotary engine, increasing power and throttle response.

Brands like AEM, K&N, and Racing Beat offer popular cold air intake options for the RX-8.


Exhaust Upgrades:

Cat-back and header-back exhaust systems reduce exhaust restriction, enhancing both performance and the distinctive rotary engine sound.

Companies like Racing Beat, HKS, and Borla offer performance exhaust options for the RX-8.


Forced Induction:

Forced induction options for the RX-8 are limited due to the unique nature of the rotary engine.

Some tuners have experimented with turbocharging and supercharging the RX-8's rotary engine, but such modifications can be complex and require specialized expertise.


Fuel Management:

Upgraded fuel injectors and fuel pumps may be necessary to support higher power levels with forced induction.

Brands like ID Injectors and DeatschWerks offer fuel system upgrades suitable for the RX-8.


Ignition Options:

Performance ignition coils and spark plugs can optimize combustion and ignition timing for the rotary engine.

Companies like NGK and Racing Beat offer ignition upgrades for the RX-8.


Chevrolet Camaro 1SS:

Upgraded Intake:

Cold air intake systems enhance airflow to the Camaro 1SS's V8 engine, improving power and throttle response.

Brands like K&N, Airaid, and SLP Performance offer popular cold air intake options for the Camaro 1SS.


Exhaust Upgrades:

Cat-back and axle-back exhaust systems reduce exhaust restriction, providing a more aggressive exhaust note and a power boost.

Companies like Borla, MagnaFlow, and Corsa offer performance exhaust systems for the Camaro 1SS.


Forced Induction:

Supercharger and turbocharger kits are popular options for boosting power significantly in the Camaro 1SS.

Tuning companies like ProCharger, Edelbrock, and Whipple offer comprehensive supercharger solutions for the Camaro 1SS.


Fuel Management:

High-flow fuel injectors and upgraded fuel pumps are essential to support higher power levels with forced induction.

Brands like Injector Dynamics and Aeromotive offer fuel system upgrades suitable for the Camaro 1SS.


Ignition Options:

Performance ignition coils and spark plugs optimize combustion and ignition timing, improving engine performance.

Companies like MSD and ACCEL offer ignition upgrades for the Camaro 1SS.


Comparison:

The Mazda RX-8's tuning potential is somewhat limited due to the unique nature of its rotary engine, especially when it comes to forced induction.

The Camaro 1SS, with its V8 engine, offers a wider range of tuning options, including supercharger and turbocharger kits, to achieve significant power gains.

The availability of aftermarket components, such as cold air intakes, exhaust systems, and fuel management upgrades, is more extensive for the Camaro 1SS due to its popularity and larger enthusiast community.

Tuning the RX-8 requires specialized knowledge and expertise, particularly for forced induction modifications, which may limit its accessibility to the average tuner.

The Camaro 1SS, with its robust V8 engine, offers the potential for substantial power increases with relatively common and readily available aftermarket components.





Suspension

Mazda RX-8 Suspension:


Front Suspension:

The Mazda RX-8 features a double-wishbone front suspension, a setup known for providing precise steering response and excellent feedback to the driver.

The double-wishbone design allows for independent control of wheel movement, contributing to improved handling and stability.


Rear Suspension:

At the rear, the RX-8 utilizes an independent multi-link suspension, which enhances the car's ability to absorb bumps and maintain traction during cornering.

The multi-link design optimizes wheel control and reduces unsprung weight, improving overall handling and ride quality.


Handling Characteristics:

The RX-8 is lauded for its balanced and nimble handling, thanks to its well-tuned suspension system.

The car feels agile and responsive, making it enjoyable to drive on twisty roads and through corners.


Ride Comfort:

The RX-8's suspension strikes a balance between sportiness and comfort, providing a relatively smooth and composed ride on most road surfaces.

However, some may find the ride a bit firm, especially on rough roads.


Track Performance:

While the RX-8 handles well on the track, its suspension may lack the track-focused components and adjustability found in more track-oriented sports cars.



Chevrolet Camaro 1SS Suspension:


Front Suspension:

The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS features a MacPherson strut front suspension, which is a common design in many modern sports cars.

The MacPherson strut setup is simple and cost-effective, offering good ride comfort and handling performance.


Rear Suspension:

At the rear, the Camaro 1SS also uses an independent multi-link suspension, similar to the Mazda RX-8.

The multi-link design allows for precise wheel control and improved handling dynamics.


Handling Characteristics:

The Camaro 1SS delivers a more assertive and planted feel on the road, with a focus on high-speed stability and cornering confidence.

The suspension setup emphasizes a traditional muscle car experience, with an emphasis on straight-line performance.


Ride Comfort:

The Camaro 1SS provides a sporty ride with firmness to match its performance-oriented nature.

The suspension strikes a balance between sportiness and comfort, but some drivers may find it less forgiving on rough roads compared to the RX-8.


Track Performance:

The Camaro 1SS, particularly with performance packages, excels on the track with its tuned suspension, enhanced brakes, and chassis upgrades.

The suspension components and adjustability of the 1SS are more track-focused compared to the RX-8.


Comparison:

The Mazda RX-8 offers a more balanced and nimble handling experience, making it ideal for driving enthusiasts who prioritize agility and responsiveness.

The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS, with its muscle car heritage, delivers a more assertive and planted feel on the road, prioritizing high-speed stability and straight-line performance.

Both cars feature an independent multi-link rear suspension, providing improved wheel control and handling dynamics.

The RX-8's suspension strikes a good balance between sportiness and comfort, while the Camaro 1SS leans slightly more towards a firm and performance-oriented setup.

The Camaro 1SS, especially with performance packages, is more track-ready with its suspension components and adjustability compared to the RX-8.





Interior

Mazda RX-8 Interior:

Design and Layout:

The Mazda RX-8's interior follows a driver-focused design, with a simple and functional layout.

The center console is angled towards the driver, emphasizing a connection to the driving experience.


Materials and Finishes:

The RX-8's interior materials consist of a mix of soft-touch surfaces and hard plastics.

While some parts feel relatively high-quality, overall, the cabin lacks the premium feel of some of its competitors.


Seating Comfort:

The RX-8 offers comfortable and supportive front seats, which are well-suited for spirited driving.

However, the rear seats are quite small and can be challenging for adult passengers to use comfortably.

Infotainment System:

The RX-8's infotainment system, depending on the model year, may feel dated compared to modern standards.

It lacks advanced features like a touchscreen display, and smartphone integration may not be as seamless as in newer vehicles.

Interior Space:

While the RX-8's front cabin provides adequate space for the driver and passenger, the rear seats are more suitable for occasional use or additional storage space.


Chevrolet Camaro 1SS Interior:

Design and Layout:

The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS features a more modern and driver-centric interior design.

The dashboard layout and control placement enhance the feeling of being in control of a performance machine.


Materials and Finishes:

The Camaro 1SS boasts higher-quality materials throughout the interior, contributing to a more upscale and premium feel.

Soft-touch surfaces, leather-trimmed seats, and attention to detail create a more refined cabin environment.


Seating Comfort:

The Camaro 1SS provides comfortable and supportive front seats, offering a good balance between comfort and bolstering for spirited driving.

Rear seats are relatively small, but they can accommodate passengers for shorter trips more comfortably than the RX-8.


Infotainment System:

The Camaro 1SS comes equipped with a modern and user-friendly infotainment system, featuring a touchscreen display and smartphone integration with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto.

The system offers advanced features and intuitive controls for a more seamless driving experience.


Interior Space:

The Camaro 1SS offers adequate front cabin space for the driver and passenger, and the rear seats provide more practical usability compared to the RX-8.



Comparison:

The Mazda RX-8's interior design is straightforward and functional, emphasizing a driver-focused experience.

The Chevrolet Camaro 1SS's interior design is more modern and refined, offering a premium feel and a sense of sophistication.

The RX-8's interior materials and finishes, while decent, may not match the upscale feel of the Camaro 1SS.

The Camaro 1SS provides a more comprehensive and advanced infotainment system, enhancing convenience and connectivity on the road.


In terms of interior space, the Camaro 1SS is more practical with its better-utilized rear seats, making it more suitable for occasional rear passengers.
