Honda Civic (4th generation) vs. Honda Civic (6th generation)



Stock Motor

Compare the stock motors of the Honda Civic 4th Generation and Honda Civic 6th Generation to highlight the advancements in power and performance that have accompanied the Civic's evolution.

v Honda Civic 4th Generation:

The 4th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1987 to 1991, introduced several engine options to cater to varying customer preferences. One notable engine variant in this generation was the 1.6-liter D16A6, a naturally aspirated inline-four engine. This engine produced around 108 horsepower and 100 lb-ft of torque, offering respectable performance for its time.

The D16A6 engine showcased Honda's commitment to fuel efficiency and reliability. It utilized advanced technologies such as programmed fuel injection (PGM-FI) and Honda's Variable Valve Timing and Lift Electronic Control (VTEC-E) system, which optimized fuel economy and power delivery.

The Civic's 4th Generation stock motor provided a smooth and efficient driving experience, suitable for daily commuting and light sporty driving. While it may not have been considered a high-performance engine, it exhibited Honda's engineering prowess in balancing power, efficiency, and reliability.


Honda Civic 6th Generation:

The 6th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1996 to 2000, marked a significant leap forward in terms of performance and technology. This generation saw the introduction of a more powerful and advanced engine lineup, including the iconic B16A2 engine.

The B16A2 engine, a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated inline-four, was a highlight of the 6th Generation Civic. It boasted Honda's renowned VTEC system, which offered variable valve timing and lift control for enhanced power and performance. The B16A2 engine produced approximately 160 horsepower and 111 lb-ft of torque, providing a noticeable increase in power compared to its predecessor.

The B16A2 engine's spirited performance and high-revving nature made it a favorite among driving enthusiasts. Its power delivery, combined with the Civic's lightweight chassis, allowed for exhilarating acceleration and agile handling. The 6th Generation Civic's stock motor was celebrated for its blend of power, responsiveness, and reliability, positioning it as a sought-after choice among compact performance cars.


Performance and Driving Experience:

The comparison between the 4th Generation and 6th Generation Honda Civic stock motors showcases a clear advancement in performance and driving dynamics. The 6th Generation Civic's B16A2 engine, with its VTEC technology, delivered a substantial increase in horsepower, torque, and overall driving excitement.

The 4th Generation Civic, with its D16A6 engine, offered a reliable and efficient driving experience, ideal for daily commuting and light sporty driving. It prioritized fuel efficiency and reliability, catering to those seeking an economical and practical compact car.

The 6th Generation Civic, on the other hand, embraced a more performance-oriented approach. Its B16A2 engine provided a noticeable power boost, making it a more engaging and exhilarating car to drive. The improved power-to-weight ratio, combined with the Civic's nimble handling characteristics, created a dynamic driving experience suitable for enthusiasts and spirited driving.


Comparing the stock motors of the Honda Civic 4th Generation and Honda Civic 6th Generation reveals the evolutionary advancements made in terms of power and performance. The 4th Generation Civic offered reliable and efficient performance, delivering a comfortable and practical driving experience. However, the 6th Generation Civic elevated the compact car segment, introducing a more spirited and dynamic driving experience with its powerful B16A2 engine.





Tuning Ability

Honda Civic 4th Generation:

The 4th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1987 to 1991, offers a solid platform for tuning enthusiasts. With its lightweight chassis and strong aftermarket support, the 4th Generation Civic can be transformed into a spirited and performance-oriented car.

One of the popular engine choices for tuning in the 4th Generation Civic is the 1.6-liter D16A6 engine. With the right modifications, such as intake and exhaust upgrades, performance headers, and a tuned ECU, the D16A6 can deliver noticeable power gains and improved throttle response. Additionally, enthusiasts can explore forced induction options like turbocharging or supercharging to extract even more performance.

The suspension system can also be upgraded with performance springs, dampers, and sway bars to improve handling and reduce body roll. Upgraded brake systems and lightweight wheels can further enhance the vehicle's overall performance and driving dynamics.


Honda Civic 6th Generation:

The 6th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1996 to 2000, continues the legacy of the Civic as a highly tunable platform. With its robust engine options and advanced technology, the 6th Generation Civic offers enthusiasts ample opportunities for performance upgrades.

The B16A2 engine, a 1.6-liter naturally aspirated inline-four, is a popular choice for tuning in the 6th Generation Civic. With modifications such as intake and exhaust enhancements, a performance camshaft, and a tuned ECU, the B16A2 engine can deliver significant power gains and an exhilarating increase in revving capabilities. Forced induction options like turbocharging or supercharging can be explored to push the engine's performance to new heights.

Beyond the engine, suspension modifications play a crucial role in improving the Civic's handling and cornering capabilities. Upgraded coilovers, sway bars, and performance bushings can enhance the vehicle's responsiveness and stability, allowing for sharper turns and better overall control.


Performance Comparison:

When comparing the tuning ability of the Honda Civic 4th Generation and Honda Civic 6th Generation, the latter offers a more robust platform for performance upgrades. The B16A2 engine's reputation for high-revving power and the 6th Generation Civic's improved chassis dynamics provide a solid foundation for achieving substantial performance gains.

While the 4th Generation Civic may not match the same level of performance potential as the 6th Generation, it still offers a wide range of possibilities for enthusiasts seeking to enhance their driving experience. With the right modifications, the 4th Generation Civic can be transformed into an engaging and spirited performer.


Aftermarket Support:

Both the 4th Generation and 6th Generation Honda Civics benefit from strong aftermarket support, with a plethora of performance parts and tuning options available. Enthusiasts can explore a variety of upgrades, including intake and exhaust systems, engine management solutions, suspension components, brakes, and aerodynamic enhancements.


Comparing the tuning ability of the Honda Civic 4th Generation and Honda Civic 6th Generation highlights the potential for enthusiasts to create exhilarating and high-performing vehicles. The 6th Generation Civic, with its B16A2 engine and improved chassis dynamics, offers a stronger foundation for achieving significant power gains and enhanced handling.

However, the 4th Generation Civic still presents a viable platform for performance upgrades and customization, allowing enthusiasts to transform their vehicles into exciting performers.

Ultimately, the choice between the 4th Generation and 6th Generation Honda Civic for tuning will depend on personal preferences, budget considerations, and the desired level of performance. Both generations offer ample opportunities for enthusiasts to unleash the potential of their Civics, creating personalized driving experiences that exemplify the Honda Civic's reputation for versatility and excitement.





Suspension

Honda Civic 4th Generation:

The 4th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1987 to 1991, showcased Honda's commitment to engineering a well-rounded driving experience. The suspension setup of the 4th Generation Civic consisted of independent MacPherson struts at the front and a trailing arm suspension at the rear.

The 4th Generation Civic's suspension was designed to strike a balance between ride comfort and nimble handling. It delivered a smooth and compliant ride quality, absorbing bumps and road imperfections with ease. The MacPherson strut design at the front provided stability during cornering, while the trailing arm suspension at the rear contributed to a planted feel.

While the suspension of the 4th Generation Civic offered a satisfactory driving experience, it leaned more towards comfort than outright performance. It catered to drivers seeking a smooth ride for daily commuting and practicality, with adequate handling characteristics for urban driving and occasional spirited driving.


Honda Civic 6th Generation:

The 6th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1996 to 2000, marked a notable advancement in suspension design and engineering. Honda aimed to enhance the Civic's handling capabilities and responsiveness while maintaining ride comfort.

The 6th Generation Civic featured an improved suspension system, utilizing independent double-wishbone suspension at both the front and rear. This suspension setup contributed to improved stability, reduced body roll, and enhanced cornering capabilities.

The double-wishbone suspension design allowed each wheel to move independently, optimizing tire contact with the road and providing better control over bumps and uneven surfaces. The result was a more precise and engaging driving experience, allowing drivers to tackle corners with confidence and enjoy a heightened sense of control.


Performance Comparison:

Comparing the suspensions of the Honda Civic 4th Generation and Honda Civic 6th Generation reveals notable improvements in handling and responsiveness in the latter.

The 6th Generation Civic's double-wishbone suspension, with its superior geometry and improved suspension tuning, offered more precise and agile handling characteristics. The suspension enhancements contributed to reduced body roll, improved stability during cornering, and increased traction, making the 6th Generation Civic a more engaging and sporty car to drive.

In contrast, the 4th Generation Civic's suspension prioritized comfort and a smooth ride, catering to drivers seeking a more relaxed driving experience with decent handling capabilities for everyday use.


Driving Experience and Practicality:

Both the 4th Generation and 6th Generation Honda Civics strike a balance between comfort and handling, catering to different driving preferences.

The 4th Generation Civic's suspension delivers a comfortable and compliant ride, making it suitable for daily commuting and practical urban driving. It excels in providing a smooth and comfortable experience, ideal for drivers prioritizing comfort and ease of use.

On the other hand, the 6th Generation Civic's suspension offers a more engaging and dynamic driving experience. Its enhanced handling capabilities and improved stability make it a popular choice among driving enthusiasts seeking spirited drives and sharper handling characteristics.


Comparing the suspensions of the Honda Civic 4th Generation and Honda Civic 6th Generation showcases the evolution of the Civic's driving dynamics. The 4th Generation Civic's suspension prioritized comfort and practicality, providing a smooth and enjoyable ride. In contrast, the 6th Generation Civic's suspension demonstrated advancements in handling and responsiveness, delivering a more engaging and sporty driving experience.





Interior

Honda Civic 4th Generation:

The 4th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1987 to 1991, featured an interior design that reflected the era's sensibilities. The cabin offered a simple and functional layout, with user-friendly controls and a focus on practicality.

The 4th Generation Civic's interior utilized durable materials, with cloth or vinyl seating options and plastic trim pieces. While the materials may not have had a premium feel, they were well-suited for daily use and provided satisfactory longevity. The seating offered sufficient comfort and support, ensuring a pleasant driving experience for occupants.

In terms of technology, the 4th Generation Civic introduced several conveniences for its time. It offered features such as air conditioning, power windows, and an AM/FM radio. However, compared to more modern vehicles, the technology was relatively basic.

Overall, the 4th Generation Civic's interior prioritized functionality and practicality, focusing on delivering a comfortable and straightforward driving experience.


Honda Civic 6th Generation:

The 6th Generation Honda Civic, produced from 1996 to 2000, marked a significant leap forward in interior design and technology. Honda aimed to create a more refined and driver-centric cabin, catering to the evolving expectations of customers.

The 6th Generation Civic's interior featured improved materials and a more modern aesthetic. Soft-touch surfaces, higher-quality plastics, and upgraded trim accents gave the cabin a more upscale feel. The seating was designed for improved comfort and support, making longer drives more enjoyable.

In terms of technology, the 6th Generation Civic introduced notable advancements. It offered features such as power-adjustable side mirrors, improved sound systems, and optional features like a CD player and keyless entry. The dashboard layout was more driver-focused, with ergonomically positioned controls for easy reach.

The 6th Generation Civic's interior showcased Honda's commitment to providing a more refined and comfortable driving experience, blending practicality with improved aesthetics and technology.


Performance and Enthusiast Modifications:

Both the 4th Generation and 6th Generation Honda Civics have garnered a significant following among automotive enthusiasts. The interiors of these models have served as a canvas for customization and personalization.

The 4th Generation Civic's interior, with its straightforward layout, offers a versatile platform for modifications. Enthusiasts can enhance the interior with aftermarket accessories such as sport seats, upgraded audio systems, and personalized trim accents, allowing for a customized driving environment.

The 6th Generation Civic's interior, with its improved materials and driver-centric design, offers an enhanced starting point for customization. Enthusiasts can further enhance the cabin with aftermarket modifications such as upgraded audio systems, sportier seating options, and performance-oriented gauges, adding a touch of personalization and refinement.


Legacy and Practicality:

Both the 4th Generation and 6th Generation Honda Civics have left an indelible mark on the automotive landscape. The 4th Generation Civic's interior, with its focus on functionality and practicality, appeals to those seeking a straightforward and no-frills driving experience. It caters to drivers prioritizing reliability, value, and everyday usability.

The 6th Generation Civic's interior, with its improved materials, modern design, and enhanced technology, reflects Honda's commitment to delivering a more refined and comfortable driving experience. It appeals to those who desire a touch of sophistication and modern conveniences without compromising on the Civic's practicality.


Comparing the interiors of the Honda Civic 4th Generation and Honda Civic 6th Generation reveals the evolution of design, materials, and technology. The 4th Generation Civic's interior focused on functionality and practicality, providing a comfortable driving experience. In contrast, the 6th Generation Civic's interior showcased advancements in material quality, aesthetics, and technology, offering a more refined and driver-centric cabin.
