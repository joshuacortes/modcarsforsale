Hyundai Veloster Turbo vs. Hyundai Veloster N



Stock Motor

The Hyundai Veloster Turbo and Hyundai Veloster N are two performance-oriented models that have gained attention for their sporty styling and spirited driving experience. Central to their performance is the stock motor that powers these vehicles. In this article, we will compare the differences between the stock motors of the Hyundai Veloster Turbo and Hyundai Veloster N, highlighting their unique characteristics and the impact they have on the driving dynamics of each model.


Power Output:

The power output of the stock motors differentiates the Veloster Turbo from the Veloster N. The Veloster Turbo is equipped with a turbocharged 1.6-liter four-cylinder engine that delivers approximately 201 horsepower and 195 lb-ft of torque. While this power output is impressive, the Veloster N takes performance to the next level. The Veloster N features a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine, producing around 275 horsepower and 260 lb-ft of torque. The additional power of the Veloster N translates to a more exhilarating and dynamic driving experience.


Engine Tuning and Performance Enhancements:

The engine tuning and performance enhancements distinguish the Veloster N as the more performance-focused variant. The Veloster N's engine benefits from specific enhancements such as a larger intercooler, an electronically-controlled wastegate, and a high-performance air intake system. These modifications optimize engine performance and provide improved throttle response, making the Veloster N more responsive and engaging on the road or track.


Transmission Options:

Both the Veloster Turbo and Veloster N offer a choice of transmissions, albeit with some differences. The Veloster Turbo is available with either a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission. The Veloster N, on the other hand, comes exclusively with a six-speed manual transmission. The manual transmission in the Veloster N is specifically tuned for performance, offering precise shifts and enhancing the driver's connection with the vehicle.


Performance and Driving Dynamics:

The differences in power and performance enhancements between the Veloster Turbo and Veloster N directly impact their driving dynamics. The Veloster Turbo delivers spirited acceleration and nimble handling, making it an enjoyable and agile daily driver. The Veloster N, with its higher power output and performance-focused enhancements, takes the driving experience to another level. It features an adaptive suspension system, an electronically-controlled limited-slip differential, and selectable driving modes, allowing drivers to fine-tune the performance characteristics to their liking. The Veloster N offers sharper handling, improved cornering abilities, and a more engaging driving experience overall.


Exhaust Note:

The stock exhaust systems on the Veloster Turbo and Veloster N also contribute to their distinctive characters. The Veloster Turbo offers a sporty exhaust note, providing a satisfying growl under acceleration while maintaining a reasonable level of refinement. The Veloster N, on the other hand, features an active exhaust system that enhances the engine sound in its more aggressive modes, resulting in a more exhilarating and spirited soundtrack.


The stock motors of the Hyundai Veloster Turbo and Hyundai Veloster N showcase distinct differences, catering to different performance preferences. The Veloster Turbo delivers a fun and spirited driving experience with its turbocharged engine and nimble handling. On the other hand, the Veloster N elevates performance to a higher level with its more powerful engine, enhanced performance tuning, and specialized features. The Veloster N's additional power and performance-focused enhancements result in a more dynamic and engaging driving experience overall. Whether you seek a spirited daily driver or crave a performance-oriented hatchback, both the Veloster Turbo and Veloster N offer compelling options within the Hyundai lineup.

Comparison and Critique:

Turbocharger Options: The Veloster N offers more aggressive turbocharger upgrade options compared to the Veloster Turbo, allowing for higher power gains and tuning potential.

Fuel Management: The Veloster N benefits from additional fuel management enhancements, such as dual injector kits and higher-capacity fuel pumps, supporting more significant power upgrades.

Forced Induction Upgrades: Both vehicles have a range of turbocharger upgrade options, but the Veloster N's offerings are generally more focused on higher-performance setups.

Aftermarket Support: The Veloster N enjoys a wider range of aftermarket support for tuning upgrades, offering a more extensive selection of products and solutions compared to the Veloster Turbo.

Ignition Options: Both vehicles have similar ignition options for improved spark performance, enhancing overall engine efficiency and power delivery.





Suspension

Hyundai Veloster Turbo:

Front Suspension: The Veloster Turbo is equipped with a MacPherson strut front suspension, a common choice for many compact cars due to its cost-effectiveness and straightforward design.

Rear Suspension: At the rear, the Veloster Turbo features a torsion beam rear suspension, which provides a simple and lightweight solution for smaller vehicles.

Handling: The Veloster Turbo's suspension is sport-tuned for responsive handling, offering agile and precise cornering characteristics, making it a joy to drive on twisty roads.

Ride Comfort: The suspension setup in the Veloster Turbo strikes a balance between sportiness and ride comfort, providing a relatively smooth and enjoyable ride for daily commuting.

Ground Clearance: The Veloster Turbo's suspension design allows for adequate ground clearance, suitable for navigating urban streets and speed bumps with ease.


Hyundai Veloster N:

Front Suspension: The Veloster N features a MacPherson strut front suspension with a unique N-specific geometry, providing improved handling and responsiveness compared to the standard Veloster Turbo.

Rear Suspension: At the rear, the Veloster N is equipped with an independent multi-link suspension, offering better control over wheel movement and enhanced handling characteristics.

Handling: The Veloster N's suspension is specifically tuned for high-performance driving, offering exceptional cornering abilities, precise steering feedback, and improved stability during spirited driving.

Ride Comfort: The Veloster N's suspension is firmer compared to the Veloster Turbo, prioritizing sportiness and handling performance over maximum comfort. As a result, the ride might feel slightly stiffer on rough roads.

Ground Clearance: The Veloster N's suspension is lowered compared to the Veloster Turbo, reducing the ground clearance to enhance aerodynamics and reduce body roll during aggressive driving.


Comparison and Critique:

Suspension Design: The Veloster N's independent multi-link rear suspension provides superior handling and stability compared to the Veloster Turbo's torsion beam rear suspension.

Handling Balance: The Veloster N's N-specific suspension tuning and multi-link rear setup offer a more balanced and engaging driving experience compared to the Veloster Turbo.

Tuning Potential: The Veloster N's suspension setup is better suited for track performance and enthusiastic driving, with aftermarket support offering more significant tuning potential.

Ride Comfort: The Veloster Turbo provides a smoother and more comfortable ride for everyday driving, making it a more practical option for daily commutes and long journeys.

Ground Clearance: The Veloster N's lower ground clearance enhances stability and aerodynamics during high-speed driving but may require more caution on uneven or rough roads.





Interior

Hyundai Veloster Turbo:

Interior Design: The Veloster Turbo's cabin boasts a modern and sporty design, with an emphasis on driver-oriented controls and a sleek dashboard layout.

Materials: The materials in the Veloster Turbo's interior are of good quality, with soft-touch surfaces and well-fitted panels providing a premium feel.

Infotainment System: The Veloster Turbo comes with Hyundai's touchscreen infotainment system, offering smartphone integration, Bluetooth connectivity, and a user-friendly interface.

Seating: The Veloster Turbo features supportive sport seats with ample bolstering, providing comfort and stability during spirited driving.

Cargo Space: The Veloster Turbo provides a decent cargo space with a hatchback design, making it practical for everyday use and weekend getaways.


Hyundai Veloster N:

Interior Design: The Veloster N's interior exudes a more performance-focused vibe, with sporty touches like N-exclusive seats, red accents, and N-specific steering wheel.

Materials: The Veloster N's cabin materials are similar in quality to the Veloster Turbo's, featuring soft-touch surfaces and premium upholstery.

Infotainment System: The Veloster N shares the same infotainment system as the Veloster Turbo, offering similar features and connectivity options.

Seating: The Veloster N's sport seats feature N-specific branding and enhanced bolstering, providing excellent lateral support during aggressive driving.

Cargo Space: The Veloster N's cargo space remains practical, making it suitable for everyday use despite its performance-oriented character.


Comparison and Critique:

Interior Aesthetics: The Veloster N's interior design features sportier elements, reflecting its high-performance nature, while the Veloster Turbo offers a more modern and refined appearance.

Seating Comfort: Both models provide comfortable and supportive sport seats, but the Veloster N's N-specific seats offer enhanced lateral support during spirited driving.

Infotainment Features: The Veloster Turbo and Veloster N share the same infotainment system, providing a similar range of features and connectivity options.

Interior Quality: Both models offer a high-quality interior, with soft-touch materials and well-crafted details, providing a premium feel to occupants.

Practicality: The Veloster Turbo and Veloster N offer decent cargo space and practicality, making them versatile choices for daily driving and weekend adventures.
