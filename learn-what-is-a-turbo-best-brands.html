What is a turbo and what does it do?



A turbocharger, commonly known as a turbo, is a device that is used to increase the power output of an internal combustion engine. A turbo works by compressing the incoming air, allowing more air and fuel to be burned in each combustion cycle. This results in a higher power output from the engine.

A turbocharger consists of two main components: the turbine and the compressor. The turbine is connected to the exhaust system and uses the exhaust gases to spin a shaft. The compressor is connected to the intake system and uses the spinning shaft to compress the incoming air.

When the engine is running, the exhaust gases flow through the turbine and spin the shaft. The compressor on the other end of the shaft takes in air and compresses it before it enters the engine. The compressed air is denser than normal air, which means that more fuel can be added to the air before it enters the combustion chamber.

By adding more fuel, the engine can produce more power. The amount of power that a turbo can add to an engine depends on the size and design of the turbocharger. Larger turbos can add more power, but they can also create more lag before the turbo starts to spin and provide boost.

Turbocharged engines have become increasingly popular over the years because they offer a great balance between power and fuel efficiency. By adding a turbo, car manufacturers can increase the power output of a smaller engine, which allows for better fuel economy without sacrificing performance.

However, it's important to note that turbochargers can also create additional wear and tear on the engine. The increased pressure and heat generated by the turbo can cause additional stress on the engine components, which means that regular maintenance is crucial for keeping the engine running smoothly.

Best Turbo Brands

Choosing the right turbo brand can make a significant difference in the overall performance of your car. In this article, we'll explore some of the best turbo brands for cars on the market today.


Garrett

Garrett is one of the most well-known and respected turbo brands in the industry. They offer a wide range of turbos, including both OEM replacements and high-performance upgrades. Their products are known for their durability, reliability, and high-quality construction. Garrett turbos are commonly used in race cars, as well as in street cars that are looking for a significant performance boost.


BorgWarner

BorgWarner is another top turbo brand that has been around for over 100 years. They offer a variety of turbos, including both gasoline and diesel options. BorgWarner turbos are known for their advanced engineering and innovative design. They use materials such as titanium aluminide and Inconel to create lightweight and durable turbos that can withstand high temperatures and pressures.


Precision Turbo

Precision Turbo is a newer brand that has quickly gained popularity among car enthusiasts. They specialize in high-performance turbos that are designed for racing applications. Precision Turbo uses advanced technology such as billet compressors and lightweight turbine wheels to create turbos that offer exceptional power and performance.


Holset

Holset is a well-respected turbo brand that is known for its expertise in diesel engine applications. They offer a wide range of turbos that are specifically designed for diesel engines, including those used in heavy-duty trucks and industrial applications. Holset turbos are known for their reliability, durability, and efficiency.


Mitsubishi

Mitsubishi is a Japanese brand that has been producing turbos for over 50 years. They offer a range of turbos that are designed for both OEM replacement and high-performance applications. Mitsubishi turbos are known for their precision engineering and advanced design, which results in turbos that are efficient, powerful, and durable.


Turbonetics

Turbonetics is a California-based company that has been producing turbos for over 35 years. They offer a wide range of turbos that are designed for both street and track applications. Turbonetics turbos are known for their high-quality construction, advanced technology, and excellent performance.
