Nissan 370Z Nismo vs. Toyota Supra

Acceleration and Speed:


The Toyota Supra demonstrates superior acceleration compared to the Nissan 370Z Nismo, with a 0-60 mph time of approximately 4.1 seconds compared to the 370Z Nismo's approximately 4.6 seconds. Both models are electronically limited to a top speed of 155 mph.


Power and Torque:

The Toyota Supra boasts higher torque at 365 lb-ft, compared to the 370Z Nismo's 276 lb-ft. However, in terms of horsepower, the 370Z Nismo offers 350 HP, surpassing the Supra's 335 HP.


Powertrain and Transmission:

Both the Nissan 370Z Nismo and the Toyota Supra utilize a front-engine, rear-wheel drive powertrain configuration, providing a classic sports car driving experience. The 370Z Nismo is available with a 6-speed manual or a 7-speed automatic transmission, while the Supra comes exclusively with an 8-speed automatic transmission.


Power-to-Weight Ratio:

The power-to-weight ratio is a critical metric that indicates a vehicle's performance potential. The Toyota Supra boasts a power-to-weight ratio of approximately 10.4 lb/HP, indicating slightly better performance in relation to its weight. The Nissan 370Z Nismo offers a power-to-weight ratio of approximately 10.9 lb/HP, reflecting slightly lower performance in comparison.


Additional Metrics:

Unfortunately, specific lap times and RPM (shaft speed) data for these vehicles are not available from the manufacturers at this time.

Suspension

Nissan 370Z Nismo:

The Nissan 370Z Nismo has built a reputation for its raw and aggressive driving dynamics. Its suspension setup reflects this ethos with a focus on sharp handling and driver engagement.


Firm Sports-Tuned Suspension:

Stiffer springs and dampers enhance responsiveness.

Reduced body roll ensures precise cornering.

Offers a direct connection to the road, delivering a visceral experience.


Rear Multi-Link Suspension:

Provides excellent traction during acceleration and cornering.

Promotes stability and control under high-speed maneuvers.

Offers a balanced and predictable feel, inspiring driver confidence.


Toyota Supra:

The Toyota Supra has made a comeback with its fifth-generation model, bringing back the iconic nameplate with a focus on modern performance and luxury. Its suspension system is designed to deliver a refined and composed ride without compromising agility.

Adaptive Variable Suspension (AVS):

Automatically adjusts damping force according to driving conditions.

Offers a comfortable ride for daily commuting and long trips.

Instantaneously adapts to provide a firm and responsive feel during spirited driving.


Double-Joint MacPherson Strut Front Suspension:

Provides a lightweight and compact design, contributing to improved handling.

Offers a good balance between comfort and performance.

Reduces unsprung weight, enhancing overall responsiveness.

Critique and Comparison:


While both the Nissan 370Z Nismo and the Toyota Supra are designed to deliver thrilling driving experiences, their suspension setups cater to different priorities.


Nissan 370Z Nismo:


Pros:

The firm sports-tuned suspension delivers sharp handling and excellent feedback.

The rear multi-link suspension ensures stability and control during aggressive driving.

Ideal for enthusiasts seeking an engaging and raw driving experience.


Cons:

The stiffer suspension may result in a slightly harsher ride on uneven roads.

Limited adjustability may not suit drivers looking for a more refined or versatile setup.




Toyota Supra:


Pros:

The adaptive variable suspension provides a comfortable ride for everyday use.

The double-joint MacPherson strut front suspension offers a good balance between comfort and performance.

Ideal for drivers seeking a well-rounded sports car with a focus on refinement and versatility.


Cons:

The adaptive suspension may lack the razor-sharp responsiveness of the Nissan 370Z Nismo's fixed setup.

Some enthusiasts may desire a more aggressive suspension tune for spirited driving.
