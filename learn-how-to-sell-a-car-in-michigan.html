How to sell a car in Michigan



Selling a car in Michigan can be a straightforward process if you understand the necessary steps and requirements. Whether you're selling your vehicle privately or trading it in at a dealership, this article will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to sell a car in Michigan. Let's explore the process step by step.

Gather All Relevant Documents:

Before listing your car for sale or approaching a dealership, make sure you have the following documents ready:

Vehicle Title: Ensure that you have the original vehicle title in your name. If there's an existing lien, you'll need to contact the lienholder for the necessary documentation.

Bill of Sale: Prepare a bill of sale to provide a written record of the transaction. Include information such as the buyer's name, seller's name, vehicle details (make, model, VIN), sale price, and date of sale.

Odometer Disclosure Statement: Complete the Odometer Disclosure Statement accurately, which is required by federal law to record the mileage on the vehicle.

Release of Liability: Complete a Release of Liability form, available from the Michigan Secretary of State (SOS) website. This form releases you from any liability associated with the vehicle after the sale.


Determine the Vehicle's Value:

Before setting an asking price, research the market value of your car. Utilize online resources, such as Kelley Blue Book (kbb.com) or NADA Guides (nadaguides.com), to get an estimate based on the vehicle's make, model, year, mileage, and condition.


Selling Privately:

If you decide to sell your car privately, follow these steps:


Advertise Your Car: Create compelling ads with clear photos and a detailed description of your vehicle. Utilize online platforms such as AutoTrader, Cars.com, or local classifieds to reach potential buyers.


Arrange Test Drives: Once potential buyers express interest, schedule safe and supervised test drives. Ensure that the driver has a valid driver's license and insurance coverage.


Negotiate the Sale: Be prepared to negotiate the selling price. Consider factors such as the vehicle's condition, mileage, service history, and market demand.


Complete the Sale: If you reach an agreement with a buyer, complete the bill of sale, collect payment (preferably through a secure method such as a cashier's check or electronic transfer), and provide the buyer with a signed title and other relevant documents.


Selling to a Dealership:

If you prefer to sell your car to a dealership, follow these steps:

Research Local Dealerships: Research reputable dealerships in your area that accept trade-ins or buy used cars.

Get Multiple Quotes: Reach out to several dealerships to get multiple quotes for your vehicle. Compare offers to ensure you're getting a fair price.

Visit the Dealership: Schedule an appointment with the chosen dealership to have your vehicle appraised. Bring all relevant documents and be prepared to negotiate the selling price.


Complete the Sale: If you agree on a price, sign the necessary paperwork, including the bill of sale, title transfer documents, and other required forms. Ensure that the dealership provides you with a Release of Liability form.


Notify the Michigan SOS:

After selling your vehicle, it's essential to notify the Michigan Secretary of State within 15 days. Visit a local SOS branch or submit the Notice of Sale online through the Michigan SOS website. This step ensures that you won't be held responsible for any future infractions or incidents involving the vehicle.


Selling a car in Michigan involves gathering the necessary documents, determining the vehicle's value, and choosing between selling privately or to a dealership. By following the steps outlined in this comprehensive guide, you can navigate the process smoothly and ensure a successful sale. Remember to research the market value, advertise effectively, negotiate wisely, and comply with the Michigan SOS requirements.
