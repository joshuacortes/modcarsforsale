BMW 5 Series (F10) vs. BMW 5 Series (G30)



Stock Motor

0-60 Acceleration:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) comes equipped with various engine options, providing a range of performance levels. The acceleration times may vary depending on the specific engine chosen. The range typically spans from approximately 4.5 seconds for the more powerful engines to around 7 seconds for the base models.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) introduces updated powertrain options designed to improve performance. The acceleration times have been further enhanced, with the G30 achieving impressive 0-60 mph (0-96 km/h) times ranging from approximately 3.9 seconds for the top-performing models to around 6 seconds for the entry-level variants.


Torque and Horsepower:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) offers a range of engine options with varying torque and horsepower outputs. The torque figures typically range from around 250 lb-ft to 553 lb-ft, depending on the engine selection. Horsepower output can range from approximately 241 HP to 591 HP, providing a broad spectrum of performance capabilities.

With the BMW 5 Series (G30), there is an emphasis on improved power and efficiency. The torque figures generally range from around 295 lb-ft to 627 lb-ft, while horsepower output can vary from approximately 248 HP to 617 HP. The G30's engines deliver more power and torque compared to its predecessor, enhancing overall performance.


Top Speed:

The top speed achievable by the BMW 5 Series (F10) depends on the engine and the model variant. The range typically extends from approximately 130 mph (209 km/h) for base models to around 155 mph (250 km/h) for higher-performance versions.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) offers increased top speeds across the range, thanks to upgraded powertrains. The top speed varies between approximately 130 mph (209 km/h) for base models and can go up to around 190 mph (305 km/h) for the high-performance M models.


Quarter-Mile Time:

The quarter-mile time is a key metric for straight-line acceleration. While the exact times may differ based on engine and model variations, the BMW 5 Series (F10) typically achieves quarter-mile times ranging from around 12 seconds for the more powerful variants to approximately 15 seconds for the base models.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) improves upon the quarter-mile times of its predecessor, with the range typically spanning from around 11 seconds for the high-performance models to approximately 14 seconds for the entry-level versions.


Lap Times:

Lap times are a measure of a vehicle's performance on a racetrack, indicating its handling and agility. While lap times can be influenced by numerous factors, including tires and driver skill, the BMW 5 Series (F10) exhibits competitive lap times, allowing drivers to enjoy a dynamic and engaging experience.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) builds upon the handling prowess of its predecessor, providing improved lap times and enhanced agility. With advancements in suspension and chassis technology, the G30 offers better cornering capabilities and stability, making it a more capable performer on the track.


Powertrain Configuration:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) offers a range of powertrain options, including inline-4, inline-6, and V8 engines, catering to different performance requirements. The engines are typically paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive, depending on the model and customer preference.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) continues this tradition, offering a selection of powertrain options designed to provide a balance of performance and efficiency. The powertrains include inline-4, inline-6, and V8 engines, paired with rear-wheel drive or xDrive all-wheel drive systems, ensuring optimal traction and control.


Transmission Type:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) offers a range of transmission options, including six-speed manual and eight-speed automatic transmissions. The transmission type depends on the engine and model variant, with some higher-performance models exclusively equipped with automatic transmissions.

Similarly, the BMW 5 Series (G30) offers a variety of transmission options, including six-speed manual and eight-speed automatic transmissions. The choice of transmission depends on the engine and model variant, with the automatic transmission being the standard choice for most configurations.


Power-to-Weight Ratio:

The power-to-weight ratio is a critical factor in determining a vehicle's performance potential. The BMW 5 Series (F10) provides a competitive power-to-weight ratio, which is achieved by pairing powerful engines with lightweight construction. The ratio typically ranges from around 10 to 13 pounds per horsepower, depending on the specific model.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) improves upon the power-to-weight ratio of its predecessor, thanks to advancements in powertrain technology and lightweight construction. The ratio generally falls within the range of 9 to 12 pounds per horsepower, providing improved performance and agility.


RPM (Shaft Speed):

The specific RPM range varies depending on the engine and powertrain configuration. However, both the BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) feature engines with high-revving capabilities, allowing for exhilarating acceleration and responsive power delivery. The engines can reach impressive RPM levels, providing enhanced performance and a dynamic driving experience.





Tuning Ability

Intake System:

Enhancing the intake system is a popular modification to improve engine performance. Aftermarket cold air intake kits or performance air filters can increase airflow to the engine, resulting in improved horsepower and torque. While both the BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) offer well-engineered stock intake systems, upgrading to aftermarket options can provide incremental gains and a more aggressive engine sound.


Exhaust System:

Modifying the exhaust system can have a significant impact on performance and sound. Aftermarket exhaust systems, such as cat-back or full exhaust systems, can improve exhaust flow, reduce backpressure, and enhance the overall engine note. Upgraded exhaust components, such as headers or high-flow catalytic converters, can further optimize performance gains. Custom exhaust options also allow for personalized sound profiles, giving the BMW 5 Series a unique auditory experience.


Forced Induction:

Forced induction is a popular method to achieve substantial power gains. Supercharging or turbocharging the engine can significantly increase horsepower and torque output. The BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) offer various engine options, some of which are already equipped with forced induction. Upgrading the existing forced induction system or retrofitting forced induction to naturally aspirated engines requires careful consideration and expertise to ensure compatibility and reliability.


Fuel Management:

Optimizing fuel management is crucial when pursuing higher performance levels. Upgraded fuel injectors, high-flow fuel pumps, and aftermarket engine control units (ECUs) can enhance fuel delivery and provide custom tuning options. Tuning the fuel management system ensures the engine operates with the correct air-fuel ratios, maximizing performance gains. It is essential to work with experienced tuners who understand the specific requirements of the BMW 5 Series models and have expertise in optimizing fuel management.


Ignition System:

Upgrading the ignition system can improve combustion efficiency and power delivery. High-performance ignition coils, spark plugs, and ignition controllers can provide a stronger and more consistent spark, resulting in better engine performance. Custom ignition systems can optimize ignition timing and improve throttle response, contributing to increased power and drivability. Choosing ignition components that are compatible with the engine's characteristics and designed for high-performance applications is vital for achieving desired results.


The BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) offer tremendous tuning potential for enthusiasts looking to extract even more performance from their vehicles. Upgrading the intake and exhaust systems, implementing forced induction, optimizing fuel management, and improving the ignition system are popular modifications that can unlock substantial power gains and enhance the driving experience.

When modifying any vehicle, it is crucial to work with reputable tuning companies that have expertise in BMW vehicles and understand the intricacies of the specific models. Proper installation, tuning, and compatibility with the vehicle's overall setup are essential to ensure reliable performance gains and preserve the longevity of the engine.





Suspension

Suspension Design and Layout:



The BMW 5 Series (F10) features a well-engineered suspension system that combines comfort and sportiness. It utilizes a double-wishbone front suspension and a multi-link rear suspension setup. This configuration provides a good balance between ride comfort and precise handling, allowing the F10 to tackle corners with confidence while maintaining a compliant ride.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) introduces an updated suspension system, building upon the foundation of its predecessor. It features a double-wishbone front suspension and a multi-link rear suspension similar to the F10. However, the G30 incorporates refinements and enhancements in terms of materials and design to further enhance handling and ride quality.


Ride Comfort and Handling:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) strikes a balance between ride comfort and sporty handling. The suspension system is tuned to provide a smooth and controlled ride, effectively absorbing road imperfections and minimizing vibrations. The F10 exhibits excellent stability at high speeds and offers precise and responsive handling, making it an enjoyable car to drive.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) takes ride comfort and handling to the next level. The suspension upgrades on the G30 provide a more refined and composed ride quality, offering improved isolation from road irregularities. Additionally, advancements in suspension technology and chassis design enhance the car's agility, allowing for better cornering and increased driver confidence.


Adaptive Suspension:

Both the BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) offer optional adaptive suspension systems. These systems continuously adjust damping characteristics based on road conditions, driving style, and selected driving modes. Adaptive suspension provides the ability to switch between comfort-oriented settings for a relaxed ride and sportier modes for enhanced handling and responsiveness.


Optional Suspension Upgrades:

Both the BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) offer optional suspension upgrades to further enhance their dynamic capabilities. These upgrades typically include features like adjustable dampers, sport-tuned suspensions, or air suspensions. These options allow drivers to fine-tune the suspension to their desired preferences, prioritizing either comfort or sportiness.


M Performance Models:

For those seeking even higher levels of performance, BMW offers M Performance models within the 5 Series lineup. These models come equipped with specially tuned suspensions, incorporating stiffer springs, enhanced damping, and additional chassis enhancements. The M Performance models deliver heightened handling and a more engaging driving experience.





Interior

Interior Design and Layout:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) showcases a sophisticated interior design that exudes elegance and refinement. The dashboard is well laid out, featuring high-quality materials such as premium leather, wood trim, and aluminum accents. The F10's cabin emphasizes a driver-centric layout, with controls and displays angled towards the driver for easy access and enhanced ergonomics.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) builds upon the foundation set by its predecessor, introducing a more modern and refined interior design. The dashboard features clean lines, soft-touch materials, and subtle ambient lighting, creating a contemporary and luxurious ambiance. The G30's layout maintains a driver-focused approach, with intuitive controls and a harmonious blend of technology and craftsmanship.


Technology and Infotainment:

Both the BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) offer an array of advanced technology and infotainment features. The F10 features an iDrive system with a central display screen, providing access to navigation, multimedia, and vehicle settings. The G30 takes this technology further with an updated iDrive system, larger touchscreens, and enhanced voice control capabilities. Additionally, the G30 introduces BMW's latest digital instrument cluster, providing a customizable and futuristic visual experience.


Comfort and Seating:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) provides a comfortable and spacious cabin, offering well-contoured seats that provide excellent support for long journeys. The seating materials and upholstery options range from premium leather to high-quality synthetic fabrics, catering to individual preferences. The F10's interior focuses on providing a refined and serene atmosphere, ensuring passenger comfort during extended drives.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) continues to prioritize comfort and ergonomics, offering even more luxurious seating options. The seats feature increased adjustability, allowing occupants to find their ideal position. The G30 introduces enhanced seating materials, including available Nappa leather upholstery, further elevating the interior's premium feel. The G30's cabin provides an exceptionally comfortable and inviting space for both the driver and passengers.


Interior Space and Practicality:

Both the BMW 5 Series (F10) and BMW 5 Series (G30) offer generous interior space, accommodating passengers with ample headroom and legroom. The rear seats provide a comfortable seating position, allowing passengers to enjoy extended journeys without feeling cramped. Additionally, the 5 Series models offer practical storage options, including door pockets, center console compartments, and a decent-sized trunk for luggage.


Craftsmanship and Attention to Detail:

The BMW 5 Series (F10) showcases exemplary craftsmanship, with high-quality materials and meticulous attention to detail. The interior surfaces are precisely finished, and the controls and switchgear exhibit a solid and refined feel. The F10's interior exudes a sense of luxury and durability.

The BMW 5 Series (G30) elevates the level of craftsmanship with enhanced materials, refined finishes, and improved fit and finish. The attention to detail is evident throughout the cabin, with carefully designed accents and a harmonious integration of technology. The G30's interior exudes sophistication and quality, surpassing its predecessor in terms of overall craftsmanship.
