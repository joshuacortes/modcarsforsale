Toyota Celica

Toyota's R series engine was used to power the first three generations of Celicas sold in North America. All-wheel drive turbocharged variants were available from 1986 to 1999. The car's drive configuration was modified from rear-wheel drive to front-wheel drive in August 1985. Beginning in December 1997, some Japanese models began to use variable valve timing, and starting with the 2000 model year, all vehicles had this feature as standard. The six-cylinder Celica Supra variant was separated off as a new vehicle in 1986 and became known simply as the Supra.


Modified Toyota Celica

Performance Enhancements

One of the primary motivations for modifying a Toyota Celica is to boost its performance capabilities. The Celica's lightweight and well-balanced chassis provides an excellent foundation for upgrades. Popular modifications in this category include:

Engine Upgrades: Enthusiasts often opt for engine swaps or enhancements, such as turbocharging or supercharging, to increase power output. The 3S-GTE engine is a common choice for turbocharging, turning the Celica into a high-performance machine.

Suspension and Handling: Upgrading suspension components, like struts, sway bars, and coilovers, helps improve cornering abilities and provides a more engaging driving experience.

Braking System: Enhanced brake kits with larger rotors and high-performance pads and calipers contribute to better stopping power, essential for aggressive driving and track use.


Aftermarket Support

The Toyota Celica benefits from a thriving aftermarket community, with a wide range of parts and accessories available for customization. Enthusiasts can find components for engine upgrades, body kits, interior enhancements, and more. This extensive support makes it easier for owners to personalize their Celicas according to their specific tastes and preferences.


Motorsports History and Success

The Toyota Celica has a notable history in motorsports, particularly in rally racing. The Celica GT-Four, with its all-wheel-drive system and turbocharged power, achieved considerable success in the World Rally Championship (WRC) during the 1990s. This rich history not only adds to the car's appeal but also inspires owners to modify their Celicas for motorsports events and spirited drives.


Driving Experience

Driving a modified Toyota Celica is an exhilarating experience. The combination of the car's responsive steering, balanced chassis, and well-tuned suspension provides a dynamic and engaging feel on the road. The roar of a modified engine and the precise handling add to the excitement, making every drive a memorable one. Whether you're navigating winding roads or taking it to the track, the Celica offers a rewarding driving experience that keeps enthusiasts coming back for more.


Modified Toyota Celicas stand as a testament to the enduring appeal of this iconic sports car. With a solid foundation, a wealth of aftermarket support, a successful history in motorsports, and a thrilling driving experience, the Celica is a natural choice for those looking to personalize and enhance their cars.

Whether you're seeking a significant power boost, fine-tuning the suspension for better handling, or simply want to turn heads with a customized appearance, the Toyota Celica offers a versatile platform for automotive enthusiasts to express their passion for performance and style. COMMON TOYOTA CELICA MODIFICATIONS:

1. Suspension Upgrades

Upgrading the suspension is one of the most common and essential modifications for Toyota Celica owners. It not only improves handling but also contributes to the car's overall performance. Common suspension upgrades include:

Coilovers: Coilover suspension systems allow for ride height adjustment and fine-tuning of damping, providing better control and a more dynamic driving experience.

Strut Bars: Installing front and rear strut bars enhances chassis rigidity, reduces body roll, and improves cornering capabilities.

Sway Bars: Upgraded sway bars minimize body sway during cornering, resulting in improved stability and sharper turns.


2. Engine Enhancements

Enhancing the engine's performance is a favorite pastime for Celica owners who seek more power and an exhilarating driving experience. Common engine modifications include:

Cold Air Intakes: Cold air intakes improve airflow to the engine, increasing horsepower and throttle response.

Exhaust Systems: Upgrading the exhaust system with high-flow headers and performance mufflers can enhance engine efficiency and produce a sportier exhaust note.

Turbocharger/Supercharger: For those looking for significant power gains, turbocharging or supercharging is a popular route to increase horsepower and torque.


3. Wheels and Tires

Custom wheels and tires not only enhance the Celica's aesthetics but also impact its performance and handling. Common wheel and tire modifications include:

Alloy Wheels: Upgrading to lightweight alloy wheels improves the car's looks and reduces unsprung weight, leading to better handling and braking.

Performance Tires: Installing high-performance tires provides improved grip, cornering capabilities, and overall traction, especially during spirited driving.


4. Exterior Styling

Many Celica owners aim to make their cars stand out with unique exterior modifications. Common exterior styling enhancements include:

Body Kits: Adding front and rear bumpers, side skirts, and rear spoilers can give the Celica a sportier and more aggressive appearance.

Custom Paint and Vinyl Wraps: Changing the car's color or applying vinyl wraps allows for personalization and a refreshed look.


5. Interior Upgrades

Enhancing the interior can make your driving experience more enjoyable and comfortable. Common interior modifications include:

Aftermarket Seats: Upgrading to racing or sport seats provides better support and comfort during spirited driving.

Steering Wheels: Custom steering wheels not only look great but also provide a better grip and improved control.

Upgraded Audio Systems: Installing a higher-quality audio system with a powerful head unit and speakers can make long drives more enjoyable.


6. Brake Systems

Improving the Celica's braking system is vital for safety and performance. Common brake modifications include:

Upgraded Brake Pads and Rotors: High-performance brake pads and slotted or drilled rotors enhance braking performance and reduce brake fade during intense driving.


The Toyota Celica's popularity among automotive enthusiasts is no accident. Its combination of sporty design, balanced handling, and potential for customization make it a natural choice for those who want to personalize their cars. These popular and common modifications enhance not only the Celica's aesthetics but also its performance, ensuring an even more thrilling driving experience. Whether you're focused on better handling, more power, or a unique look, the Celica offers a versatile platform to express your passion for cars and driving.
