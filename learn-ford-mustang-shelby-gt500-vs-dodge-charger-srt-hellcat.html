Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 vs. Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat



Stock Motor

When it comes to American muscle cars, the Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat are two heavyweight contenders that represent the pinnacle of performance and power. These iconic machines are renowned for their blistering acceleration and thrilling driving experiences. In this article, we will compare the stock motors of the Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat to see how they stack up against each other.


Ford Mustang Shelby GT500:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 is a legendary nameplate that embodies the raw power and performance of American muscle. Under its muscular hood lies a supercharged 5.2-liter V8 engine that produces an astonishing 760 horsepower and 625 lb-ft of torque. This beast of an engine is mated to a seven-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission, enabling lightning-fast gear changes and optimal power delivery.

The GT500's engine is a marvel of engineering, featuring a supercharger and a cross-plane crankshaft for maximum performance. It boasts impressive acceleration, rocketing from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.3 seconds and achieving a top speed of around 180 mph. With its aggressive styling and track-focused features, the GT500 is a force to be reckoned with on both the street and the track.


Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat:

The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat is a four-door muscle sedan that pushes the boundaries of power and performance. Its heart is a supercharged 6.2-liter V8 engine, delivering a jaw-dropping 707 horsepower and 650 lb-ft of torque. This mighty powerplant is paired with an eight-speed automatic transmission, ensuring seamless shifts and instant throttle response.

The Hellcat's engine is the epitome of American muscle, providing exhilarating acceleration and a thunderous soundtrack. It sprints from 0 to 60 mph in just 3.6 seconds and reaches a top speed of approximately 196 mph. The Charger SRT Hellcat combines its impressive straight-line performance with comfortable seating for five, making it a practical yet adrenaline-inducing machine.


Comparison:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat are both supercharged powerhouses that deliver mind-boggling performance. The GT500's 5.2-liter V8 engine offers incredible horsepower and torque figures, allowing for blistering acceleration and an unforgettable driving experience. Its track-focused design and aggressive styling make it a true performance icon.

The Charger SRT Hellcat, on the other hand, boasts a larger 6.2-liter V8 engine and slightly higher horsepower. Its four-door configuration adds a touch of practicality while still providing jaw-dropping speed and power. The Hellcat's menacing presence and comfortable interior make it a unique offering in the high-performance sedan segment.


When comparing the stock motors of the Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat, it's clear that both vehicles offer exhilarating performance and breathtaking power. The GT500's track-focused design and its supercharged V8 engine deliver astonishing acceleration and a visceral driving experience. Meanwhile, the Charger SRT Hellcat combines the thrill of a muscle car with the practicality of a four-door sedan.

Ultimately, choosing between the GT500 and the Hellcat depends on personal preferences. If you desire the iconic Mustang heritage, aggressive styling, and track-ready performance, the GT500 is an exceptional choice. On the other hand, if you value the combination of raw power, practicality, and a unique four-door muscle car experience, the Charger SRT Hellcat will undoubtedly deliver.

Regardless of your preference, both the Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat showcase the indomitable spirit of American muscle cars and provide an exhilarating driving experience that enthusiasts crave.





Tuning Ability

Intake System:

The intake system is vital for optimizing air intake and fuel combustion. Both the Shelby GT500 and the Charger SRT Hellcat come equipped with well-engineered intake systems from the factory. Upgrading the intake system can enhance airflow and improve engine performance. Aftermarket options such as cold air intakes, high-flow air filters, and larger throttle bodies can increase horsepower and torque by maximizing the engine's breathing capabilities. It is important to select components that are properly calibrated and matched to the specific engine to achieve optimal results.


Exhaust System:

The exhaust system plays a significant role in enhancing performance and sound. Upgrading the exhaust system on the Shelby GT500 and the Charger SRT Hellcat can result in increased power, improved throttle response, and a more aggressive exhaust note. Aftermarket options such as cat-back exhaust systems, headers, and high-flow catalytic converters can reduce exhaust backpressure, improve exhaust gas flow, and unleash additional horsepower and torque. Compliance with local noise regulations is important when selecting and installing aftermarket exhaust components.


Forced Induction:

Forced induction systems, such as superchargers or turbochargers, offer substantial power gains by forcing more air into the engine. The Shelby GT500 and the Charger SRT Hellcat come equipped with supercharged engines from the factory, showcasing their immense power potential. Aftermarket forced induction upgrades can provide even greater performance gains. Upgrading to a larger supercharger, adding an intercooler, or installing a more efficient supercharger pulley can significantly increase horsepower and torque. However, careful tuning and consideration of supporting modifications are necessary to ensure proper engine performance and reliability.


Fuel Management:

Fuel management systems play a crucial role in optimizing engine performance. Upgrading the fuel management system, including the fuel injectors, fuel pump, and engine control unit (ECU) calibration, can maximize the potential power output of the Shelby GT500 and the Charger SRT Hellcat. Aftermarket tuning solutions can provide custom fuel maps, ignition timing adjustments, and other parameters to extract additional performance gains while maintaining safe operating conditions. Expert tuning and compatibility with the specific engine and fuel system are essential to achieving optimal results.


Ignition System:

The ignition system is responsible for delivering a precisely timed spark to the combustion chambers. Upgrading the ignition system can improve ignition performance and potentially increase horsepower. Aftermarket ignition coils, spark plugs with enhanced spark characteristics, and ignition system controllers can enhance combustion efficiency, throttle response, and power output. It is crucial to select components that are compatible with the specific engine and fuel management system and ensure that the ignition system upgrades are properly tuned to optimize performance.


Both the Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat offer incredible tuning potential for enthusiasts seeking to maximize their performance capabilities. Aftermarket modifications to the intake, exhaust, forced induction, fuel management, and ignition systems can unlock significant power gains and enhance overall customization options.

Upgrading the intake and exhaust systems can improve airflow and unleash additional horsepower and torque, while forced induction upgrades can deliver substantial power increases. Upgrading the fuel management system and ignition system can optimize fuel delivery, combustion efficiency, and ignition performance for enhanced power output.

It is crucial to work with reputable tuners and ensure that modifications are properly matched, calibrated, and tested to maintain reliable engine performance. Each modification should be approached with a comprehensive understanding of the vehicle's specific requirements and the goal of achieving a balance between power gains and engine reliability.





Suspension

Suspension Design and Tuning:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 features a sophisticated suspension setup that combines performance-oriented design with everyday usability. Its independent front and rear suspension, along with MagneRide damping system, provide precise control, improved handling, and superior ride quality. The GT500's suspension is tuned for aggressive driving, delivering exceptional cornering ability and minimal body roll. The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat incorporates a well-engineered suspension system that is tailored to balance performance and comfort. The Charger SRT Hellcat features an independent front suspension and a multi-link rear suspension, complemented by adaptive damping. The suspension tuning strikes a balance between sporty handling and a smooth ride, making it suitable for both spirited driving and long-distance cruising.


Handling and Performance:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 is renowned for its exceptional handling capabilities. The combination of its advanced suspension setup, precise steering, and impressive grip allows for confident cornering and precise control. The GT500's suspension is designed to provide outstanding feedback and dynamic handling, making it an ideal choice for enthusiastic driving and track performance. The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat offers a balance between performance and comfort, allowing for a more relaxed driving experience while still delivering impressive handling. The Charger SRT Hellcat's suspension provides excellent stability, predictable handling, and a composed ride. While not as track-focused as the GT500, it offers a solid balance of performance and everyday drivability.


Adjustability and Customization:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500's suspension system offers adjustable settings, allowing drivers to fine-tune the suspension characteristics to their preferences. The MagneRide damping system offers multiple selectable driving modes, including Normal, Sport, and Track, allowing drivers to tailor the suspension's responsiveness to different driving conditions and road surfaces. The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat offers limited adjustability in its suspension setup, focusing more on providing a balanced and comfortable driving experience without extensive customization options. The suspension is tuned to deliver a confident and controlled ride, but it does not provide the same level of adaptability as the GT500.


Ride Comfort:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500's suspension setup prioritizes performance and handling, which may result in a firmer ride compared to more comfort-oriented vehicles. The suspension's firmness provides a connected and engaging driving experience, but it can transmit more road imperfections to the cabin. The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat, while still offering impressive performance capabilities, provides a more comfortable and forgiving ride. Its suspension strikes a balance between handling and comfort, ensuring a smoother driving experience and better absorption of road imperfections.


The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat offer distinct suspension experiences tailored to their respective performance goals. The GT500's suspension system delivers exceptional handling, precise control, and track-ready capabilities. Its suspension setup is designed to provide dynamic handling characteristics and an engaging driving experience. The Charger SRT Hellcat's suspension provides a balanced combination of performance and comfort, offering a smoother and more comfortable ride without compromising handling and control. It is an ideal choice for those seeking a high-performance sedan that can deliver both spirited driving and everyday usability.





Interior

Interior Design and Layout:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 features a driver-focused interior design that combines modern aesthetics with classic Mustang heritage. The cabin is designed to provide an immersive and sporty driving experience, with controls and gauges within easy reach. The GT500's interior incorporates high-quality materials, such as soft-touch surfaces, premium leather upholstery, and carbon fiber accents, creating a sense of luxury and sophistication. The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat showcases a spacious and comfortable interior with a design that blends performance and practicality. The cabin features a driver-centric layout with user-friendly controls and a mix of soft-touch materials and premium upholstery. The Charger SRT Hellcat's interior design exudes a sporty ambiance while offering ample room for both front and rear passengers.


Materials and Build Quality:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500's interior is crafted with high-quality materials and meticulous attention to detail. The use of premium leather, aluminum accents, and soft-touch surfaces contributes to a luxurious and refined cabin. The GT500's interior exhibits solid build quality, ensuring durability and longevity in its construction. The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat also employs high-quality materials throughout its interior, offering a combination of soft-touch surfaces, premium upholstery, and durable components. While it may not match the same level of refinement as the GT500, the Charger SRT Hellcat provides a well-built and comfortable cabin that withstands the demands of daily use.


Seating and Comfort:

Both the Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat provide comfortable seating arrangements suitable for extended drives and spirited driving. The GT500's seats offer excellent bolstering and lateral support, ensuring drivers feel connected to the car during high-performance maneuvers. The Charger SRT Hellcat's seats provide a balance between comfort and support, offering sufficient cushioning and legroom for both front and rear passengers.


Technology and Infotainment:

Modern technology and infotainment systems are essential components of a car's interior. The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 incorporates an advanced infotainment system with a user-friendly touchscreen display, offering access to various connectivity features, including Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. The GT500's system integrates seamlessly with the driver's needs, providing navigation, audio controls, and performance data.

The Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat features a similar user-friendly infotainment system, providing touchscreen controls and smartphone integration features. While it may not offer the same level of advanced features as the GT500, the Charger SRT Hellcat's infotainment system provides essential functions for navigation, audio, and connectivity.


Storage and Practicality:

As performance-oriented vehicles, both the Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat prioritize performance over extensive storage. Trunk space is limited in both vehicles, with enough capacity for everyday essentials or a weekend getaway. The Charger SRT Hellcat offers the advantage of four doors, providing easier access to the rear seats and additional practicality.


The Ford Mustang Shelby GT500 and the Dodge Charger SRT Hellcat offer distinct interior experiences tailored to their respective performance-oriented objectives. The GT500's interior combines modern design, high-quality materials, advanced technology, and a luxurious ambiance, creating an immersive and driver-focused environment. The Charger SRT Hellcat's interior showcases a spacious and comfortable cabin with a sporty appeal and user-friendly controls.
