Mazda RX8

The RX8 is a sports car that Mazda, a Japanese automaker, produced between 2002 and 2012. At the North American International Auto Show in 2001, it made its debut. It is the RX-7's successor and, like the RX models before it, is propelled by a rotary Wankel engine. North American consumers could purchase the RX-8 starting with the 2003 model year.

On August 23, 2011, Mazda announced that the RX-8 would be retired, with the 2012 model year serving as its final year of production.

1. Performance Enhancements:

a. Engine Upgrades: The RX-8's rotary engine has a lot of untapped potential. Enthusiasts often opt for upgrades like porting and polishing, engine rebuilds, and forced induction (turbocharging or supercharging) to extract more power. Careful tuning and engine management systems are crucial for achieving reliable and substantial performance gains.

b. Exhaust Systems: Upgrading the exhaust system can improve both power and sound. Performance exhaust systems, like those from Borla, HKS, or Racing Beat, offer a better flow and a deeper exhaust note, enhancing the overall driving experience.

c. Suspension Tuning: The RX-8's excellent handling can be further refined with suspension upgrades. Coilover kits, adjustable sway bars, and upgraded bushings can enhance cornering ability, making it more agile and responsive.


2. Aftermarket Support:

The RX-8 enjoys strong aftermarket support, with a range of manufacturers offering performance parts and accessories. Well-established companies like Racing Beat, RE Amemiya, and Pettit Racing provide a variety of options for engine, suspension, and exterior modifications. Enthusiast communities and forums are valuable resources for discovering new products and gathering insights on effective modifications.


3. Design Modifications:

a. Aerodynamics: Many RX-8 owners opt for front splitters, rear wings, and diffusers to improve aerodynamics and downforce. These modifications can enhance stability at higher speeds.

b. Body Kits: Upgrading the RX-8's aesthetics is a popular choice. Body kits, like those offered by Veilside, MS Design, and RE Amemiya, can transform the car's appearance while maintaining its sporty identity.

c. Wheels and Tires: Changing the wheels and tires can significantly impact both performance and aesthetics. Lightweight alloy wheels and performance-oriented tires can enhance grip and handling.


4. Motorsports Success/History:

The Mazda RX-8 has a notable presence in motorsports, particularly in endurance racing. The RX-8 achieved success in series like the Grand-Am Rolex Sports Car Series and the American Le Mans Series (ALMS). Its reliability, nimble handling, and rotary engine's unique characteristics made it a competitive choice in endurance races.


5. Driving Experience:


Modifying a Mazda RX-8 can transform the driving experience. With the right upgrades, the car becomes more powerful, better-handling, and a thrill to drive. The enhanced exhaust note, improved acceleration, and precise handling result in a more enjoyable and engaging driving experience, whether you're on the street or the track.






COMMON MAZDA RX8 MODIFICATIONS:

1. Engine Performance:

a. Intake Upgrades: One of the most common modifications is upgrading the intake system. Replacing the stock airbox with a cold air intake or a short ram intake can increase airflow and provide a more robust induction sound. Brands like AEM and K&N offer popular intake solutions.

b. Exhaust Systems: Upgrading the exhaust system is a popular choice for both power and sound improvement. Performance exhaust systems from manufacturers like Racing Beat and Borla offer better exhaust flow, enhanced engine sound, and power gains.

c. Forced Induction: Enthusiasts looking for significant power boosts often turn to forced induction. Turbocharger and supercharger kits from companies like Pettit Racing and AEM are available for the RX-8. These modifications require careful installation and tuning but can provide substantial power increases.

d. Engine Management: To support engine modifications, aftermarket engine management systems like the AEM Infinity or Haltech are popular. They allow for precise tuning and optimization of air-fuel ratios, ignition timing, and other engine parameters.


2. Suspension Tuning:

a. Coilover Suspension: Upgrading to a quality coilover suspension system, such as KW Suspension or Tein, can significantly improve handling and ride comfort. Coilovers offer adjustable ride height, damping, and rebound control, allowing for precise tuning.

b. Sway Bars and Strut Tower Braces: Upgrading sway bars and adding strut tower braces can reduce body roll and improve chassis rigidity. This results in enhanced stability and sharper cornering, making it a popular choice among RX-8 owners.


3. Styling and Body Modifications:

a. Aerodynamics: Enhancing aerodynamics with front splitters, rear wings, and diffusers not only improves the RX-8's aesthetics but also helps stabilize the car at higher speeds.

b. Body Kits: Aftermarket body kits from brands like Veilside and MS Design offer a wide range of options for transforming the RX-8's appearance. These kits can provide aggressive lines and unique designs, allowing owners to make their cars stand out.


4. Wheel and Tire Upgrades:

Changing the wheels and tires can have a significant impact on both performance and aesthetics. Lightweight alloy wheels from Enkei or Volk Racing, paired with performance-oriented tires, can improve grip, handling, and the car's overall visual appeal.


5. Braking System Enhancements:

Upgrading the braking system with performance rotors, calipers, and high-friction pads is crucial for improved stopping power. This is particularly valuable for RX-8 owners who enjoy spirited driving and track days.


6. Interior Customization:

Many RX-8 enthusiasts choose to upgrade the interior with aftermarket seats, steering wheels, shift knobs, and gauges. These enhancements can enhance the driving experience and add a personalized touch to the cabin.


Modifying a Mazda RX-8 is a passionate endeavor that allows owners to unlock the car's full potential and make it uniquely their own. The RX-8's rotary engine and sporty chassis offer an excellent platform for customization, whether you aim to increase power, refine handling, enhance aesthetics, or all of the above.

Before diving into modifications, it's essential to plan your upgrades carefully, consult with experienced tuners or mechanics, and research the enthusiast community for valuable insights. With the right modifications, your Mazda RX-8 can become a more powerful, better-handling, and visually appealing sports car that delivers a thrilling and unique driving experience on both the street and the track.

3. AVO Turboworld Turbo Kit:

4. Full-Race Turbo Kit:

Full-Race Engineering is renowned for its expertise in forced induction solutions. Their turbo kits are designed for optimal performance and reliability. The Full-Race RX-8 turbo kit is available with various turbocharger options, allowing you to tailor your setup to your desired power level. Full-Race kits are known for their quality and compatibility with stock components.


5. GReddy Turbo Kits:

GReddy, a well-known name in the aftermarket automotive world, offers turbo kits for the RX-8. Their kits, like the GReddy T518Z or T67, are engineered for optimal performance and reliability. Adding a turbocharger to your Mazda RX-8 is a thrilling modification that can unlock the car's full potential and provide a significant increase in power.
