Lexus ES (6th generation) vs. Lexus ES (7th generation)



Stock Motor

Lexus ES (6th Generation):

0-60 Acceleration: The sixth-generation Lexus ES achieves a 0-60 mph time that varies depending on the engine and trim level, typically ranging from 6.5 to 8 seconds.

Torque: The torque output of the stock motors in the sixth-generation Lexus ES ranges from around 248 lb-ft to 267 lb-ft, depending on the engine configuration.

Horsepower: The horsepower ratings of the sixth-generation Lexus ES range from approximately 204 to 268 horsepower, depending on the engine and trim level.

Top Speed: The top speed of the sixth-generation Lexus ES varies among different models but generally falls within the range of 130 to 145 mph.

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time of the sixth-generation Lexus ES typically ranges from the mid-14-second to low-15-second range, depending on the engine and trim.

Lap Times: Official lap times for the sixth-generation Lexus ES are not readily available, as it is primarily designed as a luxury sedan rather than a performance-oriented vehicle.

Powertrain Configuration: The sixth-generation Lexus ES features a front-engine, front-wheel-drive layout, providing a comfortable and smooth driving experience.

Transmission Type: The stock transmission options include a 6-speed automatic and a continuously variable transmission (CVT), offering smooth and efficient gear shifts.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: The power-to-weight ratio of the sixth-generation Lexus ES varies depending on the engine and trim level, typically ranging from 14 to 18 pounds per horsepower.

RPM (Shaft Speed): The engine in the sixth-generation Lexus ES achieves its maximum power output at varying RPM ranges, depending on the specific engine variant.


Lexus ES (7th Generation):

0-60 Acceleration: The seventh-generation Lexus ES delivers improved acceleration, with 0-60 mph times ranging from 6.0 to 7.6 seconds, depending on the engine and trim level.

Torque: The torque output of the stock motors in the seventh-generation Lexus ES ranges from around 184 lb-ft to 267 lb-ft, depending on the engine configuration.

Horsepower: The horsepower ratings of the seventh-generation Lexus ES range from approximately 203 to 302 horsepower, depending on the engine and trim level.

Top Speed: The top speed of the seventh-generation Lexus ES varies depending on the model and engine, typically falling within the range of 130 to 155 mph.

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time of the seventh-generation Lexus ES typically ranges from the high 14-second to low 15-second range, depending on the engine and trim.

Lap Times: Official lap times for the seventh-generation Lexus ES are not readily available, as it remains primarily a luxury sedan rather than a dedicated performance vehicle.

Powertrain Configuration: The seventh-generation Lexus ES also features a front-engine, front-wheel-drive layout, ensuring a comfortable and refined driving experience.

Transmission Type: The stock transmission options include an 8-speed automatic and a continuously variable transmission (CVT), providing smooth and responsive gear shifts.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: The power-to-weight ratio of the seventh-generation Lexus ES ranges from approximately 14 to 19 pounds per horsepower, depending on the engine and trim level.

RPM (Shaft Speed): The engine in the seventh-generation Lexus ES achieves its maximum power output at varying RPM ranges, depending on the specific engine variant.

Suspension

The suspension systems of the Lexus ES (6th generation) and the Lexus ES (7th generation) play a crucial role in delivering a smooth and comfortable driving experience. Let's explore the differences in their suspension setups, including design, components, and overall performance:

Lexus ES (6th Generation):

Front Suspension: The sixth-generation Lexus ES features a MacPherson strut front suspension, which provides a balance between ride comfort and responsive handling.

Rear Suspension: The rear suspension of the sixth-generation Lexus ES incorporates a dual-link independent setup, enhancing stability and minimizing road noise.

Springs and Dampers: The suspension system of the sixth-generation Lexus ES utilizes coil springs and gas-charged shock absorbers to provide a comfortable ride while maintaining control.

Stabilizer Bars: Front and rear stabilizer bars are present in the sixth-generation Lexus ES to reduce body roll during cornering and enhance overall stability.

Adaptive Suspension (Optional): Some higher-end trims of the sixth-generation Lexus ES offer an optional adaptive suspension system that adjusts damping settings based on driving conditions.


Lexus ES (7th Generation):

Front Suspension: The seventh-generation Lexus ES also employs a MacPherson strut front suspension, maintaining a similar design philosophy for a comfortable and controlled ride.

Rear Suspension: The rear suspension of the seventh-generation Lexus ES features a trailing arm multi-link setup, enhancing stability and ride quality by isolating road imperfections.

Springs and Dampers: Similar to its predecessor, the suspension system of the seventh-generation Lexus ES utilizes coil springs and gas-charged shock absorbers for optimal ride comfort and control.

Stabilizer Bars: Front and rear stabilizer bars continue to be present in the seventh-generation Lexus ES, contributing to improved cornering stability and reduced body roll.

Adaptive Variable Suspension (Optional): The seventh-generation Lexus ES offers an optional adaptive variable suspension system that adjusts damping characteristics in real-time, enhancing ride comfort and handling responsiveness.


Differences:

Rear Suspension Design: While both generations feature independent rear suspensions, the sixth-generation utilizes a dual-link design, while the seventh-generation adopts a trailing arm multi-link setup for improved stability and ride quality.

Adaptive Suspension: The sixth-generation Lexus ES offers an optional adaptive suspension system, which adjusts damping settings based on driving conditions. In the seventh-generation, this feature is further enhanced with an optional adaptive variable suspension, providing even greater control over ride characteristics.

Overall Ride Quality: While both generations prioritize a smooth and comfortable ride, the seventh-generation Lexus ES benefits from refinements in suspension tuning and improved design, resulting in an even more refined and controlled driving experience.





Interior

Lexus ES (6th Generation):

Design: The interior design of the sixth-generation Lexus ES emphasizes a harmonious blend of luxury and functionality, featuring a clean and elegant layout with premium materials.

Materials: The sixth-generation Lexus ES incorporates high-quality materials such as leather upholstery, soft-touch surfaces, and real wood trim, providing a luxurious and refined atmosphere.

Seating: The sixth-generation Lexus ES offers comfortable seating for up to five occupants, with plush and supportive front seats and ample legroom and headroom in the rear.

Infotainment: The sixth-generation Lexus ES features a comprehensive infotainment system with a color touchscreen display, offering features such as navigation, Bluetooth connectivity, and available premium audio systems.

Driver Assistance: Depending on the trim level, the sixth-generation Lexus ES may include driver assistance features such as blind-spot monitoring, adaptive cruise control, and lane departure warning.


Lexus ES (7th Generation):

Design: The interior design of the seventh-generation Lexus ES showcases a more modern and refined approach, featuring sleek lines and a driver-centric cockpit.

Materials: The seventh-generation Lexus ES elevates the interior ambiance with upgraded materials, including premium leather upholstery, genuine wood trim, and soft-touch surfaces, delivering a heightened sense of luxury.

Seating: The seventh-generation Lexus ES provides enhanced seating comfort with improved ergonomics and additional support, ensuring a relaxing and comfortable driving experience for both the driver and passengers.

Infotainment: The seventh-generation Lexus ES introduces an updated infotainment system with a larger color touchscreen display, offering advanced features such as Apple CarPlay, Android Auto, and available Mark Levinson premium audio systems.

Driver Assistance: Similar to its predecessor, the seventh-generation Lexus ES offers a range of driver assistance features, including blind-spot monitoring, adaptive cruise control, lane keeping assist, and automatic emergency braking.


Differences:

Interior Design: The seventh-generation Lexus ES features a more modern and driver-focused interior design compared to the sixth-generation, offering a sleeker and refined aesthetic.

Material Quality: The seventh-generation Lexus ES elevates the overall interior ambiance with upgraded materials, including premium leather upholstery, genuine wood trim, and enhanced fit and finish.

Seating Comfort: The seventh-generation Lexus ES provides enhanced seating comfort with improved ergonomics, additional support, and refined cushioning, ensuring a more relaxing and comfortable driving experience.

Infotainment Technology: The seventh-generation Lexus ES introduces an updated and more advanced infotainment system with a larger touchscreen display and improved connectivity features, offering a more modern and connected driving experience.
