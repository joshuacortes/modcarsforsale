Toyota MR-S vs. Ford Probe GT



Stock Motor

In the world of sports cars, performance metrics are often the battleground where enthusiasts debate the superiority of different models. The Toyota MR-S and the Ford Probe GT are two iconic vehicles that have left their mark in the automotive landscape. Let's break down the numbers and details behind their stock motors in terms of acceleration, power, and overall performance.


Toyota MR-S:

0-60 mph Acceleration: The Toyota MR-S, also known as the MR2 Spyder, achieves a 0-60 mph time of around 7.5 to 8.0 seconds.

Torque and Horsepower: The MR-S is powered by a 1.8-liter inline-4 engine producing approximately 138 horsepower and around 127 lb-ft of torque.

Top Speed: The MR-S boasts a top speed of approximately 130 to 135 mph.

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time for the MR-S is typically in the range of 15.5 to 16.0 seconds.

Lap Times: While specific lap times can vary based on factors like driver skill and track conditions, the MR-S is known for its agile handling and balanced chassis on twisty tracks.


Ford Probe GT:

0-60 mph Acceleration: The Ford Probe GT achieves a 0-60 mph time of approximately 7.5 to 8.0 seconds, similar to the MR-S.

Torque and Horsepower: The Probe GT features a 2.5-liter V6 engine delivering around 164 to 168 horsepower and approximately 156 to 160 lb-ft of torque.

Top Speed: The Probe GT reaches a top speed of around 130 to 135 mph, on par with the MR-S.

Quarter Mile Time: The quarter-mile time for the Probe GT falls in the range of 15.0 to 15.5 seconds, showcasing its stronger power output.

Lap Times: Like the MR-S, the Probe GT's lap times can vary, but its V6 powertrain provides good straight-line speed and solid cornering capabilities.


Comparative Analysis:

Power and Torque: While the MR-S has a lightweight body and balanced handling, the Probe GT's V6 engine provides more power and torque, offering a strong mid-range punch and improved acceleration.

Acceleration and Top Speed: Both vehicles exhibit similar 0-60 mph times and top speeds, making them competitive in straight-line performance.

Quarter Mile Time: The Probe GT gains an edge in the quarter-mile time due to its higher horsepower and torque figures.

Lap Times and Handling: The MR-S is known for its agile and responsive handling, making it a favorite on twisty tracks. The Probe GT, though not as nimble, still offers commendable cornering abilities.

Powertrain Configuration and Transmission Type: The MR-S features a lightweight inline-4 engine, while the Probe GT boasts a V6. Both models offer manual transmissions, allowing drivers to engage more in the driving experience.


Power to Weight Ratio and RPM:

Toyota MR-S: The MR-S has a lower power-to-weight ratio due to its relatively lower horsepower. The engine can rev up to around 7,800 RPM, showcasing its high-revving nature.

Ford Probe GT: The Probe GT's higher horsepower gives it a slightly better power-to-weight ratio. The engine typically revs up to around 6,500 RPM.


When it comes to the performance numbers, the Toyota MR-S and the Ford Probe GT are closely matched in terms of acceleration and top speed. The MR-S offers a lightweight and nimble experience, ideal for twisty roads, while the Probe GT's V6 powerplant gives it an advantage in straight-line performance. Drivers seeking a more engaging and raw driving experience might lean toward the MR-S, while those craving a bit more muscle could find satisfaction in the Probe GT's V6 power. Ultimately, the choice between these two iconic sports cars boils down to personal preference and driving priorities.





Tuning Ability

Car enthusiasts around the world share a common desire to enhance their vehicles' performance, and the Toyota MR-S and Ford Probe GT offer enticing platforms for tuning and modification. Let's explore the tuning potential of these two iconic cars, focusing on specific upgrades for intake, exhaust, forced induction, fuel management, and ignition options.


Toyota MR-S Tuning Potential:


Intake Upgrades:

Aftermarket cold air intakes like AEM or K&N can enhance airflow, increasing horsepower and throttle response.

Short ram intakes provide a more direct path for air, improving mid-range power.


Exhaust Enhancements:

Performance exhaust headers, such as those from DC Sports or Megan Racing, can boost exhaust flow and power.

Cat-back exhaust systems like Borla or Invidia offer improved sound and potentially higher horsepower gains.


Forced Induction Possibilities:

Supercharger kits from companies like HKS or KraftWerks can provide substantial power gains and maintain drivability.

Turbocharger setups from brands like Garrett or BorgWarner offer higher potential horsepower increases.


Fuel Management Solutions:

Standalone engine management systems like AEM Infinity or Haltech allow precise tuning of fuel and ignition maps.

Piggyback systems such as the Apexi SAFC or Hondata can fine-tune fuel delivery without a complete overhaul.


Ignition Enhancement:

Upgraded ignition coils from brands like MSD or Ignition Projects can improve combustion efficiency.

Performance spark plugs from NGK or Denso can aid in better combustion and smoother power delivery.



Ford Probe GT Tuning Potential:

Intake Upgrades:

Cold air intake kits from brands like AEM or Spectre can enhance air intake and power output.

Short ram intakes offer a balance between improved airflow and throttle response.


Exhaust Enhancements:

Headers from companies like Pacesetter or OBX Racing can optimize exhaust flow and performance.

Cat-back exhaust systems from MagnaFlow or Flowmaster provide sound improvement and power gains.


Forced Induction Possibilities:

Supercharger kits from Vortech or ProCharger offer substantial power increases without excessive complexity.

Turbocharger setups from Precision Turbo or Garrett provide significant potential for high horsepower gains.


Fuel Management Solutions:

Standalone engine management systems like Megasquirt or SCT can be used to finely tune fuel and ignition maps.

Piggyback systems like the Apexi SAFC or DiabloSport can optimize fuel delivery while retaining some factory control.


Ignition Enhancement:

High-performance ignition coils from Accel or MSD can lead to improved ignition efficiency.

Upgraded spark plugs from NGK or Bosch can help optimize combustion and power delivery.


Comparative Analysis:

Engine Architecture: The MR-S's inline-4 engine might make it easier to install forced induction, while the Probe GT's V6 has more inherent power potential.

Fuel Management: Standalone systems provide more control but require more expertise. Piggyback systems are user-friendly but might have limitations.

Exhaust and Intake Upgrades: Both cars benefit from improved airflow, enhancing overall engine performance.

Forced Induction: The MR-S's lighter weight could lead to more dramatic power-to-weight gains from forced induction.

Ignition Upgrades: Enhanced ignition components can benefit both vehicles, ensuring efficient combustion.



Tuning potential varies based on the vehicles' inherent characteristics, but both the Toyota MR-S and Ford Probe GT offer exciting opportunities for enthusiasts to extract more power and performance. Whether through intake, exhaust, forced induction, fuel management, or ignition upgrades, both vehicles can be transformed into exhilarating machines on the road or track. Ultimately, the choice between the two depends on your preferences, budget, and the kind of driving experience you're seeking.





Suspension

The suspension system of a car plays a vital role in defining its handling characteristics, comfort, and overall driving experience. The Toyota MR-S and Ford Probe GT are two distinct vehicles that offer unique suspension setups. Let's delve into the differences in their suspension systems, highlighting their strengths and weaknesses.


Toyota MR-S Suspension:

Double-Wishbone Design: The MR-S employs a double-wishbone suspension setup at all corners, known for its superior handling capabilities and precise control.

Balance and Responsiveness: The MR-S's suspension design contributes to its balanced weight distribution and responsive handling, making it agile and enjoyable to drive on twisty roads.

Low Center of Gravity: The mid-engine layout of the MR-S enhances its center of gravity, resulting in reduced body roll and improved stability during cornering.

Sporty Tuning: Toyota tuned the MR-S's suspension to deliver a sporty and engaging driving experience, focusing on driver involvement and road feedback.

Ride Comfort Trade-off: While the MR-S provides excellent handling, its sporty suspension setup might sacrifice some comfort, especially on rough roads.


Ford Probe GT Suspension:

MacPherson Strut Front, Multi-Link Rear: The Probe GT features a front MacPherson strut and a multi-link rear suspension setup, aimed at delivering a blend of ride comfort and handling.

Balanced Ride Quality: The multi-link rear suspension design helps balance comfort and cornering abilities, making the Probe GT suitable for daily driving and spirited outings.

Smoother Ride: The Probe GT's suspension is generally more compliant over various road surfaces, providing a smoother ride compared to the MR-S.

Cruising Comfort: While the Probe GT can handle corners reasonably well, its suspension setup prioritizes cruising comfort and stability over aggressive handling.


Comparative Analysis:

Handling vs. Comfort: The Toyota MR-S's double-wishbone suspension focuses on responsive handling and driver engagement, making it a preferred choice for enthusiasts seeking sporty performance.

Balanced Performance: The Ford Probe GT's suspension strikes a balance between comfort and handling, making it suitable for drivers who want a versatile and comfortable daily driver.

Agility vs. Stability: The MR-S's suspension design offers higher levels of agility and nimbleness, particularly suited for winding roads and spirited driving.

Daily Drivability: The Probe GT's suspension setup is more forgiving on daily commutes and longer journeys, ensuring comfort for both driver and passengers.

Road Conditions: The MR-S's sporty suspension might feel firmer on rough roads, while the Probe GT's setup can better absorb imperfections without sacrificing stability.


The choice between the Toyota MR-S and Ford Probe GT's suspension systems comes down to individual preferences and driving priorities. If you prioritize sharp handling, responsiveness, and a more aggressive driving experience, the MR-S's double-wishbone setup is the way to go. On the other hand, if you desire a more balanced and comfortable ride for daily driving with occasional bursts of spirited fun, the Probe GT's suspension offers the right compromise. Ultimately, understanding your driving style and preferences will guide you in making the right choice between these two distinct suspension setups.





Interior

The interior of a car significantly impacts the overall driving experience, comfort, and convenience. The Toyota MR-S and Ford Probe GT are two distinct vehicles with unique interior designs. In this article, we will explore and critique the differences in the interior features, layout, and quality of these two models.


Toyota MR-S Interior:

Driver-Centric: The MR-S's interior design focuses on placing controls and displays within easy reach of the driver, enhancing the overall driving experience.

Simple and Functional: The interior features a straightforward layout, minimizing distractions and promoting a seamless driving environment.

Minimalist Aesthetics: The MR-S's cabin boasts a minimalist design, with clean lines and a lack of excessive clutter, contributing to a sleek and modern ambiance.

Cockpit-Inspired: The MR-S's interior resembles a cockpit, with the driver-oriented center console and controls emphasizing a sporty feel.

Compact Cabin: The MR-S offers a snug cabin, creating an intimate and connected experience between the driver, the car, and the road.


Ford Probe GT Interior:

Spacious Comfort: The Probe GT's interior provides more cabin space, offering a comfortable and roomier environment for both driver and passengers.

Ergonomic Layout: Controls and displays in the Probe GT's interior are designed to be easily accessible, contributing to a user-friendly experience.

Aesthetic Accents: The interior of the Probe GT features sporty accents and design elements that highlight its performance-oriented character.

Functional Features: The Probe GT offers features like power-adjustable seats, power windows, and additional convenience features that enhance everyday usability.

Driver-Focused Setup: While not as cockpit-oriented as the MR-S, the Probe GT's interior still aims to keep the driver at the center of attention.


Comparative Analysis:

Driving Experience: The MR-S's driver-centric layout creates a more immersive and focused driving experience, ideal for those who prioritize the thrill of driving.

Cabin Space: The Probe GT's roomier interior makes it a more comfortable option for longer journeys and accommodating passengers.

Aesthetics: The MR-S's minimalist design exudes modernity, while the Probe GT's sporty accents provide a more aggressive and performance-oriented vibe.

Usability: The Probe GT's additional features and cabin space make it a more practical choice for daily driving and longer trips.

Personal Preference: The choice between the MR-S and Probe GT's interiors ultimately depends on whether you value a more intimate and focused driving experience (MR-S) or a more spacious and versatile cabin (Probe GT).


Both the Toyota MR-S and Ford Probe GT offer unique interior experiences catering to different preferences and priorities. The MR-S excels in providing a driver-focused environment with a minimalist design, perfect for enthusiasts seeking a sporty and immersive driving experience. On the other hand, the Probe GT's interior offers more cabin space, comfort, and additional features, making it a practical choice for those who value everyday usability and passenger comfort. Your choice between these two models will depend on your desired driving dynamics, interior aesthetics, and usability requirements.
