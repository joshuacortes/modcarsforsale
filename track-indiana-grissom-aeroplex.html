Grissom Aeroplex: Where Aviation Takes Flight and Innovation Soars Located in the heart of Indiana, the Grissom Aeroplex stands as a testament to the human spirit of exploration and innovation. Formerly known as Grissom Air Force Base, this sprawling facility has transformed into a hub for aerospace activities that span from aviation history to cutting-edge research. With a rich legacy, diverse events, and a commitment to advancing the field of aerospace, Grissom Aeroplex has solidified its place as a vital center of aerospace excellence. A Legacy of Aviation Excellence Named after Lieutenant Colonel Virgil I. "Gus" Grissom, one of NASA's original Mercury Seven astronauts, the facility has a storied history that dates back to its establishment in 1942 as a military airfield during World War II. It subsequently played crucial roles during the Cold War and beyond, serving as a strategic Air Force installation. With the end of the Cold War, the base's mission shifted, leading to its transformation into a site dedicated to fostering aerospace innovation and exploration. Historical Exhibits: Grissom Aeroplex pays tribute to its military heritage with exhibits that showcase the history of aviation and the contributions of the Air Force personnel who served there. Aerospace Industry Events: The aeroplex frequently hosts industry conferences, seminars, and workshops that bring together aerospace professionals, researchers, and innovators. Public Outreach: Grissom Aeroplex is committed to engaging with the community and inspiring the next generation of aerospace enthusiasts. Open houses, STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics) programs, and educational tours provide young minds with insight into the world of aerospace.
