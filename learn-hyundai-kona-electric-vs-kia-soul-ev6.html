Hyundai Kona Electric vs. Kia Soul EV6



Stock Motor

Hyundai Kona Electric:

0-60 Acceleration: The Hyundai Kona Electric accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in approximately 6.4 seconds, providing brisk acceleration for a compact electric SUV.

Torque: The Kona Electric boasts impressive torque, offering responsive and smooth acceleration.

Horsepower: The Kona Electric's electric motor delivers a competitive horsepower output, ensuring lively performance during daily driving.

Top Speed: The Kona Electric has a top speed of around 104 mph (167 km/h), suitable for highway driving.

Quarter Mile Time: The Kona Electric completes the quarter-mile run in approximately 14.9 seconds, demonstrating its quick acceleration capabilities.

Lap Times: The Kona Electric is not primarily designed for track racing, but it delivers agile handling and precise cornering during everyday driving.

Powertrain Configuration: The Hyundai Kona Electric is equipped with a front-wheel-drive powertrain, with the electric motor powering the front wheels.

Transmission Type: The Kona Electric features a single-speed transmission, characteristic of electric vehicles, ensuring smooth and seamless acceleration.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: With a relatively lightweight design and sufficient power output, the Kona Electric achieves a favorable power-to-weight ratio.

RPM (Shaft Speed): The RPM (shaft speed) of the Kona Electric's electric motor can reach several thousand revolutions per minute, delivering efficient power delivery and responsiveness.


Kia Soul EV6:

0-60 Acceleration: The Kia Soul EV6 accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in approximately 5.2 seconds, showcasing impressive acceleration for a compact electric crossover.

Torque: The Soul EV6 generates substantial torque, offering quick off-the-line performance and effortless overtaking.

Horsepower: The Soul EV6's electric motor delivers a commendable horsepower output, ensuring spirited acceleration and highway cruising.

Top Speed: The Soul EV6 has an electronically limited top speed, ensuring safe and controlled driving at high speeds.

Quarter Mile Time: The Soul EV6 completes the quarter-mile run in approximately 13.9 seconds, making it one of the quicker performers in its class.

Lap Times: While not intended for track use, the Soul EV6's responsive handling and smooth suspension contribute to enjoyable driving experiences.

Powertrain Configuration: The Kia Soul EV6 features an all-wheel-drive powertrain, with electric motors powering both front and rear wheels.

Transmission Type: Like the Kona Electric, the Soul EV6 utilizes a single-speed transmission for seamless and efficient acceleration.

Power-to-Weight Ratio: With a well-balanced design and ample power output, the Soul EV6 achieves a favorable power-to-weight ratio, contributing to its spirited performance.

RPM (Shaft Speed): The RPM (shaft speed) of the Soul EV6's electric motor operates at high revolutions per minute, ensuring smooth and responsive power delivery.


Comparison:

The Kia Soul EV6 boasts quicker acceleration, achieving 0-60 mph in approximately 5.2 seconds, compared to the Hyundai Kona Electric's 6.4 seconds.

Both vehicles deliver impressive torque and horsepower outputs, providing spirited performance during daily driving and highway cruising.

The Soul EV6's all-wheel-drive configuration offers enhanced traction and stability, making it a favorable option for drivers seeking added confidence on various road surfaces.

The Kona Electric's front-wheel-drive setup emphasizes efficiency and agile handling, making it well-suited for urban driving and maneuverability.

The Soul EV6's slightly faster quarter-mile time and higher top speed contribute to its performance-oriented nature.

The Kona Electric delivers a competitive power-to-weight ratio, ensuring sufficient power for its compact SUV body style.

The Soul EV6's all-wheel-drive setup and spirited acceleration contribute to its favorable power-to-weight ratio, highlighting its performance-oriented design.





Suspension

Suspension of Hyundai Kona Electric:

MacPherson Strut Front Suspension:

The Kona Electric features a MacPherson strut front suspension, a common choice for compact vehicles.

This design provides a good balance between ride comfort and responsive handling, making it well-suited for city driving and commuting.


Multi-Link Rear Suspension:

At the rear, the Kona Electric is equipped with a multi-link suspension, enhancing stability during cornering and ensuring a smooth ride over uneven surfaces.


Urban Ride Comfort:

The Kona Electric's suspension is tuned to prioritize urban ride comfort, absorbing minor road imperfections and providing a pleasant driving experience on city streets.


Nimble Handling:

The combination of MacPherson strut front suspension and multi-link rear suspension contributes to nimble handling, allowing for easy maneuverability in tight spaces.


Limited Ground Clearance:

The Kona Electric's suspension provides adequate ground clearance for urban driving, but it may be less suitable for off-road adventures or rough terrains.


Suspension of Kia Soul EV6:

MacPherson Strut Front Suspension:

Like the Kona Electric, the Soul EV6 is equipped with a MacPherson strut front suspension, providing a balance of comfort and handling.


Multi-Link Rear Suspension:

The Soul EV6 also features a multi-link rear suspension, enhancing stability and control during cornering and ensuring a comfortable ride.


Comfort-Oriented Ride:

The Soul EV6's suspension is tuned to deliver a comfortable and smooth ride, making it an ideal choice for longer trips and highway driving.

Slightly Higher Ground Clearance:

Compared to the Kona Electric, the Soul EV6 offers slightly higher ground clearance, making it more suitable for light off-road driving and rougher road conditions.


Sports Suspension Option (GT-Line):

The Soul EV6 GT-Line trim offers an available sports suspension, enhancing agility and responsiveness for those seeking a more spirited driving experience.



Comparison:

Both the Hyundai Kona Electric and Kia Soul EV6 feature MacPherson strut front suspensions, ensuring a balanced combination of ride comfort and handling.

The multi-link rear suspension is present in both models, enhancing stability and control during cornering and ensuring a smooth and comfortable ride for passengers.

The Kona Electric's suspension is tuned to prioritize urban ride comfort and nimble handling, making it well-suited for city driving and tight spaces.

The Soul EV6's suspension is tailored for comfort, offering a smooth and pleasant ride for longer trips and highway cruising.

The Soul EV6's slightly higher ground clearance provides added versatility for light off-road driving and rougher terrains compared to the Kona Electric.

The Soul EV6's GT-Line trim offers an available sports suspension for those seeking a more dynamic and agile driving experience.





Interior

Interior of Hyundai Kona Electric:

Compact Cabin Design:

The Kona Electric's interior features a compact cabin design, making it ideal for city driving and easy maneuverability in tight spaces.


Ergonomic and User-Friendly Layout:

The Kona Electric's interior boasts an ergonomic layout with well-placed controls and buttons for easy accessibility.

The intuitive infotainment system and instrument cluster provide a user-friendly driving experience.


Quality Materials:

Hyundai has utilized high-quality materials in the Kona Electric's interior, creating a refined and comfortable ambiance for occupants.


Standard Seating for Five:

The Kona Electric can comfortably accommodate five passengers, making it a practical choice for families and friends.


Cargo Space:

The Kona Electric offers a reasonable cargo space of up to 45.8 cubic feet with the rear seats folded, making it versatile for everyday hauling needs.


Interior Features:

The Kona Electric comes equipped with modern amenities, including a touchscreen infotainment system, smartphone integration, and available heated seats.

Interior of Kia Soul EV6:


Spacious and Versatile Interior:

The Soul EV6's interior design emphasizes spaciousness, making it a great choice for families and those seeking ample legroom and headroom.


Premium Touches:

The Soul EV6 boasts premium touches, such as soft-touch materials, available leather-trimmed seats, and ambient lighting, creating an upscale feel.

Seating Options:

The Soul EV6 offers available rear captain's chairs, reducing seating capacity to four but enhancing passenger comfort.

The standard seating option for five remains available for those seeking maximum passenger capacity.


Cargo Capacity:

The Soul EV6 offers a generous cargo space of up to 62.8 cubic feet with the rear seats folded, providing ample room for larger cargo items.

Cutting-Edge Technology:

The Soul EV6 is equipped with advanced technology, including a 12-inch infotainment touchscreen, available augmented reality heads-up display, and wireless smartphone charging.

Comfort and Convenience Features:

The Soul EV6 features available ventilated front seats, panoramic sunroof, and dual-zone automatic climate control, adding to passenger comfort during drives.


Comparison:

The Hyundai Kona Electric's interior offers a compact and practical design, suitable for urban driving and daily commuting.

The Kia Soul EV6's interior boasts a more spacious and versatile layout, appealing to families and those seeking ample space for passengers and cargo.

The Kona Electric focuses on ergonomic controls and user-friendly technology, ensuring convenience during city drives.

The Soul EV6 prioritizes premium touches and advanced technology, creating a more luxurious and high-end interior experience.

Both models offer modern infotainment systems, smartphone integration, and available heated seats for added comfort and convenience.

The Soul EV6's available rear captain's chairs provide a more luxurious and spacious experience for rear-seat passengers, making it ideal for longer trips.

The Kona Electric's cargo space is practical for everyday use, while the Soul EV6's larger cargo capacity makes it better suited for hauling larger items and family trips.
