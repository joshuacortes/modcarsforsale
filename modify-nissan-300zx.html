Nissan 300ZX

The Nissan 300zx was produced throughout the course of two generations. The 300ZX was offered for sale on the local Japanese market under the brand name Fairlady Z, just like all other Z variations.

The Nissan S30, the first Z automobile, which was marketed as the 240Z in the United States, established the number convention with which the 300ZX moniker was used. It was sold in Japan from 1983 to 2000 and in the United States from 1984 to 1996. The "X" prefix was carried over from the 280ZX, the car's predecessor, to denote the inclusion of more luxurious and comfortable amenities. The first-generation 300ZX, also known as the Z31 model, was manufactured from 1983 to 1989 and enjoyed great commercial success, becoming Nissan's most popular Z-car.

The second generation Z31 was pushed upwards in order to increase its competitiveness in the sports car market. It was updated to be speedier and have more cutting-edge technology, but it cost more than its predecessor and saw a price increase with each passing model year. Due to declining sales each year, which was a trend in the high-end sports car market at the time, Nissan decided to stop selling new Nissan Z-Cars in the US after the 1996 model year. However, the vehicle was still offered in Japan until 2001 in limited quantities.



Modified Nissan 300ZX

Performance Upgrades

The 300ZX is renowned for its powerful V6 engine, but there's always room for improvement. Performance upgrades can take your Z to the next level, whether you're looking for more power or improved handling. Some popular modifications include:

Turbocharging: Many 300ZX models come with a turbocharged engine, but you can further boost power by upgrading to larger turbos or improving the existing setup.

Exhaust Systems: Upgrading your exhaust system can increase horsepower and enhance the engine's growl.

Suspension and Brakes: Enhanced coilovers, sway bars, and brake kits can greatly improve the 300ZX's handling and stopping power.

Engine Management: Aftermarket ECUs and tuning software can fine-tune your engine for optimal performance.


Aftermarket Support

One of the great advantages of modifying a 300ZX is the extensive aftermarket support. Numerous companies offer performance parts, body kits, and accessories designed specifically for this car. You'll find a wide range of options, whether you're looking for bolt-on upgrades or custom fabrication.


Design and Styling

The 300ZX's timeless design is part of what makes it so beloved. If you want to enhance the aesthetic appeal of your Z, there are various modifications to consider:

Body Kits: Aggressive body kits and spoilers can give your 300ZX a more unique and distinctive look.

Wheels and Tires: Upgrading your wheels and tires can improve both the appearance and performance of your car.

Interior Modifications: Custom upholstery, racing seats, and a modernized dashboard can add a touch of luxury and functionality to your cockpit.


Motorsports Success

The 300ZX has a history of success in motorsports, particularly in the IMSA GTO and 24 Hours of Le Mans races. Many enthusiasts aim to capture some of that spirit with motorsport-inspired modifications:

Aerodynamics: Upgraded aerodynamic components, such as front splitters and rear wings, can improve high-speed stability.

Roll Cages: Safety is paramount when pushing your car to the limit. A roll cage not only enhances safety but also adds to the car's racing aesthetic.


Driving Expression

Ultimately, modifying your 300ZX is about creating a unique driving experience that suits your style and preferences. Whether you prefer the thrill of the drag strip, the precision of autocross, or simply enjoy spirited drives on scenic routes, your modifications should reflect your personal expression.

Tuned Suspension: Adjusting your suspension setup for comfort or performance can drastically change the way your 300ZX handles.

Sound Systems: A high-quality sound system can make your drives more enjoyable and entertaining.

Custom Paint Jobs: Express your individuality with a unique paint job or vinyl wrap that sets your Z apart from the crowd.


Modifying a Nissan 300ZX is an exciting journey that allows you to personalize your ride to match your taste and driving style. With countless performance upgrades, a strong aftermarket community, opportunities to enhance the design, inspiration from its motorsports success, and endless possibilities for driving expression, the 300ZX remains a classic sports car that keeps enthusiasts coming back for more. Whether you're chasing more power, better handling, or simply a distinctive look, the 300ZX provides a canvas for your automotive dreams.

NISSAN 300ZX TURBO

JWT Twin Turbo Kit

JWT (Jim Wolf Technology) has a well-earned reputation for producing high-quality performance parts, and their Twin Turbo Kit is no exception. This kit is designed to fit both the Z32 300ZX models (1990-1996) and delivers a substantial power increase. The package includes two Garrett GT2560R turbochargers, intercoolers, and all necessary hardware for a complete installation. The JWT kit not only enhances the power of your 300ZX but also ensures reliability and drivability, making it a top choice for enthusiasts.


Greddy 300ZX Twin Turbo Kit

Greddy is another reputable name in the automotive performance industry, and their Twin Turbo Kit is a popular choice among 300ZX owners. The kit comes with two TD06-20G turbos, a cast manifold, intercoolers, and all required piping and hardware. The Greddy kit is known for delivering a nice balance of power and reliability, making it a favorite for those who want to boost their 300ZX without sacrificing drivability.


HKS GT2530 Turbo Kit

HKS is a globally recognized name in the world of performance tuning, and their GT2530 Turbo Kit is a fantastic option for the Nissan 300ZX. This kit features a pair of HKS GT2530 turbos, a stainless-steel manifold, and intercoolers. HKS is known for its precision engineering and commitment to quality, which is evident in the performance gains provided by this kit.


APS Twin Turbo Kit

APS (Air Power Systems) offers a well-crafted Twin Turbo Kit for the Nissan 300ZX. This kit is specifically designed for the Z32 model and features two GT28 turbochargers, intercoolers, and all necessary components for installation. APS is known for their expertise in intercooling and induction, which ensures your 300ZX gets an ample supply of cool, dense air for optimal performance.


CXRacing Twin Turbo Kit

If you're looking for a more budget-friendly option without compromising too much on quality, the CXRacing Twin Turbo Kit is a solid choice. This kit includes two T04E turbochargers, intercoolers, and piping. While not as well-known as some of the other brands on this list, CXRacing offers a cost-effective solution for those who want to add turbo power to their 300ZX without breaking the bank.


Adding a turbo kit to your Nissan 300ZX can transform it from a potent sports car into a true high-performance machine. The options mentioned in this article, including JWT, Greddy, HKS, APS, and CXRacing, are some of the best turbo kits available for the 300ZX, each with its unique strengths and characteristics. When selecting a turbo kit, it's essential to consider your specific goals, budget, and desired level of performance.

Before embarking on the installation process, it's advisable to consult with a professional mechanic or tuner experienced in working with turbocharged setups. Additionally, always ensure that your vehicle's engine, transmission, and other components are capable of handling the increased power to prevent any unwanted issues.

No matter which turbo kit you choose, you're bound to experience a significant boost in horsepower and torque, ultimately making your Nissan 300ZX an even more exhilarating and thrilling driving experience.
