How to sell a car in Wisconsin



Selling a car in Wisconsin involves a series of steps to ensure a smooth and legal transaction. Whether you're upgrading to a new vehicle or simply no longer need your current one, this article will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to sell a car in Wisconsin.


Step 1: Gather the Necessary Documents

Before selling your car, ensure you have the following documents ready:

Vehicle Title: Have the original title with all necessary fields completed, including the seller's signature, buyer's information (if known), and the date of sale.

Bill of Sale: Prepare a bill of sale that includes the buyer and seller's information, vehicle details (make, model, year, VIN), sale price, and date of sale. Both parties should sign and retain a copy.

Odometer Disclosure: Complete the Odometer Disclosure Statement, accurately recording the mileage at the time of sale.

Lien Release (if applicable): If there is an outstanding loan on the vehicle, obtain a lien release from the financing institution to transfer clear ownership.


Step 2: Determine the Vehicle's Value

Research the market value of your car to establish a fair selling price. Consider factors such as the make, model, year, mileage, condition, and any additional features or recent maintenance. Online resources, local dealerships, and classified ads can provide insights into comparable prices in Wisconsin.


Step 3: Advertise the Vehicle

Create an appealing advertisement for your car, including clear photos and a detailed description highlighting its features, condition, and any recent upgrades or maintenance. Utilize online classifieds, local newspapers, and social media platforms to reach potential buyers in Wisconsin.


Step 4: Respond to Inquiries and Arrange Test Drives

Promptly respond to inquiries from potential buyers and provide accurate information about the car. Schedule test drives at safe and convenient locations, ensuring compliance with traffic regulations.


Step 5: Negotiate and Finalize the Sale

When a buyer is interested and ready to purchase, negotiate the sale price and any additional terms. Once both parties agree, proceed with finalizing the sale:

Complete the bill of sale: Fill out the bill of sale with all required details, ensuring both the buyer and seller sign it.

Payment: Determine the method of payment (e.g., cash, certified check, bank transfer), ensuring a secure transaction. It is advisable to conduct the transaction at a bank or financial institution.

Release of Liability: Complete a Notice of Transfer form, available from the Wisconsin Department of Transportation (WisDOT), and submit it to the department within 30 days of the sale. This notifies WisDOT that you are no longer responsible for the vehicle.


Step 6: Transfer Ownership

The buyer assumes responsibility for transferring ownership. Within 30 days of the sale, the buyer must:

Obtain a Wisconsin Title: The buyer must visit a Wisconsin Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) office with the necessary documents, including the completed bill of sale, the original title, and the Odometer Disclosure Statement. They will also need to pay the required fees.

Register the Vehicle: The buyer must register the vehicle with the DMV by providing proof of insurance and paying the applicable registration fees.


Step 7: Cancel Insurance and Notify Relevant Parties

Contact your insurance company to cancel coverage for the sold vehicle. Additionally, inform the Wisconsin DMV about the sale by submitting the Notice of Transfer form mentioned earlier.


Selling a car in Wisconsin involves gathering the necessary documents, determining the vehicle's value, advertising, and finalizing the sale. It is crucial to comply with Wisconsin's specific regulations, including completing the bill of sale, obtaining a Wisconsin title, and submitting the Notice of Transfer form to the Wisconsin DMV. By following this comprehensive guide, you can successfully sell your car in Wisconsin and complete the transaction with confidence.
