Nissan GT-R R35

The Nissan GT-R, also known as the Nissan GT-R in Japan, is a high-performance sports car and grand tourer. A 3,799 cc (3.8 L; 231.8 cu in) 60° DOHC V6 engine with plasma transferred wire arc sprayed cylinder bores, the VR38DETT engine was specifically designed for the Nissan GT-R.

Only five specially trained mechanics known as "Takumi Craftsmens" manufactured the engines by hand in a climate-controlled, dust-proof area at Nissan's Yokohama facility; their names were badged on each GT-R engine. Each engine needed 300 parts and 8.3 hours to build completely; 13 engines were constructed every day during a single shift.
