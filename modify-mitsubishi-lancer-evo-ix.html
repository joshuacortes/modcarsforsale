Mitsubishi Lancer Evo IX

The 2.0 L inline four cylinder engine that powered the Mitsubishi Lancer Evo IX boasts MIVEC technology (variable valve timing), and a new turbocharger design that increases crankshaft power to 291 PS (214 kW; 287 hp) and torque to 392 Nm (289 lb-ft).



Modified Mitsubishi Lancer Evo 9

1. Performance:

The Evo 9 was built for performance from the factory, but there's always room for improvement. Here are some key modifications to boost your Evo's performance:

Engine Upgrades: Start with basic modifications like an upgraded air intake, exhaust system, and an ECU (Engine Control Unit) tune to unlock additional horsepower and torque. Many enthusiasts also opt for larger turbochargers, intercoolers, and high-flow fuel injectors for more significant gains.

Suspension Tuning: Enhance the car's handling and road-holding capabilities with adjustable coilovers, sway bars, and strut braces. A well-tuned suspension can significantly improve cornering and stability.

Braking Systems: Upgraded brake pads and rotors, along with stainless steel braided brake lines, are essential for ensuring your Evo can stop as well as it accelerates.


2. Aftermarket Support:

One of the Evo 9's advantages is its robust aftermarket support. Countless manufacturers offer performance parts and accessories, making it easier to find and install quality upgrades.

Exhaust Systems: Brands like HKS, Invidia, and Borla offer a range of exhaust systems, providing different sound profiles and performance enhancements.

Turbochargers: Companies like Garrett, BorgWarner, and Precision Turbo produce high-quality turbochargers suitable for the Evo 9.

ECU Tuning: Numerous reputable tuners, like Cobb and EcuTek, provide ECU tuning solutions that can be customized to your specific goals and requirements.


3. Design:

The Evo 9 has an aggressive, rally-inspired design that can be further enhanced with cosmetic modifications:

Body Kits: Numerous body kits are available to give your Evo a more aggressive and unique look. Brands like Varis, Voltex, and APR Performance offer high-quality options.

Wheels: A new set of wheels can dramatically change the appearance of your Evo. Companies such as Volk Racing, BBS, and Enkei offer a wide range of styles and sizes.

Carbon Fiber Accessories: Adding carbon fiber components like hoods, spoilers, and mirrors can reduce weight and improve aesthetics.


4. Motorsports Success/History:

The Evo 9 has a rich motorsports history, with numerous accolades in the World Rally Championship and other racing series. Modifying your Evo with a nod to its racing heritage can be a fun and engaging project.

Rally Livery: Consider applying a classic rally livery to your Evo, paying homage to its success in the WRC.

Recaro Seats: Install racing-inspired Recaro seats to provide both comfort and performance during spirited driving.

Roll Cages: For those who plan to hit the track, adding a roll cage enhances safety and stiffness.


5. Driving Experience:

The driving experience is at the core of modifying any sports car. The following enhancements can take your Evo 9 to the next level:

Short-Throw Shifter: Upgrading to a short-throw shifter improves gear changes and makes driving more engaging.

Sound System: A quality sound system can transform your daily commute into a pleasurable experience, especially if you plan to drive your Evo frequently.

Aerodynamics: Adding a front splitter or rear wing can enhance stability and downforce, particularly at high speeds.


Additional Topics:

Maintenance: Regular maintenance is crucial to keeping your modified Evo in top shape. Ensure you follow maintenance schedules and use high-quality fluids and components.

Modifying a Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution 9 is not just about adding horsepower; it's about enhancing the driving experience, celebrating its motorsports history, and creating a car that truly reflects your personality. Whether you're in it for the adrenaline rush, the head-turning design, or the pursuit of motorsports glory, the Evo 9 is a canvas waiting for your personal touch.

Common Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX Modifications:

Engine Tuning and ECU Remapping

One of the first and most common modifications for an Evo 9 is engine tuning and ECU (Engine Control Unit) remapping. The factory ECU often leaves significant power untapped. Tuning and remapping can unleash additional horsepower and torque, optimize fuel delivery, and improve throttle response. These modifications can transform your Evo 9 into an even more formidable performance machine.

Why: ECU remapping and engine tuning enhance power and driveability, providing a noticeable improvement in overall performance without costly engine overhauls.


Turbocharger Upgrades

The Evo 9 comes equipped with a capable turbocharger from the factory, but many enthusiasts opt for upgrades to achieve even more power. Aftermarket turbochargers offer a wider range of powerband options, quicker spooling, and increased boost pressure. These upgrades can significantly boost horsepower and torque output.

Why: Turbocharger upgrades can take your Evo 9 to new performance heights, delivering a thrilling driving experience, especially for track enthusiasts.


Exhaust System

Upgrading the exhaust system is a modification that not only adds a sporty tone to the Evo 9 but also improves performance. Aftermarket exhaust systems typically feature larger-diameter pipes, free-flowing mufflers, and sometimes even catalytic converter deletes, reducing exhaust backpressure and boosting power.

Why: A better-breathing exhaust system not only provides a more aggressive exhaust note but also enhances power, helping the engine breathe more freely.


Suspension Upgrades

To fully harness the Evo 9's power and handling capabilities, it's essential to consider suspension upgrades. Popular modifications include coilover kits, adjustable sway bars, and performance bushings. These upgrades enhance cornering, stability, and overall handling characteristics, making your Evo 9 more responsive in curves and at high speeds.

Why: Suspension upgrades improve handling and overall balance, allowing for greater control, sharper cornering, and enhanced performance on both the street and the track.


Braking Systems

When you're pushing your Evo 9 to the limits, a capable braking system is crucial. Upgrades like performance brake pads, slotted or drilled rotors, and stainless steel brake lines can improve stopping power and reduce brake fade, which is especially important during spirited driving or track days.

Why: Enhanced braking systems are essential for maintaining control and safety, and they reduce the risk of brake fade in high-stress driving situations.


Intake and Intercooler Upgrades

Upgrading the intake system and intercooler can provide a boost in horsepower and torque. Cold air intake kits and larger intercoolers improve airflow and reduce intake temperatures, resulting in more power and a more consistent performance, particularly in high-stress driving conditions.

Why: Intake and intercooler upgrades support better air delivery to the engine and keep the intake air temperature lower, resulting in improved performance and reliability.


The Mitsubishi Lancer Evo 9 is a platform with immense potential for performance enhancements. Whether you're aiming for more power, improved handling, or a combination of both, these common modifications can help you unleash the true potential of your Evo 9. Each of these upgrades serves a distinct purpose, from unlocking hidden engine power to improving handling and safety. Before embarking on a modification journey, it's crucial to set clear goals and budget constraints, and consult with experts who can guide you in making the best choices for your specific needs. With the right modifications, your Evo 9 can become an even more exhilarating and capable driving machine.

Why it's a top choice: BorgWarner EFR turbochargers are praised for their superior build quality, responsiveness, and the ability to support a wide range of power levels.
