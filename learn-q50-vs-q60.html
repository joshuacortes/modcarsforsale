Infiniti Q50 vs Q60



Stock Motor

Infiniti Q50:

The Infiniti Q50 is a luxury sedan designed to provide a balance between comfort and performance. It offers a range of engine options, allowing drivers to tailor their driving experience to their preferences.

One of the main engine options in the Q50 lineup is a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine. Depending on the specific trim level, this engine produces different power outputs. In its base configuration, the engine delivers approximately 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, providing a satisfying level of acceleration and responsiveness.

For those seeking more power, the Q50 Red Sport 400 trim features an upgraded version of the 3.0-liter V6 engine. With enhanced tuning and modifications, it generates an impressive 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. This high-performance variant delivers exhilarating acceleration and a more engaging driving experience.


Infiniti Q60:

The Infiniti Q60 is a sleek and stylish coupe that emphasizes performance and aggressive styling. Like the Q50, the Q60 offers multiple engine options to cater to different driving preferences.

The Q60's standard engine is a 3.0-liter twin-turbocharged V6, similar to the base engine in the Q50. It produces around 300 horsepower and 295 lb-ft of torque, providing a satisfying balance of performance and refinement.

For enthusiasts seeking even greater performance, the Q60 Red Sport 400 takes things up a notch. Equipped with an upgraded version of the 3.0-liter V6 engine, it boasts 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque, delivering an adrenaline-pumping driving experience.


Performance Comparison:

Both the Infiniti Q50 and Q60 offer impressive performance capabilities with their stock motors. The twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 engines provide ample power and torque, ensuring exhilarating acceleration and confident overtaking maneuvers. The Q60 Red Sport 400's enhanced tuning further amplifies the performance, delivering an even more thrilling driving experience.

The performance characteristics of the Q50 and Q60 are further enhanced by their refined chassis and suspension systems. Both models feature rear-wheel-drive or all-wheel-drive configurations, providing excellent handling and stability.




When comparing the stock motors of the Infiniti Q50 and Q60, it's evident that both models offer impressive performance and power. The twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 engines provide exhilarating acceleration, while the Red Sport 400 variants take performance to another level with enhanced tuning.

Whether you prefer the sophisticated and spacious luxury of the Q50 sedan or the sporty and aggressive styling of the Q60 coupe, both models offer a thrilling driving experience. The choice between the two ultimately comes down to personal preference, with the Q50 emphasizing a balance between luxury and performance, while the Q60 leans more towards a sporty and dynamic driving experience.





Tuning Ability

Infiniti Q50:

The Infiniti Q50, a luxury sedan with a sporty demeanor, provides a solid foundation for tuning and modifications. Thanks to its robust engine options and responsive drivetrain, the Q50 can be transformed into a more performance-oriented machine.

One popular avenue for tuning the Q50 is through engine modifications. The twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 engine offers opportunities for power gains through aftermarket upgrades such as ECU tuning, cold air intakes, and upgraded exhaust systems. These modifications can enhance throttle response, increase horsepower, and improve overall engine performance. Some enthusiasts have reported significant power gains by optimizing the engine's air-fuel mixture and boost pressure.

Additionally, suspension upgrades, such as adjustable coilovers, performance sway bars, and upgraded bushings, can enhance the Q50's handling capabilities. Upgrading to larger wheels and high-performance tires can also improve grip and traction, enhancing the overall driving experience.


Infiniti Q60:

The Infiniti Q60, a stylish coupe with a sporty character, also offers tuning potential for enthusiasts seeking additional performance. With its robust engine options and dynamic chassis, the Q60 can be transformed into a thrilling driving machine through aftermarket modifications.

Similar to the Q50, the Q60's twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 engine provides opportunities for power gains through modifications like ECU tuning, cold air intakes, and upgraded exhaust systems. These upgrades can result in increased horsepower, improved torque delivery, and enhanced throttle response. Some enthusiasts have reported significant performance gains by optimizing the engine's boost pressure and fuel delivery.

In addition to engine upgrades, suspension modifications play a crucial role in enhancing the Q60's handling capabilities. Upgraded coilover setups, performance shocks, and sway bars can improve cornering ability, reduce body roll, and provide a more engaging driving experience. Brake upgrades, such as larger calipers and rotors, can enhance stopping power and overall braking performance.


Performance Comparison:

When it comes to tuning ability, both the Infiniti Q50 and Q60 offer similar opportunities for modifications. Their shared twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter V6 engines provide a solid foundation for increased power and performance gains. With aftermarket upgrades such as ECU tuning, intake, and exhaust enhancements, enthusiasts can unlock additional horsepower and torque, creating a more exhilarating driving experience.

Both models also benefit from suspension and handling upgrades, allowing owners to fine-tune the driving dynamics to their preferences. Suspension modifications, brake upgrades, and wheel and tire enhancements further enhance the overall performance and handling characteristics of the Q50 and Q60.


The Infiniti Q50 and Q60 provide ample opportunities for tuning and modifications, allowing enthusiasts to unleash even more power and performance from these already impressive vehicles. With their robust twin-turbocharged V6 engines and responsive drivetrains, both models respond well to aftermarket upgrades, including engine tuning, intake, exhaust, and suspension enhancements.





Suspension

Infiniti Q50:

The Infiniti Q50, a luxury sports sedan, is engineered to strike a balance between comfort and performance. Its suspension system is designed to provide stability, agility, and a smooth ride.

The Q50 features a front double-wishbone suspension and a rear multi-link suspension setup. This configuration allows for independent movement of each wheel, enhancing stability during cornering and ensuring optimal tire contact with the road surface. The double-wishbone front suspension offers precise steering response and helps reduce body roll, contributing to a sporty and engaging driving experience.

Infiniti equips the Q50 with various advanced technologies to further enhance its suspension performance. The available Dynamic Digital Suspension (DDS) system continuously adjusts damping force in real-time, optimizing ride comfort and handling based on road conditions and driver inputs. This adaptive suspension system provides a customizable driving experience, allowing drivers to switch between different modes, including Sport and Comfort.


Infiniti Q60:

The Infiniti Q60, a stylish and performance-oriented coupe, is engineered to provide dynamic handling and exhilarating driving characteristics. Its suspension system is designed to strike a balance between agility and comfort, enhancing its sporty nature.

Similar to the Q50, the Q60 features a front double-wishbone suspension and a rear multi-link suspension setup. This configuration ensures precise handling, stability, and a planted feel during high-speed maneuvers. The double-wishbone front suspension delivers excellent suspension control and improved steering response, contributing to the Q60's athletic driving dynamics.

Infiniti equips the Q60 with its available Dynamic Digital Suspension (DDS) system, similar to the Q50. This adaptive suspension technology continuously adjusts damping force to provide optimal ride comfort and handling. With selectable driving modes, such as Sport and Comfort, drivers can tailor the suspension characteristics to their preferences, fine-tuning the driving experience to suit different road conditions and driving styles.


Performance Comparison:

Both the Infiniti Q50 and Q60 showcase impressive suspension systems designed to enhance their performance-oriented nature. The front double-wishbone and rear multi-link setups in both models offer excellent stability, precise handling, and a planted feel on the road.

While the suspension setups are similar between the Q50 and Q60, the driving experience may differ slightly due to the vehicles' weight distribution and overall design. The Q60's coupe configuration may contribute to a slightly more dynamic and agile feel, perfect for spirited driving on winding roads. However, the Q50's sedan configuration may offer a slightly more comfortable and refined ride, ideal for long-distance cruising.


When comparing the suspension systems of the Infiniti Q50 and Q60, it's evident that both models offer exceptional handling characteristics and a balanced ride. The double-wishbone front and multi-link rear suspension setups contribute to precise steering response, reduced body roll, and stability during spirited driving.

Infiniti's Dynamic Digital Suspension (DDS) technology, available in both models, further enhances the driving experience by continuously adapting to changing road conditions and driver inputs. This adaptive suspension system allows drivers to customize the ride comfort and handling characteristics to their preferences.





Interior

Infiniti Q50:

The Infiniti Q50's interior showcases a blend of modern design elements and premium materials, creating a sophisticated and driver-centric cabin.

The Q50's cockpit features an ergonomic layout, ensuring essential controls and infotainment functions are easily accessible. The driver-oriented design places emphasis on the driver's comfort and convenience, making long drives a pleasure. High-quality materials, such as soft-touch surfaces, refined leather upholstery, and tasteful trim accents, add a touch of luxury to the cabin.

Infiniti has equipped the Q50 with advanced technology features that enhance the driving experience. A dual-screen infotainment system provides access to navigation, audio controls, and various vehicle settings. Available amenities like a premium sound system, dual-zone automatic climate control, and power-adjustable seats further enhance the comfort and convenience for both the driver and passengers.

Seating comfort in the Q50 is excellent, with supportive and well-cushioned seats that ensure long-lasting comfort on extended journeys. The rear seats provide ample legroom and headroom, making them suitable for passengers of various sizes.


Infiniti Q60:

The Infiniti Q60's interior exudes a combination of sportiness and luxury, reflecting its coupe character.

Similar to the Q50, the Q60 features a driver-focused cockpit with a modern and elegant design. The use of high-quality materials, including premium leather upholstery, refined trim accents, and soft-touch surfaces, adds a sense of opulence to the cabin. The interior of the Q60 showcases attention to detail, providing a premium and inviting environment.

The Q60's infotainment system offers a user-friendly interface, providing access to various features and controls. Modern amenities, such as a high-quality sound system, available smartphone integration, and advanced driver-assistance systems, contribute to a more convenient and enjoyable driving experience.

While the Q60's coupe styling provides a slightly more intimate cabin space, it still offers comfortable seating for both the driver and passengers. The front seats provide excellent support and bolstering, ensuring a secure and comfortable ride during spirited driving. The rear seats, though slightly more compact due to the coupe design, still offer reasonable comfort for shorter trips or additional passengers.


Performance Comparison:

Both the Infiniti Q50 and Q60 offer luxurious and well-appointed interiors that prioritize driver comfort and convenience. While the Q50's sedan configuration provides a more spacious cabin suitable for longer journeys and family outings, the Q60's coupe design adds a touch of sportiness and intimacy to the driving experience.

The choice between the Q50 and Q60 ultimately comes down to personal preference and the desired driving experience. The Q50's interior focuses on comfort and practicality, making it a suitable choice for those seeking a refined and versatile luxury sedan. On the other hand, the Q60's interior combines luxury with a sporty ambiance, appealing to enthusiasts who desire a more dynamic and engaging driving experience.


The interiors of the Infiniti Q50 and Q60 showcase the brand's commitment to luxury, craftsmanship, and modern design. Both models offer driver-focused cabins with high-quality materials, advanced technology features, and comfortable seating for a luxurious and enjoyable driving experience.
