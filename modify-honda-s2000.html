Honda S2000

From 1999 through 2009, Honda, a Japanese automaker, produced the open-top sports car known as the S2000. To commemorate the company's 50th anniversary, the production version of the SSM concept car was unveiled on April 15, 1999, at the Tokyo Motor Show. In keeping with the S500, S600, and S800 roadsters of the 1960s, the S2000 is called after its two-liter engine displacement.

Over the course of the vehicle's manufacturing, numerous modifications were made, including adjustments to the interior, bodywork, suspension, engine, and gearbox.

Since then, the price of valued models in good condition of the S2000 has significantly increased, making it a legitimate example of a modern vintage automobile. Special edition productions, like the CR (Club Racer) in the USA market, are the most sought-after models in the JDM community and the automotive industry as a whole. Standard AP1 and AP2 vehicles have also turned into collectibles.


Modified Honda S2000

1. Performance Enhancements:

a. Engine Upgrades: The heart of the S2000 is its F20C or F22C engine, known for its high-revving capabilities. Enthusiasts often explore options like forced induction (turbocharging or supercharging), aftermarket camshafts, and intake/exhaust improvements to extract more power. Engine management systems like Hondata are also popular for fine-tuning.

b. Suspension Tuning: The S2000's superb handling can be further refined with suspension upgrades. Coilover kits, adjustable sway bars, and stiffer bushings can enhance cornering prowess. Proper alignment is crucial for maximizing grip and balance.

c. Braking System: Upgrading the braking system with performance rotors, calipers, and high-friction pads is vital for improved stopping power, especially during spirited driving.


2. Aftermarket Support:

The S2000 enjoys strong aftermarket support, with numerous manufacturers offering performance parts and accessories. Well-established companies like HKS, Spoon Sports, and Mugen provide a wide range of options for engine, suspension, and exterior modifications. Enthusiast communities and forums are valuable resources for discovering new products and gathering insights on effective modifications.

3. Design Modifications:

a. Aerodynamics: Many S2000 owners opt for front splitters, rear wings, and diffusers to improve aerodynamics and downforce. These modifications can enhance stability at higher speeds.

b. Body Kits: Upgrading the S2000's aesthetics is a popular choice. Body kits, like those offered by Veilside, C-West, and Shine Auto, can transform the car's appearance while maintaining its sporty identity.

c. Wheels and Tires: Changing the wheels and tires can significantly impact both the vehicle's performance and aesthetics. Lightweight alloy wheels and performance-oriented tires can enhance grip and handling.


4. Motorsports Success:

The S2000 has an impressive motorsports history, particularly in the world of road racing and autocross. The car's agility, balance, and power-to-weight ratio make it a formidable competitor. The S2000 has seen success in various racing series, including the Sports Car Club of America (SCCA) competitions. It has earned a reputation for being a reliable and competitive platform, thanks in part to its modifications.


5. Driving Experience:

Modifying a Honda S2000 can create a transformative driving experience. With the right upgrades, the car becomes sharper, more responsive, and a pure joy to pilot. The enhanced exhaust note, improved acceleration, and precise handling result in a thrilling experience every time you hit the road. Whether it's on the track or winding backroads, the modified S2000 offers an exhilarating driving adventure.


Modifying a Honda S2000 is a passionate endeavor that can take your ownership experience to a new level. The car's exceptional performance potential, robust aftermarket support, and history of motorsports success make it an ideal platform for customization. Whether you seek to boost power, refine handling, or simply enhance the car's aesthetics, the S2000 offers countless opportunities for personalization and a truly exhilarating driving experience. As you embark on your journey of S2000 modification, remember to consult with experts, explore the vibrant enthusiast community, and enjoy every moment behind the wheel of your modified masterpiece.





COMMON HONDA S2000 MODIFICATIONS:

1. Forced Induction:

Turbocharging and Supercharging: One of the most sought-after modifications for the S2000 is adding forced induction. Turbochargers and superchargers can significantly boost horsepower and torque, transforming the already quick roadster into a high-performance machine. Kits from reputable manufacturers like HKS and Kraftwerks are often used to ensure reliability and power gains.


2. Performance Exhaust System:

Upgrading the exhaust system is a common modification that not only enhances engine sound but also improves performance. Aftermarket exhaust systems, like those from Invidia, Borla, and Greddy, offer freer-flowing designs, reduced backpressure, and a more aggressive exhaust note, creating a visceral driving experience.


3. Suspension Enhancements:

Coilover Kits: Coilover suspension systems, like those from Tein, KW Suspensions, and Bilstein, provide adjustable ride height, damping, and rebound control, allowing for precise tuning to suit your driving style. These kits offer improved cornering capabilities and a sportier feel.

Sway Bars and Strut Tower Braces: Upgrading the sway bars and adding strut tower braces can reduce body roll and improve chassis rigidity, resulting in enhanced stability and sharper cornering. Brands such as Eibach and Cusco offer high-quality components for S2000 suspension upgrades.


4. Wheels and Tires:

Changing the wheels and tires can significantly impact the S2000's performance and aesthetics. Lightweight alloy wheels improve acceleration, handling, and fuel efficiency. Tire upgrades to high-performance rubber can enhance grip and overall handling. Popular wheel brands include Enkei, Volk Racing, and Advan.


5. Engine Management Systems:

Aftermarket engine management systems, such as Hondata or AEM, provide precise control over engine parameters, allowing for custom tuning and optimization. These systems are often used to accommodate forced induction setups and maximize engine performance.


6. Body Modifications:

a. Aerodynamics: Many S2000 owners opt for front splitters, rear wings, and diffusers to improve aerodynamics and enhance stability at higher speeds.

b. Body Kits: Aftermarket body kits from companies like Veilside, Wings West, and C-West can transform the S2000's appearance, providing aggressive lines and distinctive designs that make the car stand out.


7. Braking Upgrades:

Enhancing the braking system with performance rotors, calipers, and high-friction pads is crucial for improved stopping power, particularly for drivers who enjoy spirited driving and track days.


8. Interior Customization:

S2000 enthusiasts often upgrade the interior with aftermarket seats, steering wheels, shift knobs, and gauges to enhance the driving experience and add a personalized touch to the cabin.


9. Lighting and Audio:

Upgrading lighting components with HID or LED bulbs and adding a high-quality audio system are popular modifications to enhance both safety and in-cabin enjoyment.


Modifying a Honda S2000 is a passionate pursuit that allows owners to unleash the car's full potential and create a personalized driving experience. Whether you're focused on increasing power, improving handling, enhancing aesthetics, or all of the above, the S2000 offers a wide range of options for customization. With careful research and consideration, you can tailor your S2000 to match your driving style and preferences, making it a thrilling and unique reflection of your automotive passion.
