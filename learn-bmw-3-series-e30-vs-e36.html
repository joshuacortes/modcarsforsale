BMW 3 Series (E30) vs. BMW 3 Series (E36)



Stock Motor

The BMW 3 Series has long been a symbol of driving pleasure and performance in the luxury compact sedan segment. Over the years, the 3 Series has evolved, and two iconic generations that stand out are the E30 and the E36. In this article, we will compare the stock motors of these two revered models, exploring their power outputs, technological advancements, and driving characteristics. Let's delve into the evolution of power in the BMW 3 Series.


BMW 3 Series (E30):

The E30 generation, produced from 1982 to 1994, established the 3 Series as a benchmark for sporty driving dynamics and engaging performance. The stock motors available for the E30 offered a range of power options suited to different driving preferences.


Petrol Engines:


1.6L, 101 horsepower (hp) four-cylinder engine: This entry-level engine provided a balanced combination of performance and fuel efficiency, making it an ideal choice for daily driving and urban commutes.
2.0L, 115 hp four-cylinder engine: Offering more power and improved acceleration, this engine enhanced the E30's sporty nature, delivering a thrilling driving experience.
2.3L, 170 hp six-cylinder engine: Positioned as the higher-performance option, this engine elevated the E30's power and responsiveness, providing a more exhilarating ride for enthusiasts.


BMW 3 Series (E36):

The E36 generation, introduced in 1990 and produced until 1999, built upon the success of its predecessor, showcasing advancements in technology and performance. The stock motors in the E36 offered increased power and refined driving dynamics compared to the E30.


Petrol Engines:


1.6L, 101 hp four-cylinder engine: Similar to the E30, this engine provided a balanced performance, offering adequate power for everyday driving situations.
2.0L, 148 hp six-cylinder engine: This engine brought a significant power increase over the E30's 2.0L engine, enhancing acceleration and overall performance. It delivered a more engaging driving experience.
2.5L, 189 hp six-cylinder engine: Positioned as the higher-performance option, this engine provided an exhilarating driving experience with its increased power and torque. It enhanced the E36's sporty nature and showcased BMW's commitment to performance.


The BMW 3 Series (E30) and BMW 3 Series (E36) are two iconic generations that have shaped the legacy of the 3 Series. While the E30 laid the foundation for sporty compact sedans with its engaging driving dynamics, the E36 took the 3 Series to new heights, embracing technological advancements and delivering increased power.

The stock motors of the E36 showcased BMW's commitment to performance and driving pleasure. With enhanced power outputs and refined driving characteristics, the E36 offered a more exhilarating experience compared to its predecessor. The six-cylinder engines in particular brought a substantial increase in power and torque, elevating the driving dynamics of the E36.

However, it's important to note that the E30 still holds its charm among enthusiasts who appreciate its simplicity and nimble handling characteristics. While the power outputs of the E30 may not match those of the E36, its lightweight construction and engaging driving experience continue to captivate driving enthusiasts to this day.

Choosing between the stock motors of the BMW 3 Series (E30) and BMW 3 Series (E36) ultimately depends on individual preferences for power, driving dynamics, and the driving experience you seek. Both generations have left an indelible mark in the automotive world, solidifying the 3 Series' reputation as a driver's car and showcasing BMW's commitment to performance and engineering excellence.
