Nissan 370Z

The 370Z was formally introduced at the Greater LA Auto Show in 2008 after being officially announced on October 29, 2006, at which point it was initially seen at a Los Angeles event prior to that show. The Nissan Z-car line's sixth generation, which replaces the 350Z, is represented by the 370Z. The 370Z is the final production vehicle to include a naturally aspirated, high-revving V6 and a manual transmission. The 370Z was discontinued after the 2020 model year. The Nissan Z (RZ34), which used a modified version of the same platform, carried on the line.




Modified Nissan 370Z

Performance Upgrades:

Turbocharging and Supercharging: For those seeking substantial power gains, adding a turbocharger or supercharger is a popular choice. Kits from respected brands such as Stillen and GTM are available, and they can significantly increase horsepower and torque.

Exhaust Systems: Upgrading the exhaust system with high-performance headers, downpipes, and cat-back systems can improve engine efficiency, add power, and give the 370Z a more aggressive exhaust note. Brands like Borla and Invidia offer quality options.

Suspension and Handling: Improving the suspension with aftermarket coilovers, sway bars, and performance bushings can transform the 370Z's handling characteristics. Many enthusiasts opt for kits from brands like KW or Eibach to enhance the car's cornering abilities.


Aftermarket Support:

The 370Z enjoys robust aftermarket support, making it easy for enthusiasts to find a wide range of parts and accessories. From body kits and wheels to interior upgrades and electronic tuning solutions, the aftermarket community offers a wealth of options to customize and enhance the 370Z.

Design Modifications:

Body Kits: Body kits, such as those from Rocket Bunny or Nismo, can dramatically alter the car's appearance, adding aggressive styling and improved aerodynamics.

Custom Paint and Wraps: A fresh coat of paint or a unique vinyl wrap can give your 370Z a distinctive and head-turning appearance.

Motorsports Success:

The Nissan 370Z has seen significant success in the world of motorsports. It's a popular choice for drifting, time attack racing, and road racing. Many professional racing teams and grassroots enthusiasts use the 370Z as their platform of choice. Its combination of power, balance, and reliability makes it a formidable competitor in various motorsport disciplines.

Driving Experience:

Interior Upgrades: Improving the interior with racing seats, steering wheels, and advanced audio systems can make your 370Z more comfortable and enjoyable.

Sound Systems: Enhancing the car's audio system can provide a rich and immersive soundtrack to your drives.


Modifying a Nissan 370Z opens up a world of possibilities for car enthusiasts. Whether you're seeking to boost performance, personalize your ride, or aim for success in motorsports, the 370Z provides an excellent platform. With a wide array of aftermarket support and a strong community of fellow enthusiasts, the only limit is your imagination and budget. So, get ready to transform your 370Z into the ultimate driving machine, tailor-made to your desires and ambitions. Whether you're pursuing track records, style accolades, or simply a more thrilling daily drive, the 370Z is ready to exceed your expectations.
