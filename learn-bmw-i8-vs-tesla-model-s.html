BMW i8 vs. Tesla Model S



Stock Motor

Acceleration (0-60 mph): BMW i8: The BMW i8, with its hybrid powertrain, accelerates from 0 to 60 mph in approximately 4.2 seconds, making it a fast and exciting performer. Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S, renowned for its Ludicrous Mode, can achieve an astonishing 0 to 60 mph time in as little as 1.99 seconds for the high-performance Plaid variant, setting new records for electric cars. Torque: BMW i8: The BMW i8's hybrid powertrain combines a gasoline engine with an electric motor, delivering a total torque figure of around 420 lb-ft, providing quick and responsive acceleration. Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S's electric powertrain generates instant and immense torque, with high-performance variants producing up to 1,050 lb-ft of torque, resulting in unparalleled acceleration. Horsepower: BMW i8: The BMW i8's hybrid system combines a gasoline engine with an electric motor to produce a total horsepower of around 369 hp, delivering a balance of power and efficiency. Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S offers different power levels, with high-performance variants boasting up to 1,020 hp, providing exhilarating and record-breaking performance. Top Speed: BMW i8: The BMW i8 has an electronically limited top speed of 155 mph (250 km/h), which is more than enough for most driving situations. Tesla Model S: The top speed of the Tesla Model S varies depending on the specific variant, with some high-performance versions reaching speeds of up to 200 mph (322 km/h), making it one of the fastest electric cars in the world. Quarter Mile Time: BMW i8: The BMW i8's quarter-mile time is around 12.5 seconds, showcasing its strong performance capabilities in straight-line acceleration. Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S, with its Ludicrous Mode engaged, can complete the quarter-mile in just under 10 seconds for the high-performance Plaid variant, setting new records for electric cars. Lap Times: BMW i8: The BMW i8 is not primarily designed for track performance, and its lap times may not be as competitive as some high-performance sports cars. Tesla Model S: While not originally intended for track use, the Tesla Model S with its high-performance variants and track-focused settings can deliver competitive lap times. Powertrain Configuration: BMW i8: The BMW i8 features a plug-in hybrid powertrain, combining a mid-mounted turbocharged gasoline engine driving the rear wheels and an electric motor powering the front wheels for all-wheel drive capability. Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S is a pure electric vehicle, powered by electric motors that deliver power to either the rear wheels or all four wheels, depending on the variant. Transmission Type: BMW i8: The BMW i8 comes with a six-speed automatic transmission, working in conjunction with the electric motor and gasoline engine to optimize performance and efficiency. Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S does not have a traditional transmission, as electric motors deliver power directly to the wheels, eliminating the need for gear changes. Power to Weight Ratio: BMW i8: The power-to-weight ratio of the BMW i8 is optimized for its hybrid powertrain, striking a balance between power output and overall vehicle weight. Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S, especially in its high-performance variants, has an impressive power-to-weight ratio, contributing to its breathtaking acceleration and responsiveness. RPM (Shaft Speed): BMW i8: The specific RPM (shaft speed) of the BMW i8's gasoline engine and electric motor varies depending on driving conditions and power demand. Tesla Model S: The RPM (shaft speed) of the electric motors in the Tesla Model S also varies based on driving conditions and power requirements.





Suspension

Suspension Design:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 features a sophisticated suspension design that combines a double-wishbone front suspension with a multi-link rear suspension. This setup is commonly found in high-performance sports cars, offering precise handling and improved stability during spirited driving.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S utilizes an air suspension system for its high-end versions, providing an adjustable ride height and a smooth driving experience. It also offers coil spring suspension for its standard variants, aiming to balance comfort and handling.


Ride Comfort:

BMW i8: The BMW i8's suspension, though sporty, may be perceived as slightly firmer compared to more comfort-oriented vehicles. It prioritizes handling and responsiveness, which may result in a slightly stiffer ride.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S, with its air suspension option, offers a smoother and more adjustable ride experience. The air suspension can adapt to different road conditions and allows drivers to adjust the ride height based on their preferences.


Handling and Cornering:

BMW i8: The BMW i8's suspension, coupled with its lightweight carbon-fiber construction, provides excellent handling and precise cornering capabilities. It offers a sporty and engaging driving experience, especially on winding roads.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S with its air suspension offers good handling and cornering, providing stability and control during turns. However, it may not offer the same level of sharpness and agility as the BMW i8 due to its heavier weight and different suspension setup.


Ground Clearance:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 has a relatively low ground clearance, which is typical for sports cars. It is suitable for well-maintained roads and urban driving but may encounter challenges on rough or uneven terrain.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S, with its adjustable air suspension, allows drivers to increase ground clearance when needed, making it more versatile for different road conditions, including uneven surfaces or steep driveways.


Adaptive Suspension:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 does not come with adaptive suspension as a standard feature. However, it offers a Dynamic Damper Control option, which allows drivers to select between Comfort and Sport modes to adjust the suspension characteristics.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S with air suspension comes with adaptive damping, automatically adjusting damping rates based on road conditions and driving mode, enhancing both comfort and handling.


Driving Modes:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 offers various driving modes, including Eco Pro, Comfort, and Sport. Each mode alters the suspension and powertrain settings to match the driver's preferences and road conditions.


Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S features different driving modes, such as Comfort, Sport, and Ludicrous (for high-performance variants), which also influence the air suspension's behavior and responsiveness.





Interior

Interior Design:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 boasts a luxurious and sporty interior design, combining premium materials like leather, carbon fiber, and high-quality trims. The driver-focused cockpit exudes a sense of sophistication and performance.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S features a minimalist and futuristic interior design. With its uncluttered dashboard and large central touchscreen, it offers a clean and modern cabin environment.


Seating Capacity and Configuration:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 is a 2+2 sports car, offering seating for up to four passengers. However, the rear seats are relatively compact and may not be as comfortable for adults on longer journeys.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S is a five-seater sedan, providing ample space and comfort for five adult passengers. The rear seats offer generous legroom and headroom.


Infotainment System:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 is equipped with BMW's iDrive infotainment system, featuring a central display screen and a touchpad controller. The system provides access to navigation, media, and vehicle settings.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S features a massive 17-inch central touchscreen that controls almost all vehicle functions, including navigation, media, climate control, and Autopilot settings.


Digital Instrument Cluster:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 comes with a digital instrument cluster displaying essential driving information, such as speed, battery level, and range. It complements the traditional analog dials for a modern touch.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S replaces traditional gauges with a fully digital instrument cluster displayed on the central touchscreen. It offers a customizable and futuristic layout.


Autonomy and Driver Assistance:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 offers some driver assistance features, such as parking assistance and lane departure warning. However, it does not have full autonomous driving capabilities.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S is equipped with Tesla's renowned Autopilot system, which provides advanced driver assistance features, including Autosteer, Traffic-Aware Cruise Control, and Full Self-Driving capabilities (requires additional software purchase).


Interior Lighting:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 features ambient lighting, allowing drivers to customize the interior ambiance according to their preferences. It adds a touch of elegance to the cabin.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S offers customizable ambient lighting options, allowing drivers to set the interior lighting color and intensity to suit their mood.


Sound System:

BMW i8: The BMW i8 comes with a high-quality Harman Kardon sound system, providing an enjoyable audio experience for all occupants.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S offers a premium sound system by renowned audio brand, Ultra High Fidelity Sound, delivering a superior listening experience.


Interior Space and Storage:

BMW i8: The BMW i8's interior space is more compact, especially in the rear seats. It also has limited cargo space due to the hybrid powertrain components.

Tesla Model S: The Tesla Model S provides ample interior space, with comfortable seating for five passengers and generous cargo capacity in both the front and rear trunks.
