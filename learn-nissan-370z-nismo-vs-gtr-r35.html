Acceleration and Speed:


The Nissan GT-R R35 outshines the 370Z Nismo in terms of acceleration and top speed. The GT-R R35 achieves a blistering 0-60 mph time of approximately 2.9 seconds, while the 370Z Nismo takes around 5 seconds. The GT-R R35 also boasts a higher top speed of 196 mph, compared to the electronically limited 155 mph of the 370Z Nismo.


Track Performance:

While lap time data for the 370Z Nismo is unavailable, the GT-R R35 has established itself as a formidable contender on the racetrack. The GT-R R35 NISMO version, in particular, has achieved an impressive Nürburgring lap time of 7:08.679, showcasing its exceptional handling and performance capabilities.


Power and Torque:

The GT-R R35 significantly outperforms the 370Z Nismo in terms of power and torque. With 565 horsepower and 467 lb-ft of torque, the GT-R R35 dominates the road with its mighty engine. In comparison, the 370Z Nismo offers 350 horsepower and 276 lb-ft of torque, providing a thrilling but relatively less powerful experience.


Powertrain and Transmission:

The 370Z Nismo features a front-engine, rear-wheel drive powertrain configuration, while the GT-R R35 utilizes a front-engine, all-wheel drive setup. The GT-R R35 further benefits from a sophisticated 6-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission, delivering lightning-fast gear shifts and optimal power delivery. The 370Z Nismo offers the choice between a 6-speed manual transmission or a 7-speed automatic transmission.


Power-to-Weight Ratio:

The power-to-weight ratio is an essential metric to assess a vehicle's performance potential. The GT-R R35 showcases an impressive power-to-weight ratio of approximately 6.1 lb/HP, while the 370Z Nismo boasts a ratio of around 10.6 lb/HP. The lower the ratio, the better the power-to-weight balance, enabling the GT-R R35 to achieve outstanding acceleration and agility.





Tuning Ability

Upgraded Intake:

Improving the airflow into the engine is an essential aspect of tuning. Here are some popular options for upgraded intake systems:


Nissan 370Z Nismo:

Cold Air Intake Kits: Upgraded cold air intake kits help increase the volume of cool air entering the engine, improving horsepower and throttle response. Examples include AEM, Injen, and K&N.


Nissan GT-R R35:

High-Flow Air Filters: Replacing the factory air filters with high-flow options from brands like K&N or BMC can increase air intake and improve engine performance.


Upgraded Exhaust:

Enhancing the exhaust system can improve both performance and sound. Consider these options:


Nissan 370Z Nismo:

Cat-Back Exhaust Systems: Upgrading to a cat-back exhaust system, such as those offered by Borla, MagnaFlow, or Invidia, can increase horsepower, improve exhaust flow, and enhance the exhaust note.


Nissan GT-R R35:

Full Titanium Exhaust Systems: Premium full titanium exhaust systems from brands like Akrapovič or HKS offer weight reduction, improved exhaust flow, and increased power gains, while delivering an aggressive exhaust tone.

Forced Induction:

Forced induction can dramatically increase engine power. Here are some options for turbocharging and supercharging:


Nissan 370Z Nismo:

Twin-Turbo Kit: Companies like Stillen and Greddy offer twin-turbo kits specifically designed for the 370Z, providing significant power gains and improved performance.


Nissan GT-R R35:

Upgraded Turbochargers: Various companies, including AMS Performance and HKS, offer upgraded turbochargers for the GT-R R35, allowing for substantial power increases and enhanced overall performance.

Fuel Management:

Proper fuel management is essential to optimize performance and prevent engine damage. Consider these options:


Nissan 370Z Nismo:

Engine Control Unit (ECU) Tuning: Companies like UpRev and Osiris offer ECU tuning solutions that optimize fuel and ignition maps, maximizing power output while maintaining reliability.


Nissan GT-R R35:

Standalone Engine Management Systems: Advanced standalone engine management systems from brands like AEM or Haltech allow precise control over fuel delivery, ignition timing, and boost levels for increased performance and tuning flexibility.

Ignition Options:

Improving ignition performance can result in better combustion and power. Consider these options:


Nissan 370Z Nismo:

Performance Ignition Coils: Upgrading to high-performance ignition coils, such as those from Ignition Projects or MSD, can improve spark intensity and combustion efficiency.


Nissan GT-R R35:

Upgraded Spark Plugs: Premium spark plugs, such as those from NGK or Denso, designed specifically for high-performance applications, can improve ignition efficiency and power output.





Suspension

Nissan 370Z Nismo Suspension:

The Nissan 370Z Nismo is known for its sporty and engaging driving dynamics. Here are some key features of the 370Z Nismo's suspension:

Sport-Tuned Suspension: The 370Z Nismo benefits from a sport-tuned suspension, offering improved handling and responsiveness compared to the standard 370Z. It features stiffer springs, upgraded dampers, and thicker anti-roll bars for enhanced cornering capabilities.

Performance Brakes and Tuned ABS: The 370Z Nismo comes equipped with larger brakes and high-performance brake pads, providing strong stopping power and better heat dissipation. The advanced Anti-lock Braking System (ABS) is specifically tuned to work in harmony with the upgraded suspension for optimal control during spirited driving.

Stabilizer Bars: The 370Z Nismo features thicker stabilizer bars, reducing body roll and improving overall stability during aggressive cornering. This contributes to the car's nimble and responsive handling characteristics.

Limited-Slip Differential: The 370Z Nismo is equipped with a limited-slip differential, enhancing traction and delivering power more efficiently to the wheels during acceleration and cornering.


Nissan GT-R R35 Suspension:

The Nissan GT-R R35 is renowned for its exceptional performance and handling capabilities. Here are some notable features of the GT-R R35's suspension:

Advanced All-Wheel Drive System: The GT-R R35 utilizes an advanced all-wheel drive system that actively distributes power between the front and rear wheels for optimal grip and stability. This contributes to its outstanding traction and cornering abilities.

Adjustable Suspension: The GT-R R35 features an electronically controlled suspension system known as Bilstein DampTronic. It allows drivers to adjust suspension settings based on driving conditions and preferences, offering enhanced comfort or more aggressive performance as desired.

Multi-Link Suspension Design: The GT-R R35 employs a sophisticated multi-link suspension design, providing precise wheel control and excellent stability during high-speed maneuvers. This contributes to the car's exceptional cornering capabilities.

Active Stability Control: The GT-R R35 incorporates an advanced Active Stability Control system that continuously monitors the car's dynamics and adjusts individual wheel braking and power delivery to maintain optimal stability and control.


Suspension Comparison:

Let's compare the suspension systems of the Nissan 370Z Nismo and the Nissan GT-R R35:

Handling and Performance: Both models offer impressive handling capabilities, but the GT-R R35 takes it to another level. Its advanced all-wheel drive system and adjustable suspension provide exceptional grip, cornering ability, and stability, making it a formidable track machine.

Comfort and Daily Usability: While the GT-R R35 is more track-focused, the 370Z Nismo strikes a better balance between performance and daily usability. Its sport-tuned suspension provides an engaging driving experience without sacrificing too much comfort during regular commuting.

Technology and Adjustability: The GT-R R35's electronically controlled suspension and active stability control system offer greater adjustability and technological sophistication compared to the 370Z Nismo's more traditional sport-tuned suspension setup.





Interior

Nissan 370Z Nismo Interior:

The Nissan 370Z Nismo offers a driver-focused interior that complements its sporty character. Here are some key features of the 370Z Nismo's interior:

Performance-Oriented Cockpit: The 370Z Nismo's cockpit is designed with a focus on the driver. It features a wraparound design that envelops the driver, placing controls within easy reach and providing an immersive driving experience.

Sport Seats: The 370Z Nismo is equipped with sport seats that offer excellent support and bolstering to keep occupants in place during spirited driving. They are often finished in a combination of leather and suede materials, providing a sporty and premium feel.

Leather-Wrapped Steering Wheel: The steering wheel of the 370Z Nismo is typically wrapped in leather and features a thicker grip for better control. It often includes contrast stitching and the Nismo emblem, adding a touch of sportiness to the interior.

Carbon Fiber Accents: To enhance the sporty ambiance, the 370Z Nismo incorporates carbon fiber accents on the center console, door panels, and dashboard. These carbon fiber elements add a touch of modernity and high-performance aesthetics.


Nissan GT-R R35 Interior:

The Nissan GT-R R35 offers a more luxurious and technologically advanced interior. Here are some notable features of the GT-R R35's interior:

Driver-Focused Cockpit: Similar to the 370Z Nismo, the GT-R R35 features a driver-centric cockpit that emphasizes performance and control. The controls and displays are oriented towards the driver, enhancing the driving experience.

Premium Materials: The GT-R R35 showcases high-quality materials throughout the interior. From the leather-wrapped dashboard to the Alcantara headliner and high-grade upholstery, the GT-R R35 exudes luxury and refinement.

Multi-Function Display: The GT-R R35 incorporates a multi-function display that provides vital performance information, such as boost pressure, lap times, and vehicle dynamics. It allows the driver to monitor the car's performance at a glance.
Dual-Zone Climate Control: The GT-R R35 offers dual-zone climate control, allowing both the driver and passenger to set their preferred temperature settings for optimal comfort.


Interior Comparison:

Let's compare the interior aspects of the Nissan 370Z Nismo and the Nissan GT-R R35:

Sportiness vs. Luxury: The 370Z Nismo's interior leans more towards a sporty and performance-oriented design, while the GT-R R35's interior emphasizes luxury and high-end materials.

Technology and Displays: The GT-R R35's interior incorporates more advanced technology, such as the multi-function display, offering a wealth of performance data. The 370Z Nismo, while not as technologically advanced, provides a more focused and driver-centric experience.

Seating Comfort: Both models offer well-bolstered seats, but the GT-R R35 provides a higher level of comfort with premium materials and additional adjustability options.

Space and Practicality: The 370Z Nismo is a two-seater sports car, while the GT-R R35 offers seating for four. The GT-R R35 provides more space and practicality for passengers, making it a better option for extended trips or accommodating additional passengers.
