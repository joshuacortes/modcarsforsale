BMW 3 Series (E36)

The third generation of the BMW 3 Series line of small executive vehicles, known as the BMW E36, was manufactured by the German automaker BMW from 1990 to 2000. The four-door sedan body style was used for the first models, then in subsequent years, the coupe, convertible, wagon ("Touring"), hatchback ("Compact"), and the uncommon four-door convertible Baur TC4 were added.


Body Styles

1990-1998: 4-Door Sedan
1990-1999: 2-Door Coupe
1993-1999: 2-Door Convertible
1994-1995: 5-Door Wagon/Touring
1994-2000: 3-Door Hatchback

Drivetrain

1994-1999: 5 Speed Manual
1996-1999: 6 Speed Manual
4 Speed Automatic
5 Speed Automatic

All models are rear-wheel drive.


Modified BMW E36

Modifying a BMW E36, building a showcar/project drift car, racecar or anything in between? -Be aware of the aftermarket ability and overall support available for your E36 build.




The BMW E36 is a classic and iconic car model that has been popular for many years. As a result, there are numerous aftermarket parts and accessories available for it, including body kits. Body kits are a great way to enhance the appearance of your BMW E36 and make it stand out from the crowd. In this article, we will look at some of the best body kits for the BMW E36.

Hamann Body Kit

The M-Tech body kit includes a front bumper, side skirts, and a rear bumper. Cold Air Intake

A cold air intake is one of the most popular aftermarket performance parts for any car, and the E36 is no exception. A cold air intake replaces the stock airbox and filter with a high-performance filter and intake system that allows more air to flow into the engine. This increased airflow can improve engine performance and increase horsepower.


Performance Exhaust System

Upgrading your E36's exhaust system can also improve engine performance and increase horsepower. A performance exhaust system replaces the stock exhaust with a high-flowing system that allows exhaust gases to flow more freely. This can also improve the sound of your E36, giving it a deeper and more aggressive exhaust note.


Performance Suspension Components

The E36 is a great car to drive, but its suspension system can be improved with the addition of performance suspension components. Upgrading your E36's suspension can improve handling, reduce body roll, and improve overall driving dynamics. Some popular suspension upgrades for the E36 include adjustable coilovers, sway bars, and strut braces.


Performance Brakes

A high-performance brake system can help improve the stopping power of your E36. Upgrading your E36's brakes can include upgrading the brake pads and rotors, as well as adding a high-performance brake caliper and brake lines. A performance brake system can also help reduce brake fade during hard driving.


Performance ECU Chip

Upgrading the ECU chip is another popular way to improve the performance of your E36. A performance ECU chip can improve engine performance by adjusting the air/fuel mixture, ignition timing, and other engine parameters. This can lead to increased horsepower and improved throttle response.


In conclusion, there are many aftermarket performance parts available for the BMW E36 that can improve its performance and driving dynamics. Cold air intakes, performance exhaust systems, suspension components, brakes, and ECU chips are just a few examples of the many upgrades available. Ultimately, the choice of which parts to install will come down to your personal preference and budget, but any of the above-mentioned performance parts are a great place to start.
