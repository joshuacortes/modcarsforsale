Ford Mustang GT vs. Ford Mustang Shelby GT350



Stock Motor

In the realm of American muscle cars, few names carry as much weight and history as the Ford Mustang. Within the Mustang lineup, two models that have gained significant attention are the Mustang GT and the Mustang Shelby GT350. In this article, we will compare the stock motors of these iconic vehicles to understand the differences in performance, technology, and driving experience they offer.


Ford Mustang GT:

The Ford Mustang GT, a staple in the Mustang lineup, comes equipped with a potent 5.0-liter V8 engine. This naturally aspirated powertrain delivers an impressive 460 horsepower and 420 lb-ft of torque. The Mustang GT's engine utilizes advanced technologies such as dual-fuel, high-pressure direct injection, and twin independent variable camshaft timing (Ti-VCT) to optimize performance and efficiency.

The Mustang GT's V8 engine embodies the essence of classic American muscle, delivering a thrilling exhaust note and robust power throughout the rev range. With its impressive power output, the Mustang GT offers exhilarating acceleration and a satisfying driving experience on both the road and the track.


Ford Mustang Shelby GT350:

Taking performance to an even higher level, the Ford Mustang Shelby GT350 features a specialized 5.2-liter V8 engine. This naturally aspirated powertrain is known as the Voodoo engine and produces an awe-inspiring 526 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque. The GT350's engine is unique, featuring a flat-plane crankshaft, which contributes to its high-revving nature and distinctive exhaust note.

The GT350's Voodoo engine is a masterpiece of engineering, designed for track-focused performance. It utilizes advanced features such as a large throttle body, high-flow cylinder heads, and a tuned exhaust system to deliver an exhilarating driving experience. The GT350's engine revs eagerly, providing incredible power and a thrilling soundtrack that enthusiasts crave.


Performance and Handling:

The Mustang GT's stock motor offers a tremendous amount of power, providing thrilling straight-line acceleration and an exciting driving experience. Its naturally aspirated V8 engine delivers strong low-end torque and an abundance of power throughout the rev range. The Mustang GT's suspension, combined with its power delivery, offers a balanced and engaging driving experience, making it suitable for both everyday driving and occasional track outings.

The GT350's stock motor, with its higher horsepower output and specialized design, takes performance to the next level. The Voodoo engine's flat-plane crankshaft allows for higher revving and quicker throttle response, delivering exhilarating acceleration and precise handling. The GT350's suspension system is fine-tuned for track performance, with enhancements such as magnetorheological dampers and larger brakes, providing exceptional handling and control.


Technological Advancements:

Both the Mustang GT and GT350 benefit from Ford's commitment to technological advancements. They feature modern conveniences such as advanced infotainment systems, smartphone integration, and driver-assistance technologies. However, it's important to note that the GT350 places a greater emphasis on track performance, with specialized features like track-tuned stability control and launch control, further enhancing its performance capabilities.


The comparison of the stock motors in the Ford Mustang GT and Mustang Shelby GT350 reveals the impressive performance and engineering prowess of both vehicles. The Mustang GT's 5.0-liter V8 engine offers thrilling power and acceleration, embodying the essence of classic American muscle. On the other hand, the GT350's specialized 5.2-liter V8 Voodoo engine takes performance to new heights, delivering exhilarating power and precision handling.

Choosing between the Mustang GT and GT350 ultimately depends on personal preferences, driving goals, and budget considerations. Both vehicles offer a captivating driving experience, with the Mustang GT providing a well-rounded blend of power and comfort, while the GT350 caters to those seeking uncompromising track performance. No matter the choice, these Mustangs embody the spirit of American muscle and provide an exhilarating ride for automotive enthusiasts.





Tuning Ability

Ford Mustang GT:

The Ford Mustang GT, with its 5.0-liter V8 engine, has long been a favorite among tuners due to its tunability and aftermarket support. The naturally aspirated V8 powerplant provides a solid foundation for performance upgrades. Enthusiasts can explore various modifications, such as cold air intakes, exhaust systems, performance headers, and engine management tuning, to extract additional power and enhance overall performance.

With the right modifications, the Mustang GT's engine can see significant power gains, improved throttle response, and a more aggressive exhaust note. Additional upgrades to the suspension, brakes, and tires can further enhance the handling and overall driving dynamics of the Mustang GT, offering a well-rounded performance package.


Ford Mustang Shelby GT350:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT350, with its specialized 5.2-liter V8 Voodoo engine, offers a higher level of performance right out of the box. However, due to its unique design and high-revving nature, modifications for the GT350 may require more specialized expertise and aftermarket components.

While the GT350's Voodoo engine can still benefit from aftermarket modifications, the options may be more limited compared to the Mustang GT. Upgrades such as cold air intakes, exhaust systems, and engine tuning can still yield noticeable performance gains, but the GT350's specialized components and tuning may require more careful consideration and consultation with experienced tuners.


Performance and Reliability Considerations:

When it comes to tuning, it's crucial to consider the potential impact on reliability and drivability. Pushing the limits of any engine through extensive modifications may necessitate additional supporting upgrades to ensure proper performance and longevity.

Upgraded fuel systems, enhanced cooling systems, reinforced drivetrain components, and suspension and brake enhancements are often recommended to handle the increased power and torque resulting from tuning. Consulting with experienced professionals and reputable tuning shops can help ensure that the modifications are performed correctly and that the vehicle's reliability and drivability are maintained.


Both the Ford Mustang GT and Mustang Shelby GT350 offer exciting tuning potential for enthusiasts seeking to extract more power and performance from their vehicles. The Mustang GT's 5.0-liter V8 engine provides a solid platform for modifications, offering a wide range of aftermarket options to enhance performance and overall driving dynamics.

While the GT350's specialized Voodoo engine may require more specialized modifications, it still offers the potential for performance gains and an exhilarating driving experience. However, due to its unique design and higher-performance nature, modifications for the GT350 may require more expertise and careful consideration.

Regardless of the choice between the Mustang GT and GT350, it is important to approach tuning with knowledge, planning, and consultation with experienced professionals. With the right modifications and supporting upgrades, both vehicles can be transformed into formidable performance machines, offering an enhanced driving experience for enthusiasts who seek to unleash the full potential of their Mustangs.





Suspension

Ford Mustang GT:

The Ford Mustang GT is equipped with a well-tuned suspension system designed to deliver a balance of comfort and sportiness. It features an independent MacPherson strut front suspension and an integral-link independent rear suspension. The suspension is further complemented by performance-tuned springs, dampers, and anti-roll bars.

The Mustang GT's suspension strikes a balance between responsive handling and a comfortable ride. The setup provides precise steering feedback, allowing drivers to confidently tackle corners while maintaining a compliant ride quality during daily driving. The suspension is optimized to offer a dynamic driving experience that blends performance and comfort, making it suitable for a variety of driving scenarios.


Ford Mustang Shelby GT350:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT350 takes the performance and handling to a higher level with a more track-focused suspension system. It features an advanced MagneRide® Damping System, which utilizes magnetorheological fluid-filled dampers that adjust damping rates in real-time based on road conditions and driving inputs. The GT350 also incorporates stiffer springs, larger anti-roll bars, and revised suspension geometry.

The GT350's suspension is specifically tuned for enhanced handling and track performance. The MagneRide® system allows for precise adjustments to suspension stiffness, optimizing grip and stability. The stiffer suspension components, combined with the revised geometry, improve body control and reduce body roll, resulting in exceptional cornering capabilities and a more responsive and engaging driving experience.


Performance and Handling:

The Mustang GT's suspension offers a balanced and enjoyable driving experience. It provides confident handling, with responsive turn-in and good grip. The suspension's tuning strikes a harmony between sportiness and comfort, making it well-suited for a wide range of driving conditions.

The GT350's suspension, on the other hand, is optimized for performance-oriented driving. The MagneRide® Damping System allows for instantaneous adjustments, providing exceptional stability, grip, and control during aggressive cornering. The stiffer suspension components and revised geometry enhance the GT350's ability to tackle sharp turns and offer precise handling that is especially appreciated on the track.


Comfort and Daily Driving:

While the GT350's suspension prioritizes performance, it remains comfortable enough for daily driving. The MagneRide® Damping System offers adaptive capabilities that can provide a more compliant ride during normal commuting, ensuring that the GT350 is not solely limited to track use. However, the GT350's stiffer suspension may result in a slightly firmer ride compared to the Mustang GT.

The Mustang GT's suspension is well-balanced for everyday driving, providing a comfortable ride with the ability to deliver exhilarating performance when desired. The suspension is designed to absorb road imperfections and provide a smooth driving experience for daily commutes and long-distance trips.


The comparison of the suspension systems in the Ford Mustang GT and Ford Mustang Shelby GT350 highlights the different approaches taken to optimize performance and handling. The Mustang GT offers a well-tuned suspension that strikes a balance between sportiness and comfort, providing an enjoyable driving experience for various scenarios. On the other hand, the GT350's suspension is specifically tuned for track performance, delivering exceptional cornering capabilities and precise handling.





Interior

Ford Mustang GT:

The interior of the Ford Mustang GT strikes a balance between sportiness and comfort, creating an inviting and driver-centric cabin. The design emphasizes ergonomics, with controls and instruments positioned for easy accessibility and visibility. The Mustang GT's interior features high-quality materials, such as soft-touch surfaces, premium cloth or leather upholstery options, and tasteful trim accents.

The Mustang GT offers a variety of available comfort features, including power-adjustable seats, heated and ventilated front seats, and dual-zone automatic climate control. The cockpit is designed to provide a comfortable and engaging driving experience, with a well-placed steering wheel and supportive seating. The interior also includes modern technology features such as an infotainment system with a touchscreen display, smartphone integration, and available premium audio systems.


Ford Mustang Shelby GT350:

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT350's interior reflects its track-oriented performance, with a focus on sportiness and driver engagement. The cabin features unique touches that differentiate it from the Mustang GT, including sport seats with increased bolstering for better lateral support during spirited driving. The Shelby GT350's interior is designed to be purposeful, with a minimalist aesthetic that removes non-essential elements to reduce weight.

While the Shelby GT350's interior maintains a driver-centric layout, it offers a comfortable and well-appointed environment. Premium materials, such as Alcantara and leather-trimmed seats, provide a luxurious feel. The GT350 also incorporates advanced technology, including an infotainment system with a touchscreen display, track apps, and available navigation.


Performance Enhancements:

In terms of performance enhancements, both the Mustang GT and GT350 offer unique features to enhance the driving experience. The GT350, being the more track-focused variant, includes specialized additions such as a flat-bottom steering wheel, aluminum pedals, and performance-oriented gauges. The GT350's interior is also equipped with a unique engine start button and a redline shift indicator, reinforcing its performance-oriented nature.

While the Mustang GT may not have the same track-focused enhancements as the GT350, it can still be equipped with optional performance packages that add sporty elements such as sport seats, unique trims, and performance gauges, allowing drivers to customize the interior to their preferences.


The interior comparison between the Ford Mustang GT and Ford Mustang Shelby GT350 showcases their individual approaches to performance and comfort. The Mustang GT offers a well-rounded interior that balances sportiness and comfort, providing a comfortable and driver-focused experience suitable for everyday driving. On the other hand, the GT350's interior prioritizes performance and driver engagement, with specialized features and a purposeful layout.
