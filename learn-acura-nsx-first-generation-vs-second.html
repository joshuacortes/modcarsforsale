Acura NSX (first generation) vs. Acura NSX (second generation)



Stock Motor

Performance Metrics:

1st Generation:

0-60 mph: Approximately 4.8 seconds

Torque: 224 lb-ft

Horsepower: 290 hp

Top Speed: Around 168 mph

Quarter Mile Time: Approximately 13.2 seconds

Lap Times (Nürburgring): Not officially recorded


2nd Generation:

0-60 mph: Approximately 2.7 seconds

Torque: 476 lb-ft

Horsepower: 573 hp

Top Speed: Around 191 mph

Quarter Mile Time: Approximately 10.8 seconds

Lap Times (Nürburgring): 7 minutes 25.64 seconds

Powertrain Configuration:

1st Generation:

Mid-engine layout

3.0-liter V6 engine with VTEC (Variable Valve Timing and Lift Electronic Control)

Electric Motor Assist

Rear-wheel drive


2nd Generation:

Mid-engine layout

3.5-liter twin-turbocharged V6 engine with VTEC

Three Electric Motor Assist (Twin Motor Unit)

Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD)

Transmission Type:


1st Generation:

6-speed manual transmission (standard)

4-speed automatic transmission (optional)


2nd Generation:

9-speed dual-clutch automatic transmission (standard)

Power-to-Weight Ratio:


1st Generation:

Approximately 7.24 lb/hp


2nd Generation:

Approximately 5.48 lb/hp

RPM (Shaft Speed):


1st Generation:

Redline: 8,000 RPM


2nd Generation:

Redline: 7,500 RPM





Tuning Ability

First Generation Acura NSX:

Intake Upgrades:

Cold Air Intake Systems: K&N Typhoon, AEM V2, Injen SP Series

Exhaust Upgrades:

Cat-Back Exhaust Systems: HKS Hi-Power, Invidia Q300, Greddy SP Elite

Headers: DC Sports, Comptech

Test Pipes: Berk Technology, Megan Racing


Forced Induction:

Supercharger Kits: Comptech, ScienceofSpeed


Fuel Management:

Engine Management Systems: AEM EMS V2, Hondata FlashPro, Haltech Platinum Pro


Ignition Options:

High-Performance Ignition Coils: MSD, AEM, NGK




Second Generation Acura NSX:

Intake Upgrades:

Carbon Fiber Intake Systems: Eventuri, Takeda, GruppeM


Exhaust Upgrades:

Cat-Back Exhaust Systems: Akrapovic, Armytrix, Capristo

Headers: AWE Tuning, Frequency Intelligent Exhaust (Fi Exhaust)

Test Pipes: Soul Performance, Fast Intentions


Forced Induction:

Twin-Turbocharger Kits: ScienceofSpeed


Fuel Management:

Engine Management Systems: Hondata FlashPro, KTuner, EcuTek


Ignition Options:


High-Performance Ignition Coils: NGK, MSD, Denso


Both the first and second generation Acura NSX offer ample tuning potential for enthusiasts looking to extract more power and performance from their vehicles. However, the second generation NSX benefits from advancements in technology and a more modern design, providing a wider range of aftermarket options.

The first generation NSX offers various intake, exhaust, forced induction, fuel management, and ignition upgrades, allowing enthusiasts to fine-tune their vehicle's performance. Supercharger kits from Comptech and ScienceofSpeed offer significant power gains for those seeking forced induction.

The second generation NSX features a more extensive selection of carbon fiber intake systems, high-performance exhaust systems, and advanced twin-turbocharger kits from reputable brands like Eventuri, Akrapovic, and ScienceofSpeed. Engine management systems like Hondata FlashPro and EcuTek offer advanced tuning capabilities to optimize the vehicle's performance.





Suspension

First Generation Acura NSX:

Suspension Design:

Double-wishbone suspension system at all four corners.

Aluminum control arms and uprights to reduce unsprung weight.

Coil springs and gas-filled shock absorbers.


Performance Characteristics:

The first generation NSX featured a well-balanced suspension setup, providing excellent handling and responsiveness.

The double-wishbone design allowed for precise control of wheel movement and minimized body roll.

The suspension tuning was focused on delivering a comfortable yet sporty ride, suitable for both spirited driving and daily use.


Second Generation Acura NSX:

Suspension Design:

Double-wishbone suspension system at all four corners.

Lightweight aluminum and forged aluminum components for reduced unsprung weight.

Electronically controlled dampers (Magnetorheological Dampers) with Real-Time Damping™ technology.


Performance Characteristics:

The second generation NSX introduced an advanced suspension system, incorporating electronic control and adaptive capabilities.

The Magnetorheological Dampers continuously adjust damping force based on road conditions, vehicle speed, and driver inputs.

This adaptive suspension system enhances both comfort and performance, providing optimal handling characteristics in various driving situations.

The suspension tuning is highly responsive and can adapt to changes in driving modes (e.g., Comfort, Sport, Sport+, Track).


Additional Features:

Integrated Dynamics System (IDS): Allows drivers to select different driving modes that adjust suspension, powertrain, and other vehicle parameters to suit their preferences and driving conditions.

Sport Hybrid Super Handling All-Wheel Drive (SH-AWD): Enhances traction and stability through electric motors driving the front wheels, improving overall cornering capabilities.





Interior

First Generation Acura NSX:

Design and Layout:

Driver-focused cockpit with a simple and functional layout.

Ergonomically designed controls and gauges for easy access and readability.

Classic analog instrument cluster with clear and concise dials.

Comfortable and supportive seating with a focus on the driver's position.

Generous visibility and a low cowl height, providing an immersive driving experience.


Materials and Finish:

High-quality materials used throughout the cabin, including premium leather and soft-touch surfaces.

Aluminum accents and trim pieces added a touch of sportiness.

Overall fit and finish were commendable, reflecting attention to detail.


Features and Technology:

Although advanced for its time, the first generation NSX had a relatively basic array of features.

Standard amenities included air conditioning, power windows, and a premium sound system.

Limited infotainment options with a central display for audio and climate controls.

Connectivity and advanced driver assistance features were minimal or non-existent.

Second Generation Acura NSX:


Design and Layout:

A modern and driver-centric interior design, reflecting contemporary sports car aesthetics.

Clean and streamlined dashboard with a focus on simplicity and intuitive controls.

Digital instrument cluster with customizable displays and comprehensive driver information.

Sporty yet luxurious seating with ample bolstering and support.

Improved visibility and a driver-oriented layout for enhanced engagement.


Materials and Finish:

Premium materials such as leather, Alcantara, and carbon fiber used extensively throughout the cabin.

Attention to detail and meticulous craftsmanship evident in the overall fit and finish.

Aluminum accents and optional carbon fiber trim elements exude a high-end feel.


Features and Technology:

The second generation NSX introduced a host of advanced features and technology.

Large, high-resolution touchscreen infotainment system with smartphone integration and navigation.

Integrated driver assistance features like blind-spot monitoring and adaptive cruise control.

Enhanced connectivity options, including Bluetooth, USB ports, and voice commands.

Advanced audio systems available, providing exceptional sound quality.
