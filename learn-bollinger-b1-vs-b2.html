Bollinger B1 vs. Bollinger B2



Stock Motor

0-60 Acceleration:

Bollinger B1: The B1 boasts an impressive 0-60 mph acceleration time of approximately 4.5 seconds, making it a quick and agile off-road machine.

Bollinger B2: Slightly edging its sibling, the B2 can reach 0-60 mph in approximately 4.3 seconds, thanks to its well-tuned powertrain.


Torque:

Bollinger B1: Equipped with a robust electric motor, the B1 generates an exceptional torque of around 668 lb-ft, ensuring ample power for conquering challenging terrains.

Bollinger B2: The B2 matches its sibling with a comparable torque output of 668 lb-ft, making it equally capable in rough off-road conditions.


Horsepower:

Bollinger B1: The B1's stock motor delivers an impressive horsepower of approximately 614 hp, giving it the necessary oomph for thrilling off-road adventures.

Bollinger B2: Keeping pace with the B1, the B2 also provides around 614 hp of power, ensuring a balanced performance on various surfaces.


Top Speed:

Bollinger B1: The B1 is electronically limited to a top speed of 100 mph (160 km/h), as it prioritizes off-road capability and safety over high-speed cruising.

Bollinger B2: Similar to the B1, the B2's top speed is electronically capped at 100 mph (160 km/h) for the same reasons.


Quarter Mile Time:

Bollinger B1: The B1 can complete the quarter-mile in approximately 12.9 seconds, showcasing its impressive acceleration and power delivery.

Bollinger B2: With similar performance, the B2 achieves the quarter-mile mark in about 12.8 seconds, highlighting its sporty characteristics.


Lap Times:

Bollinger B1: Although not primarily designed for track racing, the B1 still manages respectable lap times thanks to its powerful electric motor and capable chassis.

Bollinger B2: Like the B1, the B2 is more geared towards off-road adventures, but it can also hold its own on the track with competitive lap times.


Powertrain Configuration:

Bollinger B1: Both the B1 and B2 share the same dual-motor setup, featuring an electric motor on each axle, resulting in a versatile and efficient all-wheel-drive configuration.

Bollinger B2: With the identical powertrain setup, the B2 offers the same level of off-road prowess as its sibling, providing balanced power distribution.


Transmission Type:

Bollinger B1: As fully electric vehicles, the B1 and B2 have a single-speed transmission, which simplifies operation and reduces maintenance needs.


Power-to-Weight Ratio:

Bollinger B1: With its curb weight of approximately 5,000 lbs and horsepower of 614 hp, the B1 achieves an impressive power-to-weight ratio, contributing to its excellent performance.

Bollinger B2: The B2's similar power-to-weight ratio, owing to its comparable curb weight and horsepower, ensures an equally thrilling driving experience.


RPM (Shaft Speed):

Bollinger B1: The B1's electric motor can reach up to around 13,000 RPM (Revolutions Per Minute), translating into efficient power delivery and responsiveness.

Bollinger B2: Likewise, the B2's electric motor can spin at speeds of up to approximately 13,000 RPM, guaranteeing ample torque across different terrains.





Suspension

Bollinger B1 Suspension:

Coil Spring Suspension: The B1 features a coil spring suspension system, a tried-and-true setup commonly found in off-road vehicles. Coil springs offer excellent articulation and durability, handling rough terrains with ease.

Hydraulic Dampers: The B1's suspension is complemented by hydraulic dampers, providing consistent damping and absorbing shocks efficiently. This setup contributes to a stable and controlled ride.

Long-Travel Design: With extended suspension travel, the B1 can tackle deep ruts, steep inclines, and uneven surfaces without compromising stability. This long-travel design enhances off-road capabilities.


Bollinger B2 Suspension:

Hydropneumatic Suspension: The B2 takes a different approach with its hydropneumatic suspension system. This system uses fluid-filled struts and accumulators to adjust the ride height and damping characteristics.

Self-Leveling Function: One of the key advantages of hydropneumatic suspension is its self-leveling capability. It automatically adjusts the ride height based on load distribution, ensuring a consistent and balanced ride even with varying cargo loads.

Adaptive Damping: The B2's suspension system offers adaptive damping, allowing it to adjust the damping characteristics based on driving conditions. This results in improved handling and comfort on different terrains.


Off-Road Performance - Bollinger B1:

Articulation: The coil spring suspension in the B1 provides excellent articulation, allowing each wheel to move independently. This feature translates into improved traction and stability on challenging off-road trails.

Durability: The coil springs in the B1 are known for their ruggedness and reliability, making them well-suited for frequent off-road use and harsh environments.

Consistency: The B1's suspension delivers consistent performance across various terrains, maintaining a smooth ride even during aggressive off-road maneuvers.


Off-Road Performance - Bollinger B2:

Variable Ride Height: The hydropneumatic suspension in the B2 allows for variable ride height adjustment. The driver can choose between different ride heights to adapt to changing terrains, giving the B2 an edge in versatility.

Improved Comfort: The self-leveling and adaptive damping functions in the B2 result in a more comfortable ride, especially when carrying heavy loads or driving on uneven surfaces.

Terrain Adaptation: The B2's suspension adapts quickly to different terrains, optimizing performance and ensuring maximum traction in various off-road scenarios.

Maintenance and Repairs - Bollinger B1 vs. B2:


Bollinger B1: The coil spring suspension in the B1 is relatively straightforward and widely understood, making maintenance and repairs more accessible and potentially more cost-effective.


Bollinger B2: The hydropneumatic suspension in the B2, while innovative, may require specialized knowledge and equipment for maintenance and repairs, which could lead to higher servicing costs.





Interior

Interior Layout - Bollinger B1:

Classic SUV Design: The B1 boasts a traditional SUV layout, with two rows of seats, comfortably accommodating up to four passengers. The driver's seat and controls are oriented in a familiar and straightforward manner.

Cargo Space: The B1's interior offers a generous cargo space behind the second-row seats, making it ideal for hauling gear, luggage, or groceries during daily use or off-road adventures.

Minimalist Aesthetics: The B1's interior design follows a minimalist approach with a focus on functionality and utility, embodying a rugged and purpose-driven feel.


Interior Layout - Bollinger B2:

Extended Pickup Design: The B2 features an extended pickup configuration, with a cab that can comfortably seat up to four passengers. Behind the rear seats, it offers an open cargo bed, providing ample space for transporting larger items or equipment.

Versatile Cargo Bed: The B2's open cargo bed allows for more flexibility in loading and transporting items, making it a suitable choice for work-related tasks and outdoor activities.

Ergonomic Controls: The B2's interior layout maintains user-friendly controls, ensuring an intuitive driving experience despite its unique pickup design.


Material Quality and Comfort - Bollinger B1:

Utilitarian Finishes: The B1's interior showcases utilitarian finishes, emphasizing durability over luxury. It typically features durable plastics and cloth seats, offering easy cleanability after off-road adventures.

Cozy Seating: While it may not prioritize lavishness, the B1's seating provides ample comfort and support for occupants, ensuring a pleasant experience even on extended journeys.


Material Quality and Comfort - Bollinger B2:

Upgraded Finishes: The B2's interior aims to strike a balance between utility and refinement. It often incorporates higher-quality materials, such as premium upholstery and soft-touch surfaces, providing a more upscale feel.

Enhanced Seating: The B2's seats often offer additional cushioning and ergonomic adjustments, enhancing overall comfort during long drives or work-related tasks.


Infotainment and Technology - Bollinger B1:

Standard Features: The B1 typically includes essential infotainment features such as a touchscreen display, Bluetooth connectivity, and smartphone integration. While functional, it may lack some of the more advanced tech found in luxury vehicles.

Off-Road Features: The B1's infotainment system may include specialized off-road features like trail mapping and terrain-specific driving modes, catering to the adventurous spirit of its target audience.


Infotainment and Technology - Bollinger B2:

Enhanced Connectivity: The B2 often boasts improved infotainment capabilities, with larger touchscreen displays and additional connectivity options, elevating the overall tech experience.

Work-Focused Features: The B2's infotainment system may prioritize work-related applications, offering features like towing assist, payload management, and enhanced navigation for job-specific tasks.


Interior Space and Versatility:

Bollinger B1: With its SUV layout, the B1 provides ample passenger space and sufficient storage behind the rear seats. It suits daily commuting and off-road adventures with a focus on passenger comfort.

Bollinger B2: The B2's extended pickup design sacrifices some passenger space to accommodate the open cargo bed. It is geared towards users who require the versatility of a pickup for work or outdoor activities.
