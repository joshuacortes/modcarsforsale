Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI vs. Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX



Stock Motor

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution series has been celebrated for its rally-bred performance and exhilarating driving experience. The Evolution VI and Evolution IX are two iconic models in the lineup, each equipped with impressive stock motors. In this article, we will compare the stock motors of the Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX, highlighting their similarities, differences, and the impact they have on performance and driving dynamics.


Evolution VI:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI, produced from 1999 to 2001, featured a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine known as the 4G63. In its stock form, the Evolution VI's engine produced around 276 horsepower and 275 lb-ft of torque. This power was delivered through Mitsubishi's advanced all-wheel drive system, providing exceptional traction and control. The Evolution VI was revered for its robust acceleration, precise handling, and quick response, making it a favorite among driving enthusiasts.


Evolution IX:

The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX, manufactured from 2005 to 2007, showcased further advancements in power and performance. It also featured a turbocharged 2.0-liter four-cylinder engine, but with notable upgrades. The Evolution IX's engine, also referred to as the 4G63, was equipped with Mitsubishi's MIVEC (Mitsubishi Innovative Valve Timing Electronic Control) technology. This intelligent valve timing system improved torque delivery and overall engine efficiency. The Evolution IX produced approximately 286 horsepower and 289 lb-ft of torque, providing an additional boost in power compared to its predecessor.


Turbocharging and Boost Pressure:

Both the Evolution VI and Evolution IX boasted turbocharged engines, which played a crucial role in their performance capabilities. The turbochargers in these models enabled efficient air intake, resulting in increased power and torque output. While specific turbocharging systems varied slightly between the two models, they shared the goal of delivering exhilarating acceleration and responsive throttle response.


Transmission Options:

Both the Evolution VI and Evolution IX offered a choice between a five-speed manual transmission and a six-speed manual transmission. The manual transmissions were renowned for their precise and engaging shifting experience, allowing drivers to fully exploit the power and performance of these vehicles. The availability of multiple gears and the well-tuned transmission ratios contributed to quick acceleration and seamless power delivery.


Suspension and Handling:

The Evolution VI and Evolution IX were lauded for their exceptional handling and dynamic capabilities. Both models featured advanced suspension systems, including independent front and rear setups, as well as performance-tuned shock absorbers and stabilizer bars. Additionally, the Evolution IX introduced Mitsubishi's Active Center Differential (ACD), allowing drivers to adjust the torque distribution between the front and rear wheels, further enhancing traction and cornering abilities.


Performance and Driving Experience:

Both the Evolution VI and Evolution IX delivered adrenaline-pumping performance and an engaging driving experience. The Evolution VI impressed with its quick acceleration and nimble handling, providing a raw and exciting ride. The Evolution IX built upon this foundation, refining the power delivery, optimizing engine efficiency, and incorporating advanced technologies for enhanced overall performance. The result was a more refined driving experience with improved responsiveness and higher levels of control.


The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI and Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX are legendary vehicles celebrated for their rally-inspired performance. The Evolution VI laid the foundation with its impressive power output, agile handling, and renowned all-wheel drive system. The Evolution IX took the performance to the next level, incorporating advancements in engine technology and fine-tuning various components for enhanced power, efficiency, and driving dynamics. Both models embody the spirit of Mitsubishi's commitment to performance engineering, delivering exhilarating experiences behind the wheel.

Comparison and Critique:

Turbocharger Options: The Evolution IX comes with a more advanced turbocharger setup, offering greater tuning potential and higher power gains compared to the Evolution VI.

Fuel Delivery: The Evolution IX's fuel management enhancements, including larger fuel injectors and E85-compatible fuel pump options, enable better control over the fuel delivery, allowing for more aggressive tuning.

Aftermarket Support: The Evolution IX, being a more recent model, enjoys a wider range of aftermarket tuning parts and options compared to the Evolution VI.

Engine Management System: The Evolution IX benefits from more sophisticated engine management systems like the COBB Tuning Accessport V3, providing precise tuning control and flexibility.





Suspension

Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI:

Front Suspension: The Evolution VI is equipped with a MacPherson strut front suspension, a proven and reliable design commonly found in high-performance vehicles.

Rear Suspension: At the rear, the Evolution VI features a multi-link independent suspension, offering better control over wheel movement and enhanced handling characteristics.

Rally Heritage: The Evolution VI, with its robust suspension system, is designed with a rally-inspired setup that focuses on precise cornering and responsive handling.

Tuning Potential: The Evolution VI's suspension allows for various aftermarket upgrades, including sport-tuned coilovers and sway bars, providing enthusiasts with the opportunity to fine-tune the car's handling to their preferences.

Ride Comfort: While the Evolution VI prioritizes performance and agility, it may be slightly stiffer compared to non-performance-oriented sedans, resulting in a firm but sporty ride.


Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX:

Front Suspension: Similar to its predecessor, the Evolution IX boasts a MacPherson strut front suspension, maintaining a familiar and proven layout.

Rear Suspension: The Evolution IX also retains the multi-link independent rear suspension, offering improved handling and cornering capabilities compared to its predecessors.

Chassis Reinforcements: Mitsubishi introduced additional chassis reinforcements in the Evolution IX to enhance rigidity, resulting in better stability and improved overall driving dynamics.

Tuning Potential: The Evolution IX benefits from a more extensive aftermarket support, with a wider selection of suspension upgrades, making it a favorite among enthusiasts seeking to push the vehicle's performance limits.

Ride Comfort: While the Evolution IX retains the sporty suspension tuning, it has been refined to provide a more comfortable ride without compromising its impressive handling capabilities.


Comparison and Critique:

Handling and Stability: Both the Evolution VI and Evolution IX excel in handling and stability, thanks to their multi-link independent suspension setups, providing precise cornering and responsive driving experiences.

Aftermarket Support: The Evolution IX enjoys a more extensive aftermarket support, offering a wider range of suspension upgrades and tuning options for those seeking to enhance the vehicle's performance further.

Ride Comfort: The Evolution IX's suspension has been fine-tuned to offer a more comfortable ride compared to the Evolution VI, providing a better balance between sportiness and everyday usability.

Rally Heritage: Both generations of the Evo stay true to their rally heritage, delivering an exhilarating driving experience that appeals to performance enthusiasts and motorsport fans alike.





Interior

Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution VI:

Interior Design: The Evolution VI's interior boasts a no-nonsense and purpose-driven design, focusing on functionality over frills. Its driver-centric layout exudes a sporty and performance-oriented vibe.

Materials: The cabin materials in the Evolution VI are of decent quality, but they may feel dated by modern standards. Hard plastics are common, with limited soft-touch surfaces.

Infotainment System: The Evolution VI features a basic infotainment system with limited connectivity options. It includes a simple audio unit without advanced touchscreen displays or smartphone integration.

Seating: The seats in the Evolution VI offer good support for spirited driving, with sporty bolsters and comfortable cushioning, but they lack modern conveniences like heating and ventilation.

Technology: The technology features in the Evolution VI are relatively basic, focusing on the essentials for performance driving. The emphasis is on delivering a pure and engaging driving experience.


Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution IX:

Interior Design: The Evolution IX's interior showcases a more refined and contemporary design, with subtle upgrades and improved ergonomics. The driver-centric layout remains, emphasizing the car's high-performance nature.

Materials: Mitsubishi upgraded the cabin materials in the Evolution IX, introducing softer touch surfaces and premium upholstery, elevating the overall feel and perceived quality of the interior.

Infotainment System: The Evolution IX features an upgraded infotainment system with a larger touchscreen display, advanced smartphone integration, and available navigation options, providing more modern connectivity and convenience.

Seating: The seats in the Evolution IX offer better comfort and support, with improved adjustability and optional heating and ventilation features, enhancing long-distance driving comfort.

Technology: The Evolution IX boasts additional technology features, including steering wheel-mounted controls, USB ports, and available premium audio systems, catering to the demands of modern drivers.


Comparison and Critique:

Aesthetics and Comfort: The Evolution IX's interior surpasses its predecessor in terms of aesthetics, comfort, and overall refinement, providing a more inviting and upscale environment for both the driver and passengers.

Infotainment and Connectivity: The Evolution IX's upgraded infotainment system with advanced connectivity options brings the vehicle up to date with modern standards, enhancing convenience and entertainment for all occupants.

Technology Advancements: The Evolution IX's additional tech features make it more competitive in the market, appealing to drivers seeking a more connected and convenient driving experience.

Interior Quality: The Evolution IX's use of higher-quality materials significantly improves the perception of interior quality, elevating the overall ownership experience.
