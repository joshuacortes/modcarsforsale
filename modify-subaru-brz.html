Subaru BRZ

The Toyota 86 and the Subaru BRZ are 2+2 sports cars that Toyota and Subaru collaboratively developed.

The 2+2 fastback coupé is distinguished by its naturally aspirated boxer engine, front-engine, rear-wheel-drive configuration, 53/47 front/rear weight balance, and low center of gravity. It also drew inspiration from Toyota's earlier AE86, a small, light, front-engine/rear-drive Corolla variant that was well-liked for Showroom Stock, Group A, Group N, Rally, Club, and drift racing.

The Boxer engine, rear-wheel drive, and Z, which stands for Zenith, are the three components that give the BRZ its name. The Subaru BRZ's front fascia is different from the design of the 86 since it has a different grille, headlamp assembly, and front fender vent. The Toyota's grill is shaped like a trapezoid, while the BRZ's is hexagonal. The headlight assembly of the BRZ has a wraparound set of LED parking lights, and the bumper has daytime running lights built in. The Subaru's suspension system is different from the Toyota's.

Modified Subaru BRZ

The BRZ's performance, although impressive in stock form, can be further amplified through various upgrades. Many BRZ owners seek to unlock more power from the 2.4-liter flat-four engine. Bolt-on modifications like cold air intakes, performance exhaust systems, and ECU (Engine Control Unit) tuning can yield substantial horsepower gains. For those with a bigger appetite for power, superchargers or turbocharger kits are available to transform the BRZ into a true pocket rocket. These modifications not only increase horsepower but also enhance throttle response and torque, providing a more exhilarating driving experience.

Furthermore, brake and suspension upgrades can greatly improve the BRZ's handling and stopping power. Upgraded sway bars, coilovers, and high-performance brakes help the car grip the road better, ensuring precise cornering and improved braking distances.


Aftermarket Support

One of the BRZ's standout features is its thriving aftermarket support. A multitude of companies produce performance parts, body kits, and accessories tailored to the BRZ. Enthusiasts can personalize their cars with a wide array of choices, from carbon fiber hoods to performance-oriented wheels and tires. The availability of aftermarket support is a testament to the BRZ's popularity in the tuning community, allowing owners to create unique and personalized vehicles.


Design Enhancements

In addition to performance upgrades, the BRZ offers plenty of opportunities for design enhancements. Aerodynamic body kits and spoilers can not only improve the car's appearance but also enhance its stability at higher speeds. Custom paint jobs, vinyl wraps, and interior upgrades can be used to make the car stand out from the crowd. Many BRZ enthusiasts take pride in their ability to create a one-of-a-kind look that matches their personality.


Motorsports Success

The Subaru BRZ has also seen considerable success in motorsports, thanks to its agile and responsive nature. The BRZ has been a favorite in grassroots motorsport events such as autocross and track days. Its low center of gravity, precise steering, and balanced chassis make it a favorite among amateur and professional racers alike. Additionally, the BRZ has competed in various motorsport events, including the Nürburgring 24 Hours and the Super GT series in Japan, showcasing its competitive capabilities.


Driving Experience

What truly sets the BRZ apart is the driving experience it offers. The car's low curb weight and rear-wheel-drive layout result in a balanced and predictable handling experience. The steering is responsive, and the car communicates every nuance of the road surface to the driver. This makes the BRZ a joy to drive on winding roads or racetracks, as it encourages the driver to explore the limits of their skills.


The driver-centric cockpit of the BRZ is designed with the enthusiast in mind. The supportive seats, well-placed controls, and intuitive interface create a connection between the driver and the car that is hard to replicate in other vehicles. The six-speed manual transmission is a favorite among purists, while the automatic transmission option also offers quick shifts for those who prefer convenience.


Additional Notable Topics

Beyond the core aspects of performance, aftermarket support, design, motorsports success, and driving experience, it's worth noting that the Subaru BRZ also comes with modern tech features, including touchscreen infotainment systems, smartphone connectivity, and advanced safety aids. These features make the car more versatile for daily use without compromising its sports car DNA.


Modifying a Subaru BRZ is a rewarding endeavor that allows owners to tap into the car's true potential, transforming it into a high-performance machine that suits their tastes. With a rich ecosystem of aftermarket support, a successful track record in motorsports, and a driving experience that is second to none, the Subaru BRZ has secured its place as a beloved platform for enthusiasts to explore the limits of their passion for automotive performance and style. Whether you're seeking more power, better handling, or a unique look, the BRZ is a canvas waiting for your personal touch.

COMMON SUBARU BRZ MODIFICATIONS:

Exhaust System Upgrades

One of the most popular modifications for the Subaru BRZ is upgrading the exhaust system. A performance exhaust not only enhances the engine's sound, giving it a more aggressive and sporty tone, but it can also provide minor power gains. Many BRZ owners opt for aftermarket cat-back or axle-back exhaust systems, which replace the rear portion of the exhaust, as these modifications are relatively straightforward and offer significant sonic improvements. For those looking for maximum performance, headers and full exhaust systems can provide more substantial power increases.


Cold Air Intakes

Another common modification to enhance a BRZ's performance is installing a cold air intake system. A cold air intake replaces the factory airbox with a more open, free-flowing design, allowing the engine to breathe more easily. This modification can result in a small but noticeable boost in horsepower and improved throttle response. It also adds a unique engine bay aesthetic.


Suspension and Handling Upgrades

The BRZ's renowned handling characteristics can be further improved with suspension upgrades. Many enthusiasts opt for coilover kits, adjustable sway bars, and upgraded bushings to fine-tune the car's balance and responsiveness. Lowering the vehicle's center of gravity with coilovers not only enhances cornering capabilities but also adds to the car's overall aesthetics. Adjustable sway bars provide greater control over body roll, allowing the BRZ to handle tighter turns with ease.


Wheels and Tires

Upgrading to lightweight performance wheels and high-performance tires can significantly impact a BRZ's handling. Wider, grippier tires provide better traction, while lighter wheels reduce unsprung weight, which results in improved responsiveness and better ride quality. These modifications are not just for aesthetics; they also enhance the car's performance and make it more fun to drive.


Engine Tuning and ECU Remapping

To unlock the full potential of the BRZ's 2.4-liter flat-four engine, many enthusiasts opt for engine tuning and ECU remapping. Aftermarket ECU tuning can provide substantial power gains by optimizing air/fuel ratios and ignition timing. It's important to note that these modifications should be performed by experienced professionals to avoid engine damage or drivability issues.


Brake Upgrades

Enhancing the BRZ's braking system is vital, especially when increasing engine power and pushing the car's performance limits. Upgraded brake pads, rotors, and stainless steel brake lines can improve stopping power and reduce brake fade during spirited driving or track sessions.


Aesthetic Modifications

In addition to performance upgrades, many BRZ owners enjoy personalizing their vehicles with aesthetic modifications. These can include body kits, spoilers, vinyl wraps, custom paint jobs, and interior enhancements. Personalization is key, and the possibilities are nearly endless to create a unique and visually appealing BRZ.


Modifying a Subaru BRZ can be a fulfilling endeavor for automotive enthusiasts seeking to enhance their car's performance, handling, and overall appeal. With a wide array of available modifications, BRZ owners can tailor their vehicle to their specific tastes and driving preferences. Whether you aim to extract more power from the engine, fine-tune handling, or simply make the car look more unique, the BRZ's versatility makes it an excellent platform for customization and a fantastic canvas for automotive expression.

The Sprintex Supercharger Kit is another compelling option for BRZ owners seeking a supercharged setup. It's known for its linear power delivery and smooth performance. For those who prefer a traditional turbocharger setup, several companies offer turbo kits designed specifically for the BRZ.
