How to sell a car in California



Selling a car in California involves several steps and legal requirements to ensure a smooth and lawful transaction. To help you navigate the process, we've prepared a simple step-by-step guide that outlines the essential tasks and considerations when selling a car in the state of California.


Step 1: Gather the Necessary Documentation

Before listing your car for sale, gather the following documents:

Vehicle Title: Ensure you have the original title in your name, free of liens or encumbrances.

Smog Certification: If your vehicle is more than four years old, you'll need a valid smog certification to complete the sale.

Vehicle History Report: Obtain a vehicle history report to provide transparency to potential buyers.

Maintenance Records: Compile any maintenance or repair records you have for the vehicle.


Step 2: Set the Asking Price

Research the market value of your car to determine a fair and competitive asking price. Consider factors such as the car's age, mileage, condition, and any additional features or modifications. Online car valuation tools can assist in assessing the approximate value.


Step 3: Advertise the Car

Create an appealing advertisement that highlights the car's key features, condition, and price. Advertise the car through various channels, such as online classified platforms, local newspapers, and community bulletin boards. Include clear photos of the car from different angles to attract potential buyers.


Step 4: Prepare the Car for Sale

Before meeting with potential buyers, thoroughly clean and detail the car, both inside and out. Remove personal belongings, address any mechanical issues, and ensure all maintenance is up to date. A well-presented vehicle will make a positive impression and increase your chances of selling it quickly.


Step 5: Negotiate and Finalize the Sale

When potential buyers express interest, arrange a meeting in a safe and public location. Allow them to inspect the car and take it for a test drive. Be prepared to negotiate the price, considering reasonable offers. Once you agree on a price, proceed with the following steps:

Complete the Bill of Sale: Prepare a bill of sale that includes the vehicle's details, the buyer's and seller's information, the purchase price, and the date of sale. Both parties should sign and retain a copy.

Release of Liability: Fill out the Release of Liability form (Form REG 138) provided by the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV). Submit it online or mail it to the DMV to inform them that you are no longer the vehicle's owner, protecting yourself from any future liabilities.

Transfer the Title: Complete the back of the vehicle's title with the buyer's information, including their name and address. Sign and date the title, and provide it to the buyer.


Step 6: Payment and Financial Transactions

Arrange a secure method of payment with the buyer. Accept payment in the form of cash, cashier's check, or a verified electronic transfer. Ensure the funds clear before transferring ownership of the vehicle.


Step 7: Remove License Plates and Cancel Insurance


Remove the license plates from the sold vehicle as they belong to you, not the car. You can transfer them to another vehicle you own or return them to the DMV. Cancel the car insurance policy or transfer it to your new vehicle, if applicable.


Selling a car in California involves several important steps to protect yourself legally and ensure a successful transaction. By following this step-by-step guide, you'll be well-prepared to navigate the process smoothly, from gathering the necessary documentation to negotiating the sale and transferring ownership. Remember to consult the California DMV website or seek professional advice for any specific questions or concerns related to your unique situation.
