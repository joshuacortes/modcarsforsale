Porsche Taycan Turbo S vs. Porsche 911 Turbo S



Stock Motor

Porsche Taycan Turbo S:

0-60 mph: 2.6 seconds (estimated)

Torque: 774 lb-ft

Horsepower: 750 hp (Overboost: 751 hp)

Top Speed: 161 mph (limited, estimated)

Quarter Mile Time: N/A

Lap Times: N/A

Powertrain Configuration: Dual electric motors (all-wheel drive)

Transmission Type: 2-speed automatic gearbox for the rear motor, single-speed for the front motor

Power-to-Weight Ratio: Approximately 6.8 lb/hp

RPM (Shaft Speed): Up to 16,000 RPM


Porsche 911 Turbo S:

0-60 mph: 2.6 seconds (manufacturer claimed)

Torque: 590 lb-ft

Horsepower: 640 hp

Top Speed: 205 mph (manufacturer claimed)

Quarter Mile Time: Approximately 10.6 seconds

Lap Times: Varies depending on the model and track

Powertrain Configuration: Twin-turbocharged 3.8-liter flat-six engine (rear-engine, all-wheel drive)

Transmission Type: 8-speed PDK dual-clutch automatic

Power-to-Weight Ratio: Approximately 5.9 lb/hp

RPM (Shaft Speed): Up to 7,200 RPM


Comparison and Analysis:

Acceleration and Top Speed:

Both the Porsche Taycan Turbo S and the Porsche 911 Turbo S boast an impressive 0-60 mph time of 2.6 seconds, showcasing their incredible acceleration capabilities. While the 911 Turbo S has a higher top speed at an estimated 205 mph, the Taycan Turbo S is electronically limited to around 161 mph due to its focus on efficient electric performance.


Engine Performance:

The Taycan Turbo S derives its power from dual electric motors, producing an astonishing 750 hp (overboost) and an earth-shattering 774 lb-ft of torque. This makes it one of the most powerful electric vehicles on the market, delivering instant torque for exhilarating acceleration.

In contrast, the Porsche 911 Turbo S features a twin-turbocharged 3.8-liter flat-six engine, generating 640 hp and 590 lb-ft of torque. The 911 Turbo S offers a visceral driving experience with its iconic flat-six engine soundtrack and linear power delivery.


Transmission and Power-to-Weight Ratio:

The Taycan Turbo S's power is transmitted through a 2-speed automatic gearbox for the rear motor and a single-speed transmission for the front motor. The dual electric motor setup provides all-wheel drive capability and exceptional traction.

The 911 Turbo S utilizes an 8-speed PDK dual-clutch automatic transmission, optimizing gear changes for maximum performance and efficiency. The 911 Turbo S has a slightly better power-to-weight ratio, owing to its lighter overall weight compared to the Taycan Turbo S.


RPM and Shaft Speed:

The Taycan Turbo S's electric motors can reach shaft speeds of up to 16,000 RPM, showcasing the impressive capabilities of electric propulsion technology. The instant torque delivery and high RPM provide a unique driving experience.

In contrast, the 911 Turbo S's flat-six engine reaches up to 7,200 RPM, delivering a thrilling soundtrack and a traditional sports car feel. The higher-revving engine complements the 911 Turbo S's performance-oriented driving dynamics.





Tuning Ability

Porsche Taycan Turbo S Tuning Potential:

Upgraded Intake: Although the Taycan Turbo S doesn't have a traditional air intake system, upgrading the cooling system and optimizing airflow can enhance overall performance.

Exhaust Modifications: As an electric vehicle, the Taycan Turbo S doesn't rely on exhaust systems. However, tuning specialists may offer noise-enhancing solutions to replicate the traditional sports car exhaust notes for a more immersive driving experience.

Forced Induction: The Taycan Turbo S already features dual electric motors and doesn't require additional forced induction upgrades. However, fine-tuning the motor control and battery management systems can potentially enhance power output.

Fuel Management: The Taycan Turbo S's all-electric powertrain eliminates the need for traditional fuel management upgrades. However, optimizing battery management and control systems can improve overall performance.

Ignition Options: As an EV, the Taycan Turbo S doesn't utilize conventional ignition systems. Instead, tuning potential lies in optimizing the motor control software to extract more power and improve acceleration.


Porsche 911 Turbo S Tuning Potential:

Upgraded Intake: High-flow air filters and cold air intake systems can optimize air intake efficiency, providing more oxygen to the engine for improved combustion.

Exhaust Upgrades: Performance-oriented exhaust systems with larger-diameter pipes, free-flowing mufflers, and high-quality headers can reduce backpressure and enhance exhaust flow, resulting in increased power output and a more aggressive exhaust note. Brands like Akrapoviƒç and Capristo offer reputable exhaust upgrade options for the 911 Turbo S.

Forced Induction: Turbocharger upgrades, such as larger turbo units or twin-turbo conversions, can significantly increase horsepower and torque. Companies like Alpha Performance and ECUtek offer specialized turbo upgrade kits for the 911 Turbo S.

Fuel Management: Aftermarket ECU tuning and piggyback systems, like those offered by COBB Tuning and APR, can optimize fuel delivery and ignition timing for improved performance.

Ignition Upgrades: High-performance ignition coils and spark plugs can provide a more efficient and consistent spark, leading to better combustion and increased power output. Brands like NGK and MSD offer reliable ignition upgrade options for the 911 Turbo S.


Critique:

Electrifying vs. Traditional Tuning:

The Porsche Taycan Turbo S, being an all-electric vehicle, offers a unique tuning landscape compared to traditional internal combustion engine vehicles like the 911 Turbo S. With no exhaust or turbocharger upgrades needed, tuning the Taycan Turbo S involves optimizing software algorithms for motor control and battery management to enhance power delivery and acceleration.

In contrast, the Porsche 911 Turbo S presents a more conventional tuning process with a range of upgrades available for intake, exhaust, forced induction, fuel management, and ignition systems. These modifications can significantly impact the vehicle's power output and driving dynamics, providing a more traditional tuning experience for automotive enthusiasts.


Versatility vs. Tradition:

The Porsche Taycan Turbo S, as an all-electric supercar, offers unparalleled instant torque and acceleration. While traditional tuning components may not be applicable, optimizing battery management and motor control systems opens up possibilities for achieving faster 0-60 times and improved handling characteristics.

The Porsche 911 Turbo S, with its high-revving twin-turbocharged engine, provides tuners with a vast array of traditional aftermarket options. From boosting horsepower to enhancing exhaust notes, traditional tuning allows enthusiasts to tailor their 911 Turbo S to suit their individual driving preferences.





Suspension

Porsche Taycan Turbo S Suspension:

Suspension Type: Adaptive Air Suspension Plus

Damping Control: Electronically adjustable air dampers

Ride Comfort: Smooth and refined, ideal for daily driving and long distances

Handling Characteristics: Precise and stable, with low center of gravity due to battery placement

Adjustability: Various driving modes (Normal, Sport, Sport Plus) for customizable settings

Ground Clearance: Adjustable for easier navigation on rough roads or steep driveways


Porsche 911 Turbo S Suspension:

Suspension Type: Porsche Active Suspension Management (PASM)

Damping Control: Electronically adjustable dampers

Ride Comfort: Firm and sporty, optimized for spirited driving and performance

Handling Characteristics: Agile and responsive, ideal for cornering and high-speed driving

Adjustability: Multiple driving modes (Normal, Sport, Sport Plus, Individual) for customizable settings

Ground Clearance: Fixed, tuned for dynamic road-holding and aerodynamics


Critique:


Suspension Type and Damping Control:

The Porsche Taycan Turbo S features an Adaptive Air Suspension Plus, utilizing electronically adjustable air dampers to optimize ride comfort and handling. The adaptive air suspension system allows the Taycan Turbo S to adapt to various road conditions, ensuring a smooth and refined driving experience.

In contrast, the Porsche 911 Turbo S comes equipped with Porsche Active Suspension Management (PASM), offering electronically adjustable dampers to optimize handling and road-holding performance. The PASM system delivers a firmer ride, ideal for spirited driving and improved road feedback.


Ride Comfort vs. Sporty Handling:

The Taycan Turbo S's adaptive air suspension prioritizes ride comfort, providing a smooth and plush ride even on bumpy roads. The air suspension's ability to adjust ride height and dampening ensures a comfortable driving experience for daily commutes and long-distance journeys.

The 911 Turbo S, with its Porsche Active Suspension Management, focuses on sporty handling and responsive road manners. The suspension's sport-tuned settings deliver precise handling and optimal feedback, making the 911 Turbo S an engaging and exhilarating sports car.


Adjustability and Driving Modes:

Both models offer customizable suspension settings through various driving modes. The Taycan Turbo S offers different driving modes, such as Normal, Sport, and Sport Plus, allowing drivers to fine-tune the suspension to their preferences and driving conditions.

Similarly, the Porsche 911 Turbo S provides multiple driving modes, including Normal, Sport, Sport Plus, and Individual, giving drivers the flexibility to adjust the suspension, engine, and other vehicle characteristics to their liking.


Ground Clearance:

The Taycan Turbo S's adaptive air suspension allows drivers to adjust the ground clearance, making it more versatile for navigating rough roads or steep driveways. The ability to raise the suspension can also enhance the Taycan's capability in inclement weather or challenging road conditions.

In contrast, the 911 Turbo S has a fixed ground clearance, designed to optimize aerodynamics and road-holding performance. The lower center of gravity contributes to the 911's agility and stability during high-speed cornering.

Critique:


Design Philosophy:

The Porsche Taycan Turbo S's interior embodies a futuristic and sustainable design philosophy. With an emphasis on eco-friendly materials, the Taycan's interior sets a new standard for luxury in electric vehicles. The spacious and modern cabin design aims to create an inviting and technologically advanced atmosphere.

In contrast, the Porsche 911 Turbo S embraces a classic and sporty design theme, staying true to its heritage as a high-performance sports car. The driver-centric cockpit layout, carbon fiber accents, and sport seats emphasize the 911's sporty heritage while providing a sense of occasion for drivers.


Materials and Luxury:

The Taycan Turbo S elevates luxury with its combination of high-quality leather, Alcantara, and sustainable materials. Porsche's commitment to sustainability is evident in the use of recycled materials and eco-friendly trim elements, providing a unique and environmentally-conscious luxury experience.

The 911 Turbo S boasts premium leather upholstery, carbon fiber accents, and aluminum elements, creating a sophisticated and sporty interior. While not as environmentally focused as the Taycan, the 911 Turbo S delivers a classic Porsche luxury feel tailored to driving enthusiasts.


Infotainment and Digital Displays:

Both models feature the Porsche Communication Management (PCM) system, providing a user-friendly infotainment experience. However, the Taycan Turbo S boasts a larger 16.8-inch curved digital instrument cluster and a 10.9-inch infotainment touchscreen, offering more screen real estate and a futuristic appearance.

The 911 Turbo S retains its classic analog gauges, complemented by a centrally mounted digital tachometer. While offering essential information, the 911's interior maintains a timeless and driver-focused design.
